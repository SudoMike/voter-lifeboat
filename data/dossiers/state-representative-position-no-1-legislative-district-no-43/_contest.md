## What this office does

One of two House seats for LD 43 (central Seattle: Capitol Hill, University
District, Wallingford, Fremont, downtown areas); votes on state law,
budget, and taxes.

## Race dynamics

Two candidates, both preferring the Democratic Party; both advance to the
general regardless of primary result, so research depth is light.

- **Nicole Macri** — incumbent since 2017; DESC deputy director; a lead
  House voice on housing and homelessness, including 2025 rent
  stabilization legislation.
- **Alby Clendennin** — first-time candidate; UW resident director;
  self-described "pragmatic progressive Democrat."

## Differentiating axes

- Deep incumbent record (rent stabilization/HB 1217, tax fairness,
  behavioral health) vs. a young challenger without a record.
- Both progressive Democrats; Clendennin's distinct emphases are corporate
  influence in politics and AI regulation, while Macri's are renter
  protections, healthcare affordability, and climate-resilient
  infrastructure.
- Macri has drawn some criticism from the left for incremental rather than
  maximal housing interventions (CHS coverage) — the main substantive
  wedge in an otherwise low-contrast race.
