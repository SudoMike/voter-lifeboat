## Background

First-time candidate; Resident Director at the University of Washington,
overseeing administrative operations, student leader teams, and safety for
nearly 10,000 residents, and serving as a hearing officer for student
conduct cases [S1]. MA in Student Affairs Administration, Appalachian State
University; BA in International Studies, University of West Florida [S1].
Grew up in northwest Florida; says the 2018 Marjory Stoneman Douglas school
shooting spurred his activism; moved to Seattle after an internship at
Seattle University and took the UW role in 2024 [S2]. No campaign website
was listed in the pamphlet or found.

## Positions

- **Political reform:** Reducing corporate influence in politics is a
  stated top priority [S1].
- **Technology:** Regulating artificial intelligence to prevent unfair
  pricing [S1].
- **Public services:** Strengthening public services; great schools,
  walkable neighborhoods, affordable homes [S1].
- **Housing/transportation/higher ed:** Housing affordability, transit
  progress, and protecting higher-education and research funding [S2].
- Self-describes as a "pragmatic progressive Democrat" [S1].
