## Background
Janice Zahn is the incumbent State Representative for Legislative District 41, Position 1. The King County Council appointed her to the seat on a unanimous vote on January 21, 2025, to succeed Tana Senn, who left to lead the state Department of Children, Youth and Families [S4]. She won the November 2025 special election to retain the seat, defeating John Whitney [S5]. She served on the Bellevue City Council from 2017 to 2025 and chaired Bellevue's Transportation Commission before that [S1][S4]. Professionally she is a civil engineer who has worked at the Port of Seattle since 2000, serving as Chief Engineer, and previously as a structural engineer at Berger/ABAM [S1][S4]. A first-generation immigrant who arrived in the U.S. at age ten, she holds B.S. and M.S. degrees in civil engineering and an Executive MPA from the University of Washington [S1][S2]. Her community service includes the Bellevue Schools Foundation, The Sophia Way, Nourishing Networks, and the National League of Cities board [S1].

## Positions
- **Affordability:** focused on "improving affordability and delivering investments in housing, education, childcare, and transportation" [S1].
- **Transportation / infrastructure:** emphasizes her engineering and transportation background as the basis for infrastructure investment priorities [S1][S2].
- **Budget:** says she aims to address the state's budget challenges "responsibly" while protecting essential services [S1][S2].
- **Education:** priorities include education funding, classroom materials, and special education resources [S2].
- **Environment / healthcare:** lists environmental protection and healthcare access among her priorities [S2].

## Record
- Committees: Local Government (Vice-Chair), Housing, Finance, and Transportation [S1].
- Her campaign reports that in her first term (2025–26) she passed multiple bills signed into law, including HB 1640 (standardizing accountability/disciplinary procedures for out-of-state healthcare providers), HB 1899 (aligning homelessness point-in-time data collection with federal standards), HB 1967 and HB 2420 (streamlining public-works contracting and expanding small-works rosters), transportation procurement and safety measures (HB 1970, HB 1980), and HB 2228 (allowing scissor-stair configurations to add housing flexibility in the building code) [S2][S3].
- Bellevue City Councilmember, 2017–2025 [S1][S4].
- Campaign finance: her 2026 candidate committee reported roughly $29,500 in contributions across 71 reported contribution records in the PDC contributions dataset as of 2026-07-16 [S6].
