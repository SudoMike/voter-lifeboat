## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected per legislative district for a two-year term. Representatives write and vote on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets, controlling K-12 funding, state taxes, housing and land-use policy, healthcare programs, transportation investment, and public-safety statutes. This is the August 4, 2026 top-two primary; the top two vote-getters advance to the November 3 general election regardless of party.

## The district and this race
LD 41 is an Eastside district covering all of Mercer Island and Newcastle plus portions of Bellevue, Renton, Issaquah, and Sammamish [S2]. Three candidates filed for Position 1 [S2]:

- **Janice Zahn (D, incumbent, Bellevue)** — civil engineer and Port of Seattle Chief Engineer; Bellevue councilmember 2017–2025; appointed to the seat in January 2025 to succeed Tana Senn and retained it in a November 2025 special election; carries a first-term bill record and a broad institutional endorsement list [S1][S3].
- **Elle Nguyen (R, Mercer Island)** — small business owner and law-school graduate; prior runs for school board (2021) and LD 41 Position 2 (2022); platform of fiscal restraint, public safety, parental rights, and government transparency [S1][S2].
- **Alex Tsimerman (Standup-America Party, Bellevue)** — perennial candidate whose pamphlet statement contains slogans and no policy content; multiple PDC enforcement cases for unfiled disclosure reports [S1][S2].

The dynamic is a recently appointed-then-elected Democratic incumbent with a large resource and endorsement advantage (about $29.5k raised versus no reported fundraising by either challenger as of mid-July 2026 [S4]), a Republican challenger offering the sole policy alternative, and a perennial candidate. With three candidates, the primary eliminates one; the practical question is which challenger joins Zahn on the November ballot.

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Experience and record** — a decade of council plus legislative service and passed bills (Zahn) vs. community advocacy without elected office (Nguyen) vs. no submitted qualifications (Tsimerman) [S1].
2. **Budget and taxes** — Zahn frames the state budget as needing responsible management while protecting investments; Nguyen centers stopping wasteful spending and blocking tax increases [S1].
3. **Government's role in affordability** — public investment in housing, childcare, education, and transportation (Zahn) vs. lowering cost of living through spending restraint (Nguyen) [S1].
4. **Social-policy orientation** — Nguyen's "traditional values" and parental-rights emphasis vs. Zahn's equity- and access-oriented framing; this is the clearest values contrast [S1].
5. **Basic candidacy seriousness** — one candidate has a record of campaign-finance non-compliance and no platform, which is itself a differentiator in this field [S1].
