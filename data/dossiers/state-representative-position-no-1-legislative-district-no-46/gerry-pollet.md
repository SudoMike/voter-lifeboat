## Background

Gerry Pollet is the incumbent State Representative for the 46th Legislative District, Position 1; he has represented the district since 2011 [S7] (his pamphlet statement describes him as "Your State Representative since 2012") [S1]. He is a seven-term incumbent who has not faced a serious challenger since 2012 [S6]. He serves on the faculty of the UW School of Public Health and is a public-interest attorney; he is Director of Heart of America Northwest, the citizens' group leading efforts on the environmental cleanup of the Hanford nuclear site, and supervises a hands-on Tribal Environmental law student program [S1]. He holds a law degree from the UW School of Law [S1]. He serves on the Washington Coalition for Open Government board and has been honored by the League of Women Voters as a "Sunshine in Government" honoree [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes:** Says he championed the "millionaires" and capital gains taxes so the wealthiest pay more to fund schools, healthcare, and childcare [S1]; advocates progressive tax reform, including a millionaires' tax to fund education and reduce reliance on regressive sales taxes, plus accountability for corporate tax preferences [S4]. Campaign says he wants to cut the sales tax [S1].
- **Housing:** Supports affordable housing development, removing barriers to density, requiring affordable units in new developments, and transit-oriented development near transit investments [S2].
- **Education:** Describes himself as the legislature's leading champion for education funding, especially special education; says he led efforts to remove caps on special-education funding and co-developed the Washington College Grant (tuition-free community and technical college) [S2]. Says he wants to make college free [S1].
- **Open government:** Long-identified with legislative transparency, public-records access, and fighting secret legislator communications with lobbyists [S2][S7]; 2026-session priorities included conflict-of-interest standards for legislators and public-records legislation [S4].
- **Civil rights / federal pushback:** Cites passing "ICE mask bans," funding abortion access, and protecting immigrants [S1]; emphasizes reproductive freedom, LGBTQ+ rights, and hate-crime laws [S2].
- **Other 2026 priorities:** grocery-store closure prevention, reading-instruction modernization, and microplastic pollution reduction via washing-machine filter requirements [S4].

## Record

- Chairs the Joint Legislative Audit & Review Committee (JLARC); member of the House Appropriations, Postsecondary Education & Workforce, and Education committees [S3].
- Campaign materials credit him with helping lift the special-education funding cap (2025), passing transit-oriented development legislation (2025), co-developing the Washington College Grant, and strengthening hate-crime laws [S2].
- Was a co-sponsor of HB 1559 (2023), the student basic needs act (prime-sponsored by Rep. Entenman) [see leg.wa.gov bill summary cited in the LD 47 dossier].
- Holds monthly "Traveling Town Halls" for constituents in the interim and weekly sessions during the legislative session [S4].
- Campaign finance: about $129,598 in reported contributions across 456 donations for the 2026 cycle as of mid-July 2026 — the most individual donations in the race but the lowest dollar total of the three candidates [S5]. His donor base includes tribes and labor organizations such as SEIU and the Washington Federation of State Employees [S7].

## Endorsements

- Pamphlet statement lists: Planned Parenthood; Washington Conservation Action; nurses; teachers; Labor Council; Alliance for Gun Responsibility; Congresswoman Pramila Jayapal; "more" [S1].
- Post Alley reports establishment support including Gov. Bob Ferguson, Rep. Pramila Jayapal, Seattle-area legislators, and labor [S6].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race with a legislative record (15 years), committee chairmanship (JLARC), and seniority on budget-writing Appropriations [S3][S6].
- Signature identity is open government/transparency plus education (especially special-education) funding [S2][S4][S7].
- Backed by the Democratic establishment and labor, but is being outraised by both challengers this cycle [S5][S6][S7].
- Least emphasis among the three on AI regulation, the issue that has become the race's novel flashpoint [S6].
