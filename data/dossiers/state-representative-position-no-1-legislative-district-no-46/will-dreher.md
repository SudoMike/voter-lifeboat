## Background

Will Dreher is a first-time candidate [S1]. He is a former Assistant U.S. Attorney (federal prosecutor) who prosecuted January 6th rioters (about 25 defendants per his campaign), hate and violent crimes, and civil-rights violations by officers [S1][S2]. He clerked for U.S. Supreme Court Justice Elena Kagan [S1]. He was previously a 4th-grade public school teacher and a researcher at union-affiliated political organizations, and is now an attorney at a Seattle firm suing oil, tobacco, tech, and AI companies on behalf of consumers and communities [S1][S2][S4]. He holds a BSFS from Georgetown University and a JD from Harvard Law School [S1]. Community involvement includes the Federal Bar Association's Youth Law Day and Criminal Law Committee, St. Bridget Catholic Church, and coaching girls' softball [S1].

## Positions

- **AI and tech regulation (signature issue):** The only candidate promising to "aggressively regulate tech" to address AI disinformation, AI/automation job loss, and data-center growth [S1]; frames AI as a threat to the environment (data centers), employment, and human dignity, and wants social-media safeguards for kids [S2][S4].
- **Education:** As a former teacher, proposes fully funding schools and improving school performance [S1].
- **Housing:** Cut red tape and fund affordable housing, childcare, and transit [S1].
- **Taxes:** Fix Washington's regressive tax structure; hold government accountable for tax dollars [S1].
- **Public safety:** Implement evidence-based public safety measures; hold corporations and criminals accountable [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

- The Stranger endorsed Dreher in the 2026 primary over Davis and Pollet [S4 reports the endorsement contest; S6]. The endorsement drew backlash among some progressive activists over a letter of support Dreher wrote for Justice Brett Kavanaugh; The Stranger defended it on grounds that clerks customarily write such letters for the justices they clerked for [S6].
- Seattle City Attorney Erika Evans and Seattle City Councilmember Dionne Foster [S1][S2].
- Natalie Walton-Anderson, former chief public safety officer under Mayor Harrell [S2].

## Scoring notes

- Race's top fundraiser by a wide margin: about $262,704 across 347 contributions as of mid-July 2026 [S3]; Post Alley reported roughly $100K of his funding came from AI-industry donors, including a cascade of contributions from Anthropic and OpenAI employees after an AI-researcher endorsement — which opponents cite to question his independence as an AI regulator [S4].
- The only candidate making AI regulation the centerpiece of his platform; he entered in April 2026 and reshaped the race around the issue [S1][S4].
- Prosecutorial and appellate-law background is unique in the field (Jan. 6 prosecutions, Kagan clerkship); no legislative or elected experience [S1][S4].
- Media endorsement (The Stranger) offsets the incumbent's establishment support, but Davis has criticized his out-of-state funding and short local political history [S4][S6].
