## Background

Ron Davis is a startup founder, executive, and enterprise sales consultant who describes himself as a housing policy expert [S1]. He holds a BA from George Fox University, an MS in education from the University of Oregon, and a JD from Harvard Law School [S1]. He is an urbanist activist who narrowly lost a 2023 Seattle City Council race (District 4) [S4]. He serves on the board of Futurewise, working on housing and environmental justice, has organized grassroots campaigns around affordable transit and corporate taxation, and is active in his neighborhood association, YMCA, and as a soccer coach [S1]. He has never held elected office [S1]. His personal narrative centers on being the child of a teen pregnancy whose parents' affordable neighborhood enabled a middle-class life — a path he argues is now gone [S1][S2].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Build enough homes to reduce costs; freeze rent in publicly owned housing [S2]. Says he built coalitions that helped pass major state housing bills and defended the "millionaire tax" that funds Seattle Social Housing when Amazon sought to overturn it [S1].
- **Childcare:** Make childcare affordable/free for all families [S1][S2].
- **Transit:** Invest in reliable, fast public transit [S1][S2].
- **Taxes:** Tax wealthy individuals and corporations to fund schools, universal healthcare, and other public goods; make the tax code fairer [S1][S2].
- **AI/tech:** Added AI regulation to his platform after Dreher entered the race; prioritizes protecting consumers and workers from AI-related harms; questions Dreher's credibility as an AI regulator given AI-industry funding of Dreher's campaign [S4][S5].
- **Civil rights:** Defend civil rights and democratic freedoms [S2].

## Endorsements

- Elected officials: Seattle Mayor Katie Wilson; Port of Seattle Commission President Ryan Calkins; former Seattle City Councilmember Mike O'Brien; Bellingham Mayor Kim Lund [S2].
- Community leaders: John Binda (NAACP WA political action committee chair); Patience Malaba (Housing Development Consortium executive director); Tiffani McCoy (Seattle Social Housing Developer executive director) [S2].
- Organizations: IBEW Local 48, Progressive Victory, and housing/urbanist advocacy groups; pamphlet also cites unions, College Democrats, Tech4Taxes, and the Transit Riders Union [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- The race's urbanist/housing-abundance candidate: deepest identification with density, transit, and social-housing advocacy, backed by housing-movement figures and the new Seattle mayor [S2][S4].
- No prior elected office; ran citywide-adjacent before (2023 council race, narrow loss), so has campaign experience but no legislative record [S4].
- Middle of the field in fundraising: about $154,724 across 290 contributions as of mid-July 2026 [S3]; has criticized Dreher's out-of-state and AI-industry funding [S4].
- Overlaps heavily with both opponents on progressive taxation; differentiates on urbanism intensity and movement endorsements rather than ideology [S1][S2][S4].
