## Background

Executive Director of the Lavender Rights Project, a nonprofit focused on
social safety nets and justice for Black gender-diverse communities in
Washington [S1][S2]. Community organizer in housing, criminal, racial, and
gender justice movements; former union member and ordained family minister;
holds a Master of Divinity [S1]. No prior elected experience [S1]. If
elected, she would be the first openly transgender person to serve in the
Washington Legislature [S3].

## Positions

- **Public safety:** "Compassionate public safety" — behavioral health
  professionals rather than police for mental health crises, funding for
  community-based violence interruption, police accountability, and limits
  on surveillance technology and data sharing with federal agencies
  [S1][S2].
- **Housing:** Expand affordable and permanent supportive housing, using
  the Chief Seattle Club model as a blueprint [S1][S2].
- **Taxes:** Fairer tax code; progressive taxation on wealthy residents and
  corporations; expand cash assistance such as the Working Families Tax
  Credit [S1][S2].
- **Healthcare:** Universal healthcare including vision, dental, and mental
  health; protect Medicaid, reproductive, and gender-affirming care
  [S1][S2].
- **Childcare:** Universal childcare and publicly funded preschool [S2].

## Record

- Says she built a coalition that redirected $16 million from jail
  expansion into supportive housing and alternative public-safety
  approaches, in partnership with Chief Seattle Club [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

Pamphlet cites Rep. Pramila Jayapal, Sen. Bernie Sanders, labor, and
elected officials [S1]. News coverage lists King County Executive Girmay
Zahilay, State Sen. Rebecca Saldaña, Seattle City Council President Joy
Hollingsworth, City Attorney Erika Evans, Councilmembers Alexis Mercedes
Rinck and Dionne Foster, Port Commissioner Toshiko Hasegawa, and school
board figures [S4].

## Scoring notes

- The candidate in this race with executive/organizational leadership
  experience and a concrete claimed policy outcome ($16M jail-to-housing
  redirection) [S1][S2].
- Extensive endorsement slate spanning national progressives and local
  Democratic officials, unusual for an open-seat first-time candidate
  [S1][S4].
- Platform is explicitly progressive/Democratic-socialist-aligned;
  differentiates from her Independent opponent [S2][S3].
