## What this office does

A state representative is one of two House members for the district in the Washington State Legislature, serving a two-year term. Representatives write and vote on state laws and the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets, covering issues such as K-12 education funding, housing, taxes, public safety, health care, and transportation. The 45th District covers Eastside communities including Kirkland and Woodinville.

## Race dynamics

Only two candidates filed, so both advance from the August primary to the November general election regardless of the primary result. The race pits Roger Goodman (Prefers Democratic Party), the incumbent since 2007 and chair of the House Community Safety Committee, against JoAnn Tolentino (Prefers Republican Party), a retired music teacher and compliance professional making her first run for the Legislature after a 2025 Northshore School Board candidacy.

## Differentiating issue axes

- **Incumbency and experience:** a nearly 20-year legislator with committee chairmanships and a legal/criminal-justice background versus a first-time candidate running explicitly as "not a politician."
- **Public safety approach:** Goodman emphasizes police accountability reform, gun-violence reduction, and crisis response; Tolentino emphasizes "full support for law enforcement."
- **Education:** continued K-12 funding and school meals (Goodman) versus parental rights, academic excellence, and opposition to critical race theory and gender ideology in curricula (Tolentino).
- **Climate and energy:** climate action and carbon-cap legislation (Goodman) versus affordable energy including fossil fuels and nuclear (Tolentino).
- **Elections policy:** Tolentino makes voter ID and paper ballots a core plank; Goodman does not raise elections administration.
- **Housing:** both cite affordability, but Goodman stresses public investment in affordable housing while Tolentino stresses deregulation and zoning reform.
