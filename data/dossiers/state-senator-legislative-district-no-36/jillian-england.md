## Background
Jillian England (Prefers Republican Party) is challenging incumbent Sen. Noel Frame in the 36th Legislative District. She has been an elected Precinct Committee Officer since 2007 and is Vice Chair of the 36th Legislative District Republican Party [S1]. Her professional career is in computer science and health care business operations, which she describes as focused on data-driven problem-solving and cost-efficiency [S1]. Education: BS in Computer Science and Business, University of Puget Sound [S1]. She is an active member of a Missouri Synod Lutheran church [S1].

## Positions
- Taxes: top priority is repealing the 9.9% "Millionaires Tax," which she characterizes as a door to broader income taxation; also targets the state's high sales tax and the cascading B&O tax [S1][S2].
- Climate/energy: would repeal the Climate Commitment Act, which she says adds 50–65 cents per gallon to gas prices; supports an "all-of-the-above" energy strategy — fast-tracking nuclear power, expanding natural gas, and protecting hydroelectric/baseload sources [S1][S2].
- Homelessness/public safety: calls for accountability, clearing encampments, and "breaking feedback loops" between addiction, migration, and policy tolerance; her site features an overdose-trend model built on state data (2018–2024) [S1][S2].
- Education: says schools should "educate students rather than indoctrinate them" and demands accountability [S1].
- Governing philosophy: "Government force cannot create prosperity, safety, or abundance"; emphasizes individual agency and deregulation [S2].

## Endorsements
None listed on the pamphlet statement or campaign site homepage [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- The only Republican-preference candidate in these six races; offers a direct ideological contrast to the incumbent on taxes, climate policy, and homelessness [S1].
- No prior elected office beyond party precinct officer; runs on private-sector cost-efficiency experience [S1].
- Pledges to repeal the specific taxes (Millionaires Tax) and climate law (CCA) that her opponent and the district's House members helped pass [S1][S2].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
