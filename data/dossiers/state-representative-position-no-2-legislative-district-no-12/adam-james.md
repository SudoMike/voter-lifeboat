## Background

- Pastor and co-founder/founding member of Grace City Church in Wenatchee; roughly 28 years in ministry and community work; lifelong North Central Washington resident, age 48 [S1][S3][S4][S5].
- Founding board member of Garden City Academy, a K-12 private Christian school [S1].
- No prior elected experience [S1]. Announced his campaign March 3, 2026, challenging incumbent Republican Mike Steele for Position 2 [S5].
- Pamphlet states that during COVID he "stood alongside 40 Washingtonians in a lawsuit defending constitutional freedom and local governance" [S1].
- Education: AAS, Wenatchee Valley College; degree in Biblical Studies and Church Ministries, Northwest University (magna cum laude) [S1].

## Positions

Campaign organized around "5 E's" [S2][S5]:
- **Elections:** voter ID requirements, proof of citizenship, and reform of the mail-in ballot system [S2][S3].
- **Economy:** tax cuts, deregulation, easing business startup requirements; reduce state spending via government-efficiency measures [S2][S3]. Housing: permitting reform to lower home prices; incentivize homes at $200,000 or less [S3].
- **Education:** expand school choice, "parental empowerment," and opposition to what he calls "ideological indoctrination" in classrooms [S2][S3].
- **Enforcement:** opposes sanctuary-state policies; backs law enforcement; "tough on crime," opposing catch-and-release approaches; wants a different approach to homelessness and addiction [S2][S3].
- **Ethics:** protection of children and defense of traditional family structures; frames his candidacy as "bold, Christian, and conservative leadership" [S2].
- Also cites road maintenance, expanded airport/travel options, fire mitigation, and environmental stewardship [S3].

## Endorsements

- Republican Party organizations of King County, Snohomish County, and Douglas County (per pamphlet, and confirmed for Douglas and King counties in news coverage) [S1][S3].
- Former state House Speaker Clyde Ballard and former Bellevue City Councilmember Kevin Wallace [S3].

## Scoring notes

- A challenge to the incumbent from the right: same party as Steele but running on election-law changes, school choice, and explicitly Christian-conservative social themes that the incumbent does not campaign on [S2][S3].
- Has the official backing of three county Republican Party organizations against a sitting Republican, while the incumbent holds the House Republican caucus committee's support — a visible intra-party split [S1][S3][S4].
- Dominant fundraiser in the race: $343,869 across 595 itemized contributions as of 2026-07-16 (HeraldNet reported $336,133 as of June 30), roughly four times the incumbent's total; top donors are individuals at the $2,400 maximum [S3][S6].
- No governmental or elected record; his experience base is ministry, private-school governance, and community organizations [S1].
