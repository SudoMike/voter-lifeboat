## What this office does

A Washington State Representative is one of two House members (Position 1 and Position 2) elected from each legislative district to two-year terms in the state House of Representatives. Representatives write and vote on state law, adopt the operating, capital, and transportation budgets, and set statewide policy on housing, education funding, healthcare, taxes, public safety, and transportation. This is a partisan office decided by a top-two primary (August 4, 2026): the two highest vote-getters advance to November regardless of party.

## Race dynamics

- District 12 is a cross-Cascades district redrawn in 2024: it covers all or most of Chelan County plus eastern King County (North Bend, Snoqualmie Ridge, Carnation, and surrounding Snoqualmie Valley areas) and southeast Snohomish County (Monroe, Sultan, Gold Bar, Index) [S1][S2]. King County voters here are a minority of a historically Republican, North Central Washington-anchored district.
- Three candidates: five-term Republican incumbent Mike Steele (Chelan; chamber-of-commerce director and Capital Budget Committee ranking member), Republican challenger Adam James (Wenatchee pastor, no prior office), and Democrat Maggie Adams (Snoqualmie; IATSE union business representative) [S1][S4].
- The headline dynamic is the intra-Republican fight: James has out-raised the incumbent roughly four-to-one ($343,869 vs. $84,999 on PDC records as of 2026-07-16; Adams: $4,158) [S1][S3], and holds the King, Snohomish, and Douglas county Republican Party endorsements, while Steele retains the House Republican caucus committee and an unusual cross-spectrum set of backers including the Washington State Labor Council and the state medical association [S1][S4].
- With a top-two primary, a Republican-vs-Republican general election is possible; Adams is the only Democrat and the only candidate from the district's Puget Sound side [S1].

## Differentiating issue axes

1. **Style of Republicanism (the central axis):** Steele runs as a bipartisan, capital-budget-focused institutionalist; James runs as a movement conservative on election-law changes (voter ID, mail-ballot reform), school choice, and explicitly Christian social values. Voters choosing between the two Republicans are choosing governing approach more than party [S1][S4].
2. **Left-right contrast:** Adams provides the only progressive platform — healthcare as a right, millionaire's tax, union-negotiated worker protections, community policing — directly opposed to both Republicans' tax-cutting and tough-on-crime stances [S1][S4].
3. **Geography:** Chelan County incumbency and agricultural roots (Steele, James) vs. King County/Snoqualmie Valley residence (Adams); how candidates would represent the district's newer westside communities is a live question [S1][S2].
4. **Experience:** ten years of legislative record and budget seniority (Steele) vs. ministry/community leadership (James) vs. union bargaining leadership (Adams) [S1][S4].
5. **Water and growth:** Adams uniquely emphasizes water conservation against data-center and energy demands; Steele and James emphasize fire mitigation and stewardship framing [S1].
