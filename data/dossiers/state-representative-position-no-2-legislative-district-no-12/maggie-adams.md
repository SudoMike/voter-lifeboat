## Background

- Snoqualmie resident (the King County portion of the redrawn district), age 47; professional actor, costume designer, wardrobe technician, and stagehand [S1][S3].
- Elected union leader: Business Representative and Job Steward for IATSE Local 887 (theatrical stage employees) and delegate to the Martin Luther King Jr. County Labor Council; member of Actor's Equity Association; negotiates and oversees collective bargaining agreements [S1][S2].
- Previous work: Executive Business Administrator at Microsoft and Executive Assistant at Amazon [S1].
- Education: Coastline Community College [S1]. Volunteers with Habitat for Humanity, King County Parks, Seattle Public Schools, and the Washington State Thespian Society, among others [S1].
- No prior elected government office [S1].

## Positions

- **Healthcare:** views accessible, comprehensive healthcare as a right; wants to expand medical access across all regions of the state, saying access is "often determined by their location on the map" [S2][S3].
- **Workers/economy:** living wages, worker protections, job-safety standards, tax fairness, and inflation relief [S1]; supports a "millionaire's tax" and higher taxes on high-grossing businesses [S3].
- **Education:** fully fund schools; raise teacher wages and improve working conditions; support access to higher education regardless of economic status [S2][S3].
- **Environment/water:** emphasizes water conservation and resource protection amid drought; concerned that data centers' and energy companies' water demands could crowd out community and agricultural needs; opposes natural gas mining/fracking threats to water [S2][S3]; wants to protect parks, natural resources, and agricultural lands [S1].
- **Housing:** regulate vacation rentals; mandate affordable units in new construction [S3].
- **Public safety:** emphasizes community policing and de-escalation [S3].
- **Democracy reform:** supports constitutional term limits for elected offices and the federal judiciary, and lowering Washington's voting age to 16 for state and local elections [S2].

## Endorsements

None listed on her campaign website or found in news coverage [S2][S3].

## Scoring notes

- The only Democrat in this race; offers the sole labor-left platform (union leadership, millionaire's tax, healthcare as a right) against two Republicans [S1][S2][S3].
- The only candidate from the district's King County side (Snoqualmie); both Republicans are from Chelan County [S3].
- Distinctive structural-reform proposals not raised by opponents: term limits and voting at 16 for state/local elections [S2].
- Minimal campaign resources: $4,158 across 22 itemized PDC contributions as of 2026-07-16 (HeraldNet reported $2,345 as of June 30), and no endorsements found [S3][S4].
