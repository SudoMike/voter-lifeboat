## Background

Suzan DelBene, 64, of Medina, has been the U.S. Representative for Washington's 1st Congressional District (parts of King and Snohomish counties) since 2012 [S1][S6]. She serves on the House Ways and Means Committee [S1]. She chairs the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee (DCCC), the House Democrats' campaign arm, and was retained in that role for a second straight cycle (2026) in December 2024; she is a past chair of the New Democrat Coalition [S7]. Before Congress she spent about 20 years in business, including as a Microsoft executive and at local tech startups, and served as Director of Washington's Department of Revenue [S1]. She holds a B.A. in biology from Reed College and an M.B.A. from the University of Washington [S1].

## Positions

- **Cost of living / taxes:** Says she is focused on lowering the cost of living, reducing taxes for working families, and ensuring "the wealthy pay their fair share"; opposes the 2025 Republican tax-and-spending law's cuts to Medicaid and food assistance [S1]. Campaign site lists "Economic Security and Rising Costs for Families" as a top issue [S2].
- **Child Tax Credit:** Says she has led efforts to permanently expand the Child Tax Credit with monthly payments to families [S1].
- **Housing:** Says she has introduced bipartisan legislation to spur creation of two million affordable homes; campaign lists housing and homelessness as a priority [S1][S2].
- **Health care:** Wants to lower health care premiums and roll back Medicaid cuts [S1]; health care is a listed campaign issue [S2].
- **Trade/tariffs:** Opposes the Trump administration's tariffs and says she is fighting to end them [S1]; per the Herald, she emphasizes rolling back Republican tariffs [S6].
- **Immigration:** Supports immigration reform and says ICE agents must be "reined in"; the Herald reports she pairs reform with ICE accountability [S1][S6].
- **Climate/energy:** Campaign lists energy, environment, and climate change as an issue [S2]; supports renewable energy investment including a regional hydrogen hub [S6].
- **Social Security/Medicare:** Says she is committed to strengthening both programs [S1][S2].
- **Reproductive rights:** Supports reproductive rights and opposes a nationwide abortion ban [S1][S2].
- **Other listed campaign issues:** public safety and police accountability, infrastructure, education, digital privacy, and "holding the administration accountable" [S2].

## Record

- Congress.gov lists 153 bills and resolutions she has sponsored since taking office [S4].
- **Enacted:** Her Bulk Infant Formula to Retail Shelves Act (H.R. 8982, 117th Congress) became Public Law 117-192 during the 2022 formula shortage [S4].
- **119th Congress (2025–26) sponsored bills include:** Prevent Tariff Abuse Act (H.R. 407, limiting use of emergency powers for tariffs); Apprenticeship Opportunity Act (H.R. 1859); National Landslide Preparedness Act Reauthorization (H.R. 2250, reported out of committee); Smart Cities and Communities Act (H.R. 4649); Clean Competition Act (H.R. 6787, a carbon-based trade measure); Chronic Care Management Improvement Act (H.R. 8261); Email Privacy Act (H.R. 9016); Manufacturing Jobs for Veterans Act (H.R. 9121); Kidney Disease Education Access Expansion Act (H.R. 9509) [S4].
- **Recurring legislative themes** across her tenure include digital privacy (Email Privacy Act, repeatedly reintroduced), health care and Medicare chronic-care/prior-authorization reform (Improving Seniors' Timely Access to Care Act), portable benefits for gig workers, and technology policy (IoT, immersive technology) [S4].
- **Campaign finance (2026 cycle, through 2026-03-31):** receipts about $2.74 million; disbursements about $2.43 million; cash on hand about $1.37 million [S5]. The Herald reports her roughly $2.74 million raised far exceeds all six challengers [S6].

## Endorsements

- Elected officials: U.S. Senator Patty Murray, Governor Bob Ferguson, state legislators from districts within CD-1, and mayors and councilmembers from cities including Bellevue, Kirkland, and Redmond [S3][S6].
- Party organizations: King County Democrats, Snohomish County Democrats, and multiple legislative-district Democratic organizations [S3].
- Labor: Washington State Labor Council (AFL-CIO) and affiliated unions including machinists, communications workers, carpenters, educators (NEA), and public employees [S3].
- Advocacy groups: Sierra Club, League of Conservation Voters, Giffords, Moms Demand Action, Planned Parenthood Action Fund, among others [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Fourteen-year incumbent with a substantial legislative record, including a bill signed into law, and a Ways and Means seat; challengers in this race have little or no elected experience [S1][S4][S6].
- Holds national party leadership as DCCC chair for the 2026 cycle, giving her a profile and resources unusual for this district's field [S7].
- Dominant fundraising position (~$2.74M raised vs. minimal amounts for six challengers) and broad establishment, labor, and advocacy-group endorsements [S3][S5][S6].
- The district leans heavily Democratic and uses a top-two primary; the Herald characterizes her as the clear frontrunner in a seven-candidate field with five other Democrats and one Republican [S6].
- Her stated priorities (Child Tax Credit expansion, affordable-housing tax credits, tariff rollback) are backed by corresponding sponsored legislation, so her platform is largely record-based rather than aspirational [S1][S4].
