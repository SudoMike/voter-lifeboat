## Background

James Etzkorn, 41, is a hardware engineer and Monroe School District Board
Director running for U.S. Representative in Washington's 1st Congressional
District with the ballot preference "Independent Party" [S1][S5]. He lives in
unincorporated Snohomish County, in the Echo Lake community, with his wife and
daughter [S3][S5]. He grew up in Vancouver, WA, holds a B.S. in Electrical
Engineering from the University of Washington (2007) and an M.S. in Nanoscale
Engineering from France's National Institute for Applied Sciences (2011)
[S1][S3]. His tech-industry career of over a decade spans semiconductors,
smart contact lenses, batteries, and quantum computers; his campaign says he
holds more than 50 patents and has published in peer-reviewed journals
[S1][S2]. Community involvement includes roughly two decades of classroom
volunteering (math, reading, science tutoring), founding and coaching Monroe
High School's first FIRST Robotics team, and serving on the Echo Lost Lake
Community Group board (2022-2024) [S1][S3]. He describes himself as
historically a Democratic voter who is "tired of settling for the 'least bad'
option," and is challenging incumbent Suzan DelBene (D), who has held the seat
since 2012 [S2][S5]. The race has seven candidates: DelBene, four other
Democrats, one Republican, and Etzkorn as the sole independent [S5].

## Positions

Campaign promises ("says they will") — he holds no federal record.

- **Overall frame:** argues the two-party system has failed and that
  dysfunction and corruption drive up costs of housing, gas, food, and
  healthcare; says he would "break partisan gridlock, force compromise" and
  apply engineering, data-driven methods to governing [S1][S2].
- **Government reform:** supports term limits, campaign finance restrictions
  ("end dark money"), a ban on congressional insider trading, and closing tax
  loopholes used by the wealthy, including the "buy-borrow-die" loophole
  [S1][S2]. Says Congress has failed to hold itself and the Executive Branch
  accountable, leaving governance to executive orders and litigation [S1].
  States he is "completely against the chaos of the current administration"
  but rejects relying on "the same career politicians" [S1].
- **Government efficiency:** platform pillar to "modernize government" —
  measure program results rather than spending amounts, and eliminate waste;
  proposes clear goal-setting and program analysis [S2][S5].
- **Affordability/housing:** would strip "government friction" to increase
  supply of housing, healthcare, and energy [S1]; specifically proposes
  federal housing funding tied to local rezoning, or low-interest loans to
  builders [S5].
- **Wages/economy:** wants to incentivize domestic manufacturing via
  infrastructure and workforce-training investment, arguing labor competition
  raises wages as in 2021 [S1]; supports data-center construction paired with
  required power-plant development as a jobs strategy [S5].
- **Healthcare:** proposes reducing costs through standardized medical-records
  technology and hospital price transparency [S5]; campaign site lists
  preserving individual freedom in healthcare and personal choices [S2].
- **National debt:** platform pillar targets the "$39T debt from bipartisan
  failure" and roughly $1T/year interest payments; would combine spending
  cuts, GDP growth, and closing tax loopholes [S2][S5].
- **Immigration:** supports securing the border "humanely" paired with
  immigration reform; opposes the Trump administration's ICE policies [S2][S5].
- **Energy/climate:** would incentivize clean energy through relaxed
  permitting [S5].
- **Foreign policy:** would end U.S. involvement in the Iran war and "work
  with allies to restore global stability" [S1][S2].

## Record

- Appointed Monroe School District Board Director (Director District 4) in
  March 2023 after interviews at a March 10, 2023 special board meeting;
  approved unanimously by the four sitting directors [S3][S4].
- Ran unopposed in the November 2023 general election to serve the remainder
  of the unexpired term [S4].
- Serves as the board's WIAA (Washington Interscholastic Activities
  Association) representative [S4].
- No specific board votes or policy actions attributable to him were found in
  the sources reviewed.
- Federal campaign finance: about $2,656 in contributions reported as of
  mid-July 2026, per the Everett Herald citing federal filings; the incumbent
  reported about $2.74 million [S5]. (Direct FEC lookup not completed.)
