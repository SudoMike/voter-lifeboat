## Background

Mary Silva, 46, is a clinical audiologist from Everett running with a
"Prefers Republican Party" preference for U.S. Representative in
Washington's 1st Congressional District [S3]. She has 20 years of
experience as a clinical audiologist, including seven years as a managing
partner [S1]. She holds a Doctor of Audiology from Salus University (2009),
a Master of Arts in Audiology/Speech and Hearing Science from Washington
State University (2006), and a Bachelor of Arts in Communication Science
and Disorders from Western Washington University (2003) [S1]. She has
served as a Republican Precinct Committee Officer in Snohomish County
(2022–present) and previously in King County (2014–2016 and 2008–2012),
and was a delegate to the 2024 Republican State Convention and an
alternate to the 2008 convention [S1]. She is a former board member of the
Washington State Academy of Audiology and has volunteered with Big
Brothers Big Sisters and in schools [S1]. She has not held elected office;
she is one of seven candidates in the August 4, 2026 top-two primary, a
field that includes incumbent Suzan DelBene (D) [S3].

## Positions

**Economy, jobs, and taxes**
- Says she will pursue policies that incentivize domestic production to
  reverse offshoring of manufacturing and "return high-quality jobs to
  Washington" [S1].
- Names rising cost of living and bringing cyber-technology and
  manufacturing industries to the U.S. as her top priorities [S3].
- Proposes tax incentives for small and medium-sized businesses, and says
  she would pressure states such as Washington to revamp their tax systems
  and reduce the overall tax burden [S3].
- Calls for addressing offshore tax havens and what she describes as
  corporate welfare, shifting economic focus away from "Wall Street
  financiers and billionaires" toward small and medium-sized businesses;
  supports lowering regulatory barriers and expanding access to capital
  for local entrepreneurs [S1].

**Cybersecurity and digital infrastructure**
- Says she will prioritize federal investment to secure grids,
  communications, and private data against global disruptions and foreign
  interference [S1].
- Supports stricter cybersecurity requirements for corporations that
  receive taxpayer-funded contracts [S3].

**Health care and public health**
- Opposes large combined insurance–health care companies, citing
  UnitedHealth Group as an example, and emphasizes individual choice in
  public health decisions; described COVID-19-era policies as
  "unconscionable" [S3].
- Campaign website emphasizes individual control over medical decisions
  and bodily autonomy [S2].

**Immigration**
- Supports controlled immigration with border security; opposes open
  borders [S3].

**Climate and environment**
- Told the Everett Herald she is "not a big believer in the climate change
  narrative," while expressing support for land stewardship [S3].

**Foreign policy**
- Campaign website questions U.S. support for Israeli military actions
  [S2].

**Tone and approach**
- Pamphlet statement frames her candidacy as a "unifying voice" focused on
  "pragmatic solutions" over "partisan distractions" [S1]; her website
  describes her as an independent thinker focused on accountability [S2].

## Endorsements

- King County Republican Party [S3].
- Stand for Health Freedom, an organization the Everett Herald describes
  as opposing public health measures such as vaccine mandates, school
  health centers, and water fluoridation [S2][S3].

## Scoring notes

- The only Republican-preference candidate in this seven-way primary
  against a Democratic incumbent (Suzan DelBene) and five other
  challengers [S3].
- No elected-office record; her experience is clinical and managerial
  (audiology practice) plus party-organizational roles (PCO, convention
  delegate) [S1].
- Platform centers on reshoring manufacturing, cybersecurity investment,
  and small-business tax relief rather than the social-issue emphases
  common in Republican campaigns [S1][S3].
- Reported only $94 in campaign contributions as of the Everett Herald's
  July 2026 coverage, indicating a minimally funded campaign [S3].
- The Everett Herald reported that she cited authors Christopher Bollyn,
  William Cooper, and G. Edward Griffin as intellectual influences and
  defended their work as "well-referenced"; the Herald notes these figures
  have promoted debunked claims (about 9/11, aliens, and HIV origins), and
  Silva said mainstream media spreads disinformation [S3].
