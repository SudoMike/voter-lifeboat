## Background
Mia Su-Ling Gregerson is the incumbent State Representative for Legislative District 33, Position 2, and has represented the district since 2013 [S1][S7]. She served on the SeaTac City Council and as SeaTac's mayor during the 2008–2015 period, when SeaTac passed the first $15 minimum wage in the country [S1]. She describes herself as a fourth-generation South King County resident, adopted from Taiwan by an Air Force family [S1]. Before the legislature she worked nearly 20 years in the dental field as a surgical assistant and business manager, and she holds a history degree from the University of Washington [S1]. In the House she serves as Vice-Chair of Appropriations, co-chairs a statewide poverty-reduction task force, and says she helped establish Washington's first Office of Equity [S1].

## Positions
- **Affordability / cost of living:** frames the race around rising grocery, gas, and rent costs and points to state programs she worked on as the response — free school meals for every student, emergency food assistance when SNAP benefits dropped, and free transit for kids [S1].
- **Consumer protection:** sponsored and campaigns on Washington's Right to Repair law, which she says saves consumers money on electronics repairs [S1][S3].
- **Transportation:** cites securing millions for the 224th Street corridor and Kent's RapidRide bus line [S1].
- **Immigration and community safety:** her official legislative page highlights 2025–26 work passing bills described as strengthening community safety and protecting immigrants [S2].

## Record
- Sponsored HB 1483 (2025), the Right to Repair Act for consumer electronics, giving consumers and independent repair shops access to parts, tools, and documentation and restricting "parts pairing"; signed into law by Gov. Ferguson in May 2025 [S3].
- Listed as a sponsor of HB 1404 (2025), which requires school districts to provide free breakfast and lunch to any requesting student beginning in the 2026–27 school year [S4].
- Serves as Vice-Chair of the House Appropriations Committee [S1].
- Campaign finance: her 2026 candidate committee reported roughly $29,900 in contributions across 55 reported contribution records in the PDC contributions dataset as of 2026-07-16 [S5].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet statement lists: Washington State Labor Council, OneAmerica Votes, Rep. Adam Smith, Sen. Tina Orwall, Edwin Obras, Gov. Bob Ferguson, "and more" [S1].
- Sierra Club Washington endorsed her in the 2026 primary for LD 33 House Position 2 [S6].

## Scoring notes
- The only candidate in this race with a state legislative record (12+ years) and a budget-writing leadership post (Appropriations vice-chair) [S1][S3][S4].
- Runs on delivered state programs (school meals, right to repair, transit and corridor funding) rather than promises [S1].
- Faces an intra-party challenge from a sitting Burien councilmember, making the Democratic lane contested [S7].
- Largest reported fundraising in the race as of mid-July 2026 [S5].
