## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected from each legislative district, serving a two-year term in the part-time state Legislature. Representatives write and vote on state law and the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets — the levers that control K-12 school funding, state taxes, housing and land-use rules, Medicaid and behavioral-health programs, transit and highway funding, and public-safety statutes. This is the August 4, 2026 top-two primary: the two highest vote-getters advance to the November general election regardless of party.

## The district and this race
LD 33 covers much of south King County: SeaTac, Burien, Des Moines, Normandy Park, and parts of Kent, plus nearby communities [S2]. Three candidates filed for Position 2 [S1]:

- **Mia Su-Ling Gregerson (D, incumbent)** — representative since 2013, former SeaTac mayor, Vice-Chair of House Appropriations; runs on delivered programs (free school meals, Right to Repair, transit funding) [S1].
- **Alex Andrade (D)** — Burien City Councilmember (2024–present) and small business owner challenging the incumbent from within the party, emphasizing spending accountability and public safety [S1][S2].
- **Yuri Marinchik (R)** — first-time candidate, small business owner, running an anti-"career politician," lower-cost-of-living campaign [S1].

The central dynamic is a Democratic incumbent with 12 years of seniority facing a same-party challenge from a sitting local councilmember, with a lone Republican potentially consolidating the district's minority-party vote for one of the two general-election slots. Fundraising as of mid-July 2026: Gregerson ~$29.9k, Andrade ~$15.1k, Marinchik ~$1.5k [S3].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Legislative seniority vs. local-government freshness** — budget-writing leadership and a bill record vs. city-council and small-business perspective [S1].
2. **State spending posture** — Gregerson defends and expands state programs as the affordability answer; Andrade stresses transparency and accountability for growing state spending; Marinchik frames regulation and spending as the problem itself [S1].
3. **Public safety and homelessness approach** — co-response teams and addressing "unmanaged encampments" with accountability (Andrade) vs. poverty-reduction and program delivery framing (Gregerson) [S1].
4. **Party and role-of-government** — the only R-vs-D axis runs through Marinchik; both Democrats support abortion rights and equality-oriented policy [S1].
5. **Local control** — Andrade's opposition to unfunded state mandates on cities is a structural difference from the incumbent, who operates the state-budget levers those mandates come from [S1].
