## Background

Jan Trasen is an Administrative Law Judge with the Washington State Office of
Administrative Hearings (Child & Family Care Division) and a Judge Pro Tem for
King County District Court and Edmonds Municipal Court [S1][S2]. Before
becoming an ALJ, she spent 26 years as a public defender and appellate
attorney — 16 years with the Washington Appellate Project and 10 years with
the Legal Aid Society in New York City trial courts — arguing criminal and
dependency cases before the Washington Supreme Court and Court of Appeals
[S1][S2]. She holds a J.D. from Boston College Law School, a B.A. from Tufts
University, and a California teaching credential; before law she taught
middle-school English and ESL (Teach for America) [S1][S2]. She lives in
Kirkland, where she raised her two daughters [S1].

## Positions

- Describes her core commitments as fairness, equal justice, and ensuring
  courts work for everyone; says she treats all in her courtroom with respect
  [S1].
- Advocates community-focused approaches: mental-health and substance-use
  treatment programs designed to lower recidivism and strengthen neighborhood
  safety [S2].
- Emphasizes that her trial, appellate, administrative, and judicial
  experience across "every level of the legal system" prepares her to serve
  from day one [S1][S2].

## Record

- As an ALJ she conducts hearings and issues final orders in cases affecting
  children's safety, family stability, and access to services and medical
  care [S2]. As Judge Pro Tem she presides over jury trials, arraignments,
  civil protection orders, and small-claims matters [S2].
- She states she is "the only candidate with full-time judicial experience"
  in this race (her ALJ role); this is her characterization [S1].
- States she received "Exceptionally Well Qualified" ratings from five
  independent local bar associations; the individual associations are not
  named in her statement and the ratings were not independently verified
  (self-reported, Tier 1) [S1].
- Serves on the Cardozo Society Judicial Evaluation Committee [S2].

## Endorsements

- King County Democrats (independently confirmed on the organization's 2026
  endorsement list) [S3].
- Per her pamphlet statement: a majority of the Washington Supreme Court;
  over 75 appellate/trial judges including a majority of King County District
  Court judges; Commissioner Kuderer; Senators Dhingra, Slatter, and
  Stanford; Redmond Mayor Birney; National Women's Political Caucus;
  Machinists; ATU; 41st, 45th, and 48th LD Democrats [S1].

## Campaign finance

- PDC summary (accessed 2026-07-16): about $28,276 raised, $12,495 spent —
  second-highest fundraising in this three-way race [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in this race whose day job is full-time judging
  (administrative law), on top of pro tem service in the very court at issue
  [S1][S2].
- Professional background is defense/appellate-side (26 years as public
  defender and appellate counsel), contrasting with Tse's prosecution
  background and Schaer's government civil background [S1][S2].
- Holds the King County Democrats endorsement and claims the largest slate of
  judicial endorsers, including a stated majority of the court she seeks to
  join [S1][S3]. (Note: this is a nonpartisan judicial office; party
  endorsements indicate organizational support, not judicial philosophy.)
- Claims "Exceptionally Well Qualified" ratings from five bar associations —
  self-reported and unverified; each of the three candidates self-reports
  top ratings from different associations [S1].
