## Background

Bianca Tse is a Bellevue-based attorney and Judge Pro Tem at King County
District Court — the court she is running to join — and also serves as Judge
Pro Tem for the cities of Mercer Island, Kirkland, and Issaquah [S1][S2].
She immigrated from Hong Kong as a child and grew up in Renton [S3]. She
spent 16 years as a King County Deputy Prosecuting Attorney, trying cases
including homicides, assaults, robberies, and domestic-violence matters
[S1][S3]. Since 2015 she has run Tse & Associates, a private firm serving
the Asian community with bilingual services in real-estate and
landlord-tenant law [S3]. She holds a B.A. from the University of Washington
and a J.D. from Tulane University School of Law [S1].

## Positions

- Campaign themes: "Integrity. Fairness. Experience." and being "Ready On
  Day One," resting on her trial and pro tem experience rather than a policy
  platform [S1][S2].
- Says she is known for legal knowledge, careful consideration of all who
  appear before her, and treating everyone with respect and fairness [S1].
- Told Northwest Asian Weekly she was encouraged to run by sitting judges
  and cited a domestic-violence prosecution — a victim's gratitude at being
  believed — as a motivating experience [S3].

## Record

- Judge Pro Tem across King County courts since 2021 (her pamphlet says "as
  judge for the past five years") [S1][S3].
- 16 years as a deputy prosecutor handling matters from misdemeanors to
  felonies, including homicide jury trials [S1][S3].
- States the Loren Miller Bar Association rated her "Exceptionally Well
  Qualified" (its highest rating) and that two other bar associations did the
  same; self-reported [S1]. Northwest Asian Weekly reported the Asian Bar
  Association of Washington gave her its highest designation ("Extremely
  Well Qualified") [S3].

## Endorsements

- Per her pamphlet statement: former King County Prosecutor Daniel
  Satterberg; Senator Manka Dhingra; Representative My-Linh Thai; Bellevue
  Mayor Malakoutian; Bellevue Councilmembers Nieuwenhuis and Sumadiwirya;
  King County Superior, District, and Municipal Court judges; councilmembers
  from Mercer Island, Redmond, Issaquah, Newcastle, Sammamish, and Edmonds;
  National Women's Political Caucus of Washington [S1].

## Campaign finance

- PDC summary (accessed 2026-07-16): about $42,252 raised, $30,196 spent —
  the largest fundraising and spending totals in this race [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race with a career prosecution background (16 years
  as deputy prosecutor, including homicide trials); Trasen comes from
  defense/appellate work and Schaer from government civil practice [S1][S3].
- Currently sits pro tem in the specific court at issue (King County
  District Court) plus three Eastside municipal courts, with about five
  years of pro tem service [S1][S3].
- Her Loren Miller Bar Association "Exceptionally Well Qualified" rating is
  the only bar rating in this race with partial third-party corroboration
  (the ABAW rating was reported by Northwest Asian Weekly); other rating
  claims by all three candidates are self-reported [S1][S3].
- Leads the field in campaign fundraising [S4].
