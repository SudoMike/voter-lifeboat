## Background
Emily Alvarado (Prefers Democratic Party) is the sitting State Senator for the 34th Legislative District (West Seattle, Vashon Island, White Center, west Burien), serving since January 2025 [S1][S4]. She was appointed by the King County Council on January 21, 2025, to fill the seat vacated by Joe Nguyen, who left to lead the Washington State Department of Commerce [S4][S5]. She previously served as State Representative for the 34th LD (2023–2025) [S1]. Before elected office she was Director of the Seattle Office of Housing, where her pamphlet statement says she oversaw $290 million in investments in anti-displacement, supportive housing, renter protection, and homeownership programs [S1]; she is an attorney and works as an executive at a national affordable-housing nonprofit [S1][S3]. Education: BA, Scripps College (summa cum laude); JD, University of Washington School of Law (Gates Public Service Law Scholar) [S1].

## Positions
- Housing: campaign centers on affordable housing; says she will push reforms to make homeownership more affordable and cites legislative work on caps for housing investors [S1][S3].
- Taxes/affordability: says she championed "historic tax reform" (the 2026 "millionaires tax") paired with tax relief for working families and small businesses; wants further affordability measures on healthcare, childcare, and groceries [S1].
- Consumer/worker protection: cites protections passed for renters, workers, and consumers, and website references her surge-pricing regulation bill (SB 5600) [S1][S3].
- Social policy: supports reproductive freedom, "common sense gun reform," and climate action [S1].
- Education/safety net: says she will fight for school funding and a stronger safety net for youth and seniors [S1].

## Record
- Appointed 34th LD State Senator January 2025; previously elected State Representative in 2022 [S1][S4][S5].
- Campaign website references legislative work on housing-investor caps, surge-pricing regulation (SB 5600), and federal benefits for foster youth [S3].

## Endorsements
Campaign website lists 20+ organizational endorsements including SEIU, ILWU 19, UFCW 3000, IAM 751, WEA, AFT-Washington, and NWPC (portions of the list are labeled 2024) [S3].

- Platform emphasizes progressive tax reform and affordability legislation already enacted [S1].
