## Background
Topher Leritz states no party preference and is running as an explicitly nonpartisan candidate for the LD5 Position 1 seat; candidate filings list him as being from Enumclaw [S1][S3]. He has no elected experience [S1]. His professional experience spans marketing management, political consulting, and work as a private investigator [S1]. He holds an A.A. from Everett Community College and a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Washington [S1]. His community service includes Mothers Against Drunk Driving, the Brain Injury Association of Washington, and Washington Offroad Recovery [S1]. He describes himself as "not a career politician" who works locally and spends free time on outdoor conservation [S2].

## Positions
- **Campaign practices:** Accepts "zero endorsements or special interest donations," which he frames as putting community above party [S1].
- **Governance:** Criticizes current leadership as focused on "reelection campaigns, political outrage, and national talking points" instead of local problems; emphasizes practical solutions, immediate action, and accountability [S1][S2].
- **Housing / cost of living:** Advocates affordable-housing solutions and addressing rising costs squeezing families [S2].
- **Economy:** Supports local businesses and family-wage job creation [S2].
- **Public safety / mental health:** Wants improved public-safety and mental-health services in the district [S2].
- **Representation:** Pledges to represent all district residents regardless of party [S1].

## Record
- No elected office or legislative record [S1].
- Consistent with his no-donations pledge, no contributions for this campaign appear in the PDC contributions dataset as of 2026-07-16 [S1][S4].

## Endorsements
None — the candidate states he is accepting no endorsements as a matter of principle [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- The only candidate in the race running outside both major parties, with process-reform (anti-partisan, anti-special-interest) themes as his core differentiator [S1].
- Platform is framed at a general level (affordability, housing, safety, accountability) without specific legislative proposals, in contrast to opponents' concrete policy commitments [S2].
- Zero-fundraising, zero-endorsement campaign model means minimal campaign infrastructure relative to Hall ($44k) and Bennett ($21k) [S4].
