## Background

Edwin Obras is the incumbent State Representative for the 33rd District, Position 1 [S1]. He was appointed to the House in December 2024 to fill the vacancy created when Rep. Tina Orwall moved to the state Senate, then won the November 2025 special election over Burien Mayor Kevin Schilling, 50.57% to 47.72% (a 641-vote margin) [S1][S4]. He immigrated from the Philippines as a child, has lived in SeaTac for 23 years, and was the first in his family to graduate from college (BA, University of Washington; MPA, Northeastern University) [S1]. He spent 18 years at the Seattle Human Services Department, rising from program specialist to Division Director, working on homelessness, anti-hunger programs, youth development, and community safety [S1]. He is a past board president of the Filipino Community of Seattle, co-chaired its affordable-housing capital campaign, and chaired the SeaTac Human Services Advisory Committee [S1]. In the House he serves as Assistant Majority Whip and sits on the Health Care & Wellness, Labor & Workplace Standards, and Community Safety committees [S2][S3].

## Positions

- **Affordability/housing:** Frames affordability as the core issue ("Your rent went up $400. Your paycheck didn't."); champions rent-increase caps and anti-displacement housing legislation (says he will, building on record) [S1][S2].
- **Workers:** Prioritizes worker protections; sponsors pending legislation limiting credit-card tip-skimming and strengthening asbestos safety training [S2].
- **Immigrant rights:** Pledges to continue "fighting for immigrant rights" [S1].
- **Public safety:** Focuses on jail reform and community safety standards alongside his background-check and police-impersonation laws [S2].
- **Healthcare:** Advocates expanded mental health resources and nursing-assistant regulation [S2].

## Record

- Six of his bills became law in his first full session (2025-26 biennium), per his pamphlet statement and campaign site: protections for isolated workers (2SHB 1524), strengthened firearm background checks (SHB 1490), regulation of rideshare/transportation network companies (ESHB 1332), nursing title protections (including against AI misuse), and a law against false identification as a peace officer [S1][S2].
- Says he secured over $30 million in state investments for the district, including housing and health projects [S1].
- Serves as Assistant Majority Whip; named "Rookie of the Year" by FairVote Washington [S2][S3].
- Won a closely contested 2025 special election against a fellow Democrat, Burien Mayor Kevin Schilling, who ran on taxes, public safety, and government accountability [S4].

## Endorsements

Pamphlet lists: Washington State Labor Council, 33rd District Democrats, Washington Conservation Action, Planned Parenthood, Washington State Nurses Association, Governor Bob Ferguson, Sen. Tina Orwall, and Rep. Mia Gregerson [S1]. Campaign site adds UFCW 3000, Teamsters Local 117, SEIU Local 6, and House Speaker Laurie Jinkins, among other elected officials [S2].

## Financials

PDC records show about $253,278 raised across 431 contributions for the 2025 special election and about $18,540 across 42 contributions so far for 2026 [S5]. Both Republican opponents combined have raised under $6,000 [S5].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate in this race with legislative or governmental experience: an incumbent with six bills signed in his first full session and a leadership post (Assistant Majority Whip) [S1][S3].
- Labor-progressive profile (labor council, major unions, Planned Parenthood, conservation groups) contrasts with two Republican challengers running on tax and spending restraint [S1].
- His policy emphasis — rent caps, worker protections, immigrant rights — is the direct inverse of both opponents' affordability-via-lower-taxes framing [S1][S2].
- Overwhelming resource advantage: roughly 30x the fundraising of both opponents combined in this cycle [S5].
