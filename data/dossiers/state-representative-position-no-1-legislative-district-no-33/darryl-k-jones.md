## Background

Darryl K. Jones is a Republican candidate from Kent running for State Representative, 33rd District, Position 1 [S1]. His only prior political role is precinct committee officer in the 33rd Legislative District [S1]. He describes decades of work in public-facing roles: manager at a healthcare company, trainer for an HMO, and customer service positions in housing, technology, hospitality, and banking [S1]. He studied computer science with a mathematics minor at California State University, Hayward [S1]. He has tutored low-income students at a public housing project [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes/spending:** Core message is fiscal restraint — "Washington has a spending problem"; criticizes Democratic majorities for "an income tax, an increase in spending and higher taxes" and pledges never to vote to increase constituents' costs (says he will) [S1].
- **Affordability:** Says he will work to make life in Washington more affordable and to protect people "from greedy politicians" [S1].
- **Framing:** Runs on "policy over party," arguing one-party dominance has not benefited voters [S1].

## Endorsements

None found in the pamphlet, on his website, or in news coverage [S1][S2].
