## What the office does
One of two state representatives for the 1st Legislative District (Bothell area / north King–Snohomish suburbs) in the Washington State House. Two-year term; votes on the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets and all state legislation.

## Race dynamics
Two candidates — incumbent Democrat Davina Duerr and Republican first-time candidate Maggie Wang — so both advance from the primary to the November general election regardless of primary results. Research depth is intentionally light for this reason.

## Differentiating axes
- Incumbency and legislative record (Duerr chairs the Local Government Committee and cites seven bills signed last term) vs. an outsider small-business challenger with no elected experience.
- Tax-and-spend orientation: Duerr emphasizes public investment (housing, transit, I-405 funding); Wang runs on lower taxes and reduced government spending.
- Housing approach: permit streamlining and affordable-housing subsidy (Duerr) vs. cost-reduction through lower taxes/regulation (Wang).
