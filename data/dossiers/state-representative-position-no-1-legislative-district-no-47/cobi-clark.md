## Background

Cobi Clark is the Libertarian candidate in this race [S1]. He is a housing developer and construction project manager, and a South Sound Business "Top 40 Under 40" honoree; he says he has helped deliver hundreds of homes across Washington, including senior living, single-family housing, market-rate apartments, and middle housing [S1]. He holds an interdisciplinary bachelor's degree in economics, policy analysis, politics, and philosophy from UW Tacoma; during his studies he worked as policy staff for State Senator Jeff Wilson and as a business advocate for the Tacoma-Pierce Chamber of Commerce, and says his academic policy work has been peer-reviewed and published [S1]. He is a member of Auburn Rotary [S1]. He ran for Auburn City Council in November 2025, losing to Lisa Stirgus [S4].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Increase housing supply; argues from developer experience that excessive regulation, rising taxes, and bureaucratic delay drive up housing costs [S1][S2].
- **Taxes/spending:** Fiscal responsibility, accountable government, and continued opposition to a state income tax [S1][S2].
- **Energy:** Abundant, reliable, affordable energy; ties energy costs to regulation [S1][S2].
- **Civil liberties:** Strong civil liberties and constitutional rights; "safe communities that respect both public safety and individual rights" [S1][S2].
- **Healthcare:** Improve affordability [S2].
- **Education:** Supporting parents and schools [S2].
- His platform is organized under "Enact, Reform, and Repeal" categories, emphasizing "transparency, accountability, and real-world results rather than political slogans and partisan division" [S2].

## Endorsements

- Individuals: Auburn City Councilmembers Lisa Stirgus, Brian Lott, and Cheryl Rakes; former Auburn Councilmember Yolanda Trout [S2].
- Organizations: 47th District Republicans; Libertarian Party of Washington State; Stand for Health Freedom [S2].

## Scoring notes

- The only non-Democratic-aligned candidate in the field (Libertarian), endorsed by both his party and the 47th District Republicans — likely consolidating the district's right-of-center vote in a race with no Republican filed [S1][S2][S4].
- Only candidate with hands-on housing-development experience; his housing answer is supply-side deregulation rather than public funding [S1].
- Explicit anti-income-tax, limited-government fiscal stance distinguishes him from all three Democratic-aligned candidates [S1][S2].
- Raised about $19,526 across 59 contributions as of mid-July 2026 — comparable to Evans and above the incumbent [S3]; lost an Auburn City Council race in 2025 [S4].
