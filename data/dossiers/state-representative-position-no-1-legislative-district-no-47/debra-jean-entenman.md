## Background

Debra Entenman (D-Kent) is the incumbent State Representative for the 47th District, Position 1, serving since 2018 [S1][S7]. She was first elected in 2018 by defeating Republican Mark Hargrove and was re-elected in 2024 with 56% of the vote [S7]. Before the legislature she spent 12 years as a congressional staffer serving South King County, including three years as District Director [S1]. She was a member of the first Seattle/King County Head Start class and describes herself as a non-traditional college graduate; she holds a bachelor's degree in political science from Seattle University [S1]. She serves on the Washington State Board for Community & Technical Colleges, served 11 years on the Renton Technical College Board of Trustees, and is a member of the Urban League of Metropolitan Seattle and the NAACP [S1]. She is a member of the Legislative Black Caucus [S2].

## Positions

- **Public safety:** Data-driven approaches pairing investment with accountability — independent investigations of deadly-force incidents, hiring standards emphasizing education and anti-bias training, social workers and crisis counselors in public-safety response, and investment in mental health, housing, food assistance, and violence-reduction programs [S2].
- **Consumer data rights:** Require companies and government to disclose what personal data they collect and how it is used, with consumer control and informed consent for storage and monetization [S2].
- **Economy/workers:** Support small businesses and farmers, workforce training and reskilling for vulnerable workers, and improved food-supply logistics [S2].
- **Safety net:** Expand TANF, eviction-prevention assistance, childcare and healthcare access, and transportation for elderly and special-needs populations [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Broadband/technology access as essential utilities; funding equity for community colleges and institutions serving students of color [S2]; life-changing value of early learning and affordable education is a stated throughline [S1].
- Emphasizes working "across the aisle" to protect vulnerable households [S1].

## Record

- Committees: Housing; Civil Rights & Judiciary; Postsecondary Education & Workforce; Transportation [S1][S3].
- Prime sponsor of HB 2015 (2025), "Improving public safety funding by providing resources to local governments and state and local criminal justice agencies, and authorizing a local option tax" — signed May 19, 2025 (Chapter 350, Laws of 2025) [S4]. Her pamphlet says the bill secured $100 million for public safety [S1].
- Prime sponsor of HB 1559 (2023), the Student Basic Needs at Public Postsecondary Institutions Act — signed May 11, 2023 (Chapter 421, Laws of 2023); her pamphlet describes it as creating benefits navigators to help college students access wraparound support [S1][S5].
- Campaign finance: about $16,227 in reported contributions across 25 donations for the 2026 cycle as of mid-July 2026 — the lowest total among the race's four candidates [S6].

## Endorsements

None found beyond the pamphlet and campaign site, which do not list endorsements [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in this race with a legislative record: eight years in the seat, verifiable signed legislation on public-safety funding (HB 2015) and college-student basic needs (HB 1559), and four committee assignments [S3][S4][S5].
- Public-safety approach pairs new funding with accountability measures, contrasting with Evans's mandatory-treatment "three strikes" proposal and Hans's root-causes emphasis [S1][S2].
- Deep South King County institutional ties (congressional district staff, community/technical college governance, Head Start background) [S1].
- Notably light fundraising for an incumbent this cycle — outraised by all three challengers [S6].
