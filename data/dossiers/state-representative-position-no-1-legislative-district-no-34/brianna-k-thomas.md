## Background
Brianna K. Thomas (Prefers Democratic Party) has served as State Representative for the 34th Legislative District since January 2025 [S1][S4]. The King County Council appointed her on January 21, 2025, to fill the House seat vacated when Emily Alvarado was appointed to the State Senate [S4]; she then won the November 2025 special election for the seat [S5]. She is a Senior Policy Advisor for Seattle's Human Services Department, and previously served as Chief of Staff to Seattle City Council President Lorena González and as the Mayor's Office labor liaison [S1][S4]. Her organizing background includes managing the 2013 SeaTac $15 minimum wage campaign, leading Seattle's Honest Elections initiative that created the democracy voucher program, and serving as field director for the Washington Low Income Housing Alliance Action Fund [S1][S3]. Education: BA in Political Science, University of Washington (2010) [S1].

## Positions
- Labor/wages: emphasizes minimum-wage and worker-protection work; says she passed bills guaranteeing meal and rest breaks for healthcare workers and protections for domestic workers [S1][S3].
- Taxes: says she contributed to passing the "Millionaires Tax," which she describes as tax reform benefiting working Washingtonians [S1].
- Transit: says she passed legislation to "keep transit moving" [S1].
- Privacy/bodily autonomy: says she passed legislation ensuring employees' privacy and bodily autonomy [S1].
- Democracy reform and affordable housing are recurring themes of her organizing career [S1][S3].

## Record
- Appointed 34th LD State Representative January 2025; won the November 2025 special election [S4][S5].
- Pamphlet cites sponsored-and-passed bills on healthcare-worker meal/rest breaks, domestic-worker protections, transit, and employee privacy (bill numbers not specified) [S1].

## Endorsements
Pamphlet lists: 34th LD Democrats, Washington State Labor Council, Attorney General Nick Brown, King County Councilmember Teresa Mosqueda, UFCW, WFSE/AFSCME, "and many more" [S1].

## Scoring notes
