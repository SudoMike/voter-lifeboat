## Background

Claudia Kauffman is the incumbent State Senator for the 47th Legislative District (Kent, Auburn, Covington area), serving since 2022 [S1][S3]. She previously served in the same Senate seat from 2007 to 2011 and describes herself as the first Native American woman elected to the Washington State Senate [S2]. Her professional experience includes serving as Intergovernmental Affairs Liaison for the Muckleshoot Indian Tribe and co-founding Native Action Network [S1][S2]. She holds an associate's degree from Northwest Indian College and a certificate in project management from the UW School of Business [S1]. She formerly chaired the Green River College Board of Trustees [S1].

## Positions

- **Affordability (top stated priority):** says her priority is "making Washington affordable for everyone," citing housing, rent, healthcare, groceries, and gas costs [S1].
- **Housing:** supports increasing housing supply and "protecting renters from predatory price gouging"; says she has championed legislation to build more housing and will continue pushing for middle-income housing [S1].
- **Taxes:** says she has worked for property tax relief for seniors [S1].
- **Childcare / safety net:** says she has worked to expand childcare access for working parents and wants a robust social safety net alongside living-wage jobs [S1].
- **Other stated priorities:** her campaign site lists public safety (including road safety), public education, environmental protection, and diversity and equity [S2].

## Record

- Serves as chair of the Senate Business, Trade & Economic Development Committee, vice chair of State Government, Tribal Affairs & Elections, and a member of Ways & Means [S3].
- Her campaign cites sponsored bills including SB 5683 (foster care coordination between the state and Tribal governments), SB 5811 (expanded caregiver eligibility for home-based family care), SB 5342 (transit agency bus purchasing), and SB 5494 (lead-based paint removal safety) [S2]. (Self-reported; bill outcomes not independently verified at this research depth.)
- Her campaign cites infrastructure funding she helped secure, including $15 million for Highway 18 upgrades, $5 million for SR 516 expansion in Covington, and $2 million for Auburn Theater renovation [S2]. (Self-reported.)

## Endorsements

Per her pamphlet statement: the mayors of Auburn, Covington, and Kent; King County Executive Girmay Zahilay; the 47th District Democrats; and the Washington State Labor Council [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Incumbent with a legislative record and committee chairmanship, versus a challenger whose experience is at the city-council level [S1][S3].
- Emphasizes renter protections and state investment (childcare, safety net, infrastructure) as tools for affordability, in contrast to her opponent's spending-restraint and tax-opposition framing [S1][S2].
- Both candidates in this race name affordability, housing supply, and public safety as priorities; the difference is largely in means (state programs and protections vs. tax/spending restraint and deregulation) [S1][S2].
- Record items beyond her committee roles come from her own campaign materials and were not independently verified at light research depth [S2].
