## What the office does

A state senator is one of 49 members of the Washington State Senate, serving a four-year term. Senators write and vote on state laws, set the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets, and confirm gubernatorial appointments. The 47th Legislative District covers the Kent, Auburn, and Covington area of South King County.

## Race dynamics

Only two candidates filed, so both advance from the August primary to the November general election regardless of the primary result. The race pits incumbent Democrat Claudia Kauffman — the sitting senator, first elected to this seat in 2022 after previously holding it 2007–2011, and chair of the Senate Business, Trade & Economic Development Committee — against Republican Kristina Soltys, a Covington City Councilmember since 2019. A second Republican, Carmen Goers, withdrew before the primary, per Kent Reporter coverage. Both candidates center affordability, housing supply, public safety funding, and SR-516/traffic improvements, so the top-line issue lists overlap heavily.

## Differentiating axes

- **Fiscal/tax approach:** Kauffman emphasizes state investment (childcare access, senior property-tax relief, safety net, infrastructure dollars she cites securing); Soltys opposes recent state tax increases (B&O, gas tax), rejects an income tax, and frames the state as having a spending problem.
- **Housing policy means:** both want more supply, but Kauffman pairs it with renter protections against "predatory price gouging," while Soltys favors permitting reform and market incentives and opposes state mandates.
- **Experience type:** legislative incumbency with a committee chairmanship and Olympia record vs. municipal council experience with local public-safety and public-spaces initiatives.
- **Party alignment:** Prefers Democratic Party vs. Prefers Republican Party, which tracks the fiscal and regulatory differences above; public safety is largely a shared priority and is less differentiating in this race.
