## Background

Rob Foxcurran is a Senior Appraiser for the City of Seattle and a Certified General Real Estate Appraiser, and formerly served as a Hearing Examiner on the King County Board of Appeals and Equalization [S1][S4]. He holds a B.A. from the University of Washington and is a Seattle Public Schools graduate [S1]. He describes himself as a fifth-generation Seattleite and states his career has included high-level commercial valuation in the private sector [S1]. In his City of Seattle role, he provides real estate valuation services across departments, including work identifying land for parks and flagging publicly owned parcels for potential affordable-housing use [S6]. He is a PROTEC17 member and shop steward, and states he has lived in income-restricted housing [S1]. He registered his candidate committee with the PDC on January 8, 2026, and declared candidacy on May 4, 2026 [S3]. The race is open: incumbent Assessor John Wilson is not seeking another term, and other candidates include Chief Deputy Assessor Al Dams and Shoreline Councilmember Chris Roberts [S4][S1 (Dams statement, same page)]. As of mid-July 2026, his committee reported $59,226.00 in contributions, $29,507.89 in expenditures, and $2,000.00 in debts [S3].

## Positions

### Property taxes and affordability
- Says he will push at the state level to lift the 1% levy cap, establish homestead protections for primary residences, and create a renter's tax credit [S1]; his website frames this as a "universal homestead exemption" plus expanded relief programs for vulnerable populations [S2].
- Says he will ensure large corporate property owners "pay their fair share," asserting that corporations use appeals experts and lawyers to shift tax burden onto homeowners, renters, and small businesses [S1][S2].
- Proposes flexible monthly property-tax payment options instead of lump sums, and cites income-tied approaches to prevent displacement [S2].
- Identifies affordability as a central issue, citing residents displaced or facing homelessness due to unexpected tax bills [S2].

### Transparency and administration
- Says he will bring transparency and accountability to the assessment system, including digital tools for property-assessment transparency [S1][S4 context; S2].
- Says he will ensure large commercial properties are assessed as rigorously as residential properties [S2].
- Says he will assess small-business personal property taxes accurately and fairly [S2].

### Housing
- Says he would use the assessor's data and authority to identify surplus public land and coordinate across agencies to facilitate affordable-housing development [S2].

### Labor
- Cites his PROTEC17 shop-steward role and states workers deserve to organize and fight for fair treatment [S1]; PROTEC17's endorsement announcement describes his stated support for collective bargaining and a professionalized public workforce [S6].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race describing hands-on valuation work: current Senior Appraiser (City of Seattle) and Certified General Real Estate Appraiser, plus prior service as a Hearing Examiner on the King County Board of Appeals and Equalization, the body that hears assessment appeals [S1][S4].
- Platform pairs administrative changes (commercial-assessment rigor, transparency tools, payment flexibility) with state-legislative advocacy (levy cap, homestead exemption, renter's credit) that the Assessor cannot enact alone [S1][S2].
- Carries the Democratic party-organization endorsement slate (county party plus multiple LD organizations) and a Seattle Times editorial endorsement in a nonpartisan race against an internal-office candidate (Chief Deputy Assessor Al Dams) and an elected-official candidate (Shoreline Councilmember Chris Roberts) [S2][S5][S4][S1].
- Union-affiliated candidate profile: PROTEC17 member/shop steward with that union's endorsement [S1][S6].
- Fundraising of roughly $59k reported to the PDC as of mid-July 2026 [S3].
