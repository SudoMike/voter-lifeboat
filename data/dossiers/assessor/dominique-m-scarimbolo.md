## Background

Dominique M Scarimbolo is a small business owner, a King County resident of more than 35 years, and King County Chapter President of the Citizens Alliance for Property Rights (CAPR) [S1]. Scarimbolo holds a B.A. in Criminal Justice from Washington State University [S1]. The campaign website describes prior work at Alaska Airlines, including service as a union shop steward with IAM Local 2202, and involvement in family small-business ventures including Four Horsemen Brewery; it states Scarimbolo lives in rural unincorporated King County [S2]. PDC filings list a Kent address for the campaign committee [S3]. The pamphlet statement says, "I am proudly not a politician," and describes personal experience going through the property tax appeal process [S1]. Local press coverage of the August 2026 primary lists Scarimbolo among the candidates for Assessor in an open race after incumbent John Wilson did not refile [S4].

PDC records under the same filer ID show a prior candidacy: in 2021, filing under the name Dominique Torgerson, Scarimbolo ran for King County Council (Position 7), qualified for the general election, and lost in the general [S3].

## Positions

- **Property tax reform:** States that the growing property tax burden in King County is "unsustainable" for families, retirees, and small business owners, and calls for property tax reform "now" [S1].
- **Uniformity of assessments:** Asserts, based on self-described independent research, that the current system "produces highly non-uniform results, violating both state law and our WA state constitution," and that in a system legally required to have uniformity, "results are ultimately what matter" [S1].
- **Transparency and due process:** States the current system "completely lacks transparency, consistency, accountability, and any kind of meaningful due process for taxpayers"; campaign messaging centers on "Transparency. Accountability. Fairness." and a system property owners can "understand, challenge fairly, and trust" [S1][S2].
- **Audits of the assessment system:** Says they have "recently developed and submitted multiple audit proposals targeting our current property taxation system" [S1].
- **Taxpayer orientation:** Frames the campaign as "putting taxpayers first," with goals of understandable assessments, transparent valuation methods, fairly and uniformly applied standards, and taxpayer rights respected in the appeals process [S1][S2].
- **Commitment beyond the election:** States an intent to continue working on property tax reform regardless of whether elected [S1].

## Record

- Self-reported advocacy activities (not independently verified in sources reviewed): reviewing assessment data, submitting public records requests, filing property tax appeals, researching appraisal data, and submitting audit proposals concerning the property taxation system [S1].
- Campaign finance: the 2026 Assessor campaign registered with the PDC on 2026-05-15 (declared 2026-05-07) under the "mini reporting" option, meaning the committee expects to stay under the statutory fundraising threshold and does not file detailed contribution and expenditure reports; no reports were on file as of 2026-07-16 [S3].
- Prior candidacy: 2021 King County Council (Position 7) run under the name Dominique Torgerson, also a mini-reporting committee, which advanced from the primary and lost in the general election [S3].

## Endorsements

- Rated "Best Choice" for King County Assessor in the King County Republican Party voters guide [S5]. No endorsements are listed in the pamphlet statement or on the campaign website [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in this race campaigning explicitly as a non-politician outsider; opponents include the chief deputy assessor, a hearing examiner, and a four-term Shoreline city councilmember [S1][S4].
- Perspective is that of a taxpayer/appellant and property-rights organization chapter president rather than an assessment-administration or elected-office background [S1].
- Central claim is that current assessments are non-uniform in violation of state law and the state constitution, based on the candidate's own research; the proposed remedy emphasizes audits, transparency, and appeals-process reform [S1].
- Mini-reporting PDC status indicates a low-budget campaign relative to opponents filing itemized reports [S3].
- The office is nonpartisan; the King County Republican Party's "Best Choice" rating is the only third-party rating found [S5].
