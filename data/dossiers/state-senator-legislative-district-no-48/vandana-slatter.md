## Background

Incumbent State Senator for the 48th LD (prefers Democratic Party); vice chair
of the Environment, Energy & Technology and Health & Long-Term Care committees
and member of the Higher Education & Workforce Development Committee [S1].
Appointed to the Senate on January 7, 2025 to fill the seat vacated when Patty
Kuderer became state Insurance Commissioner [S3]. Previously a 4-term 48th LD
State Representative and Bellevue City Councilmember [S1]. Licensed clinical
pharmacist with 20+ years as a medical scientist in biotechnology companies
(including Amgen and Genentech per her campaign site); Doctor of Pharmacy and
MPA from the University of Washington [S1][S2].

## Positions

- Affordable healthcare, reproductive freedom, support for immigrants, and a
  "tax code that is fair to working people" [S1].
- Says she will continue work on lowering costs for working families, public
  education and childcare, scientific research, keeping hospitals open, and
  countering "harmful federal overreach" [S1].

## Record

- Lead sponsor of the "My Health My Data" Act (HB 1155), a consumer health
  data privacy law, and of a constitutional amendment protecting abortion
  rights [S1][S3].
- Says she voted for affordable housing, transit/transportation funding,
  career training, gun safety laws, climate action, and medical debt
  reduction [S1].

## Endorsements

King County and 48th District Democrats; WA State Labor Council; teachers and
nurses unions; conservation voters; Planned Parenthood Advocates; National
Women's Political Caucus; mayors of Redmond, Bellevue, and Kirkland;
Councilmember Balducci; AG Nick Brown; Insurance Commissioner Kuderer;
Treasurer Pellicciotti; former Gov. Locke (all per candidate's own list)
[S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- Sole candidate; advances to the general regardless of primary outcome.
- Incumbent with an 8+ year legislative record (House then Senate) and a
  healthcare/science professional background.
