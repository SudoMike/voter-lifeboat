## Background

Theo Angelis (Theodore J. Angelis, 54, of Seattle) is a sitting Washington State Supreme Court justice, appointed by Governor Bob Ferguson in March 2026 to succeed retiring Justice Barbara Madsen; he began serving on April 4, 2026 [S4][S5]. He is running to fill the remainder of Madsen's term, which runs through January 2029 [S5]. He describes himself as the first justice of Middle Eastern descent on the court, citing Turkish and Greek ancestry [S1][S4].

Before his appointment, Angelis spent 26 years as a partner at the Seattle firm K&L Gates, where he led the firm's appellate practice and later served as global leader of its intellectual property practice [S1][S4]. His pro bono work included class-action litigation that secured appointed counsel for children in immigration proceedings, for which he received the ACLU's Humanitarian Award (2017) and the Founders Award from Kids in Need of Defense (2018) [S1][S4]. He also represented an Army veteran detained by ICE for seven months, work recognized by the Northwest Immigrant Rights Project [S1].

He holds a BA from Claremont McKenna College (summa cum laude), master's degrees from Oxford University and the London School of Economics, and a JD from Yale Law School, where he was an articles editor of the Yale Law Journal [S1][S4]. His bar and community service includes serving as a King County Bar Association trustee, president of the Middle Eastern Legal Association of Washington, editing the Washington Lawyers Practice Manual for over a decade, and leading efforts to include Indian law on the Washington bar exam [S1][S4]. Washington Women Lawyers rated him "Exceptionally Well Qualified" for the 2026 election [S6].

## Positions

Judicial philosophy and approach (as described by the candidate):

- Says he has "fought for the rule of law, the rights of all individuals," and for courts that are "fair, independent, and accessible" [S1].
- Campaign describes his approach as faithfully applying Washington's Constitution and laws, maintaining independence and impartiality through careful legal analysis, and expanding access to justice for underserved populations [S2].
- Told the Spokesman-Review that he reviews all materials submitted in a case and analyzes constitutional and statutory text and structure [S5].
- Emphasizes his history of volunteering to represent people who could not afford lawyers, and mentoring attorneys to expand diversity in the legal profession [S1].

## Record

- Serving on the Washington Supreme Court since April 4, 2026 [S4]. No Supreme Court opinions authored by Angelis were identified in the court's recent-opinions listings as of this research date; his tenure on the bench began roughly three months before the primary [S8].
- As an attorney, sued the federal government over children appearing without lawyers in immigration court; the litigation ended with the federal government agreeing to pay for lawyers ("has done") [S1][S4].
- Recognized by Chambers USA and Best Lawyers in America during his private-practice career [S4].

## Endorsements

- Eight sitting Supreme Court justices, including Chief Justice Debra Stephens and Justices Charles Johnson, Steve González, Sheryl Gordon McCloud, Raquel Montoya-Lewis, Helen Whitener, Sal Mungia, and Colleen Melody, plus retired Justices Barbara Madsen and Gerry Alexander [S3][S5].
- Governor Bob Ferguson and former Governors Jay Inslee and Chris Gregoire; Lieutenant Governor Denny Heck; Insurance Commissioner Patty Kuderer; King County Executive Girmay Zahilay; Spokane Mayor Lisa Brown; former Seattle Mayor Jenny Durkan [S3][S5].
- Washington State Labor Council, Washington Federation of State Employees, American Federation of Teachers–Washington, Planned Parenthood Advocates Washington, and several legislative-district and county Democratic organizations [S3].
- His pamphlet statement claims endorsements from over 500 judges and attorneys statewide and 52 state legislators [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Only sitting justice in this four-way race, but appointed rather than elected, with about three months on the bench and no authored opinions identified as of mid-July 2026 [S4][S8].
- Career profile is appellate and complex litigation at a large firm plus extensive pro bono immigration work, contrasted with opponent Greg Miller's 40-year dedicated appellate practice [S1][S4][S5].
- Holds the broadest endorsement roster in the race, including nearly the entire sitting Supreme Court, current and former governors, and statewide labor organizations [S3][S5].
- Leads the field in fundraising: PDC filings show about $309,000 in contributions across 449 donations as of July 16, 2026, versus roughly $21,900 for Miller [S7].
- Rated "Exceptionally Well Qualified" by Washington Women Lawyers; no rating for him from other bar evaluation groups was located in this research [S6].
