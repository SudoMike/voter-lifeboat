## Background

First-time candidate; Beacon Hill resident and educator [S1][S3]. Associate
Director of Leadership Development at Rainier Scholars (2018–present);
previous UX research work and internships with U.S. Sen. Patty Murray [S1].
Seattle Planning Commissioner since 2020 [S1]. BA in political science,
Colgate University [S1]. Raised by a single mother who came to the U.S. as
a refugee; describes childhood experience of housing insecurity, medical
debt, and gun violence [S1]. Campaign message centers on "a new generation
of leadership" for the district [S3].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Housing affordability for struggling families; affordable
  housing for working families [S1][S2].
- **Childcare/education:** Quality childcare for working parents; fully
  funding early childhood education; early interventions to keep kids
  learning [S1][S2].
- **Healthcare/debt:** Interest limits on medical debt [S1].
- **Public safety:** Community-based safety built on public investment in
  historically neglected areas like South Seattle and Skyway [S2].
- **Economic security:** Safety nets so families can "stop surviving and
  start thriving" [S2][S3].

## Endorsements

Pamphlet lists former Rep. Dawn Mason, Superintendent of Public Instruction
Chris Reykdal, Sage Leaders, College Democrats of Washington, and
candidates Jaelynn Scott and Hannah Sabio-Howell [S1]. Campaign site
highlights King County Executive Girmay Zahilay's endorsement [S2].
