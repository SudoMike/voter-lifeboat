## What this office does

One of two House seats for LD 37 (southeast Seattle, Skyway, part of
Renton) in the 98-member Washington House; votes on state law, budget, and
taxes.

## Race dynamics

Two candidates on the King County primary ballot, both preferring the
Democratic Party; both advance to the general regardless of primary result,
so research depth is light.

- **Sharon Tomiko Santos** — incumbent since 1999, chairs the House
  Education Committee; the first Japanese American woman elected to a
  mainland state legislature.
- **Kelabe Tewolde** — first-time candidate, Rainier Scholars leadership
  director and Seattle Planning Commissioner, running on a
  generational-change message.

## Differentiating axes

- Incumbency and seniority (Santos, ~27 years, committee chair) vs. an
  explicit "generational change" challenge (Tewolde).
- Both are broadly progressive on education, housing, and tax fairness;
  the contrast is experience/insider effectiveness vs. new-leadership
  urgency rather than large policy gaps.
- Tewolde emphasizes lived experience with housing insecurity and medical
  debt and endorsements from newer-generation figures; Santos emphasizes
  her Education Committee chairmanship and long community roots.
