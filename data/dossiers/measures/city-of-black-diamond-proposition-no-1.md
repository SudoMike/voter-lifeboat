## What it does

Proposition No. 1 asks whether the City of Black Diamond should be annexed into the Puget Sound Regional Fire Authority (PSRFA), which would take over fire protection and emergency medical services for Black Diamond residents beginning January 1, 2028 [S1]. The city currently receives fire and EMS service under a contract with Mountain View Fire & Rescue (MVFR) that expires December 31, 2027 [S1]. PSRFA currently serves Kent, Covington, SeaTac, Tukwila, and Maple Valley, plus unincorporated King County Fire District Nos. 37 and 43; if annexed, Black Diamond would have the same number of representatives on PSRFA's governing board as those cities and districts [S1]. Passage requires a simple majority of Black Diamond voters (RCW 52.26.300(4)(a)) [S1].

Cost to households: annexation would add PSRFA's property tax levy of $1.00 per $1,000 of assessed value (about $600/year on a $600,000 home) plus a fire benefit charge, a non-voted charge set by the fire authority based on building size, risk, and response resources; the Courier-Herald reported a combined example of roughly $1,084/year for a 2,790-square-foot home assessed at $754,000 [S3]. The opposition statement cites a similar figure of about $1,083/year for a 2,800-square-foot home [S2].

## Fiscal mechanics

- This is an annexation into an existing regional fire authority, not a city levy: if approved, Black Diamond property owners would begin paying PSRFA's regular levy of $1.00/$1,000 assessed value [S3].
- PSRFA also assesses a fire benefit charge (FBC), which is not voter-approved; it is set by the fire authority based on building size, risk, and resources needed to respond, and cannot exceed 60% of the authority's budget [S3].
- Black Diamond residents currently pay less than $0.50 per $1,000 assessed value for fire service under the MVFR contract, compared to MVFR's district rate of $1.50; MVFR's chief has said the city generates about 10% of departmental revenue while accounting for roughly one-third of expenses [S3].
- The Black Diamond City Council unanimously approved the interlocal annexation agreement on April 21, 2026; if approved, the transition runs through late 2027 with PSRFA service starting January 1, 2028 [S3][S1].
- If annexation is rejected, the city says it will either pursue another annexation vote in a future election or seek to negotiate a new contract with PSRFA or MVFR [S1]. MVFR has indicated willingness to negotiate a new contract; the city has warned of risks including possible direct billing for fire responses and insurance-rating impacts if no arrangement is in place [S3].

## Arguments for

From the statement in favor (submitted per the pamphlet by Jonathan Englund) [S2]:

- A "Yes" vote strengthens fire and EMS service now and as the community grows, citing rising demand for emergency response, wildfire preparedness, rescue services, and advanced medical care [S2].
- Joining PSRFA gives Black Diamond access to a larger regional system with 24/7 staffing, experienced firefighters and paramedics, specialized equipment, and expanded emergency resources [S2].
- Annexation provides a more stable, predictable funding structure than "a smaller standalone system with limited resources" [S2].
- The rebuttal argues the decision "is not just about what's the cheapest solution" but about speed and quality of service through regional coordination [S2].

## Arguments against

From the statement in opposition (submitted per the pamphlet by Michael Carlin and Brian McGee, who describe themselves as a former fire commissioner and career firefighters living in Black Diamond) [S2]:

- The proposition creates a new tax while "the city keeps the money we already pay" for fire/EMS, which opponents characterize as paying twice for services already funded [S2].
- They estimate a 2,800-square-foot home on an average (~$750,000) Black Diamond home price would pay about $1,083/year to start, an amount that "could go up every year based on what the new fire authority decides" [S2].
- Black Diamond already has 24/7 professional firefighters; annexation "will not make response times faster" and will "move resources further away" while substantially increasing costs [S2].
- Higher taxes on local businesses could push up prices or drive businesses out of the city [S2].

## Context

- Black Diamond's council voted March 3, 2026 to begin the PSRFA annexation process and unanimously approved the interlocal agreement April 21, placing the measure on the August 4, 2026 primary ballot [S3].
- The move follows friction over the MVFR contract: MVFR has previously moved to terminate its contract with the city, and its chief has publicly described the current arrangement as under-recovering Black Diamond's share of costs [S3].
- The city's official explanatory statement frames annexation as fiscally sustainable service continuity as the city grows (Black Diamond includes the large Ten Trails master-planned development area) [S1][S3].

## Axis notes

- Cost vs. service model: the measure turns on whether voters accept a significantly higher, permanent fire tax (levy + non-voted benefit charge) in exchange for membership in a large regional fire authority versus continuing lower-cost contracted service [S2][S3].
- Local control vs. regionalization: annexation trades a city-negotiated contract for a seat on a multi-city regional governing board, with future rates partly set by the authority rather than by city contract negotiation [S1][S2].
- Trust in city fiscal handling: the con case hinges on the claim that the city will retain the tax revenue it currently spends on the fire contract, effectively a double payment; the pro case does not address disposition of existing city revenues [S2].
- Risk tolerance: rejecting annexation leaves service after 2027 dependent on renegotiating with MVFR or PSRFA, an outcome with cost and insurance-rating uncertainty [S1][S3].
