## What it does

Proposition No. 1 authorizes Skykomish School District No. 404 to levy excess property taxes for four years, replacing an expiring educational programs and operations (EP&O) levy, to support district programs and operations not funded by the state [S1]. Approved amounts are $164,521 for collection in 2027, $173,405 in 2028, $182,682 in 2029, and $192,237 in 2030, each at an estimated rate of $0.45 per $1,000 of assessed value [S1]. Proceeds go to the district's general fund for general operating expenses including employee costs (such as salaries), instructional materials, special programs, activities and sports, technology systems operation, transportation, facility maintenance, and other non-capital expenses [S1]. Passage requires a simple majority (Wash. Const. art. VII, sec. 2(a)) [S1].

The pamphlet does not state a median-household dollar cost; at the estimated rate, the tax is $0.45 per $1,000 of assessed value per year [S1]. Property tax exemptions may be available to certain homeowners; the explanatory statement directs voters to the King County Assessor [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- Replacement excess levy, not a new tax: it replaces the district's expiring EP&O levy authorized by voters at the August 2, 2022 election, which collected $142,561 to $150,684 per year in 2023-2026 at an estimated rate of $0.61/$1,000 [S1][S3].
- The new estimated rate of $0.45/$1,000 is lower than the expiring levy's estimated $0.61/$1,000, while dollar amounts rise year over year (from $164,521 in 2027 to $192,237 in 2030) [S1][S3]. The exact rate will be adjusted based on actual assessed value and the legal levy-amount limit at collection time [S1].
- Four-year duration, collections 2027-2030; adopted by the school board via Resolution No. 8-2026 [S1].
- As an EP&O levy, funds supplement state basic-education funding for operations; they cannot fund capital construction (non-capital expenses only, per the explanatory statement's list) [S1].

## Arguments for

From the statement in favor (submitted per the pamphlet by Sarah Bender, on behalf of "Skykomish Citizens for Quality Schools") [S2]:

- Voting yes "ensures Skykomish classrooms, and facilities stay safe, warm, and dry"; the statement stresses it "is not a new tax; it maintains the same current assessed value rate" [S2]. (Note: the pamphlet's estimated rate for this levy is $0.45/$1,000 versus an estimated $0.61/$1,000 for the expiring levy [S1][S3].)
- Renewal enables facility updates and installation and maintenance of security components described as important for student and staff safety [S2].
- The levy funds district technology and programs said to equip students with skills for the modern world [S2].
- Investing in maintenance of the district's historic facilities is described as fiscally responsible and foundational to the educational environment [S2].

## Arguments against

No statement against this measure was filed. No one in the jurisdiction contacted King County Elections to serve on the committee to write a statement against the measure [S2].

## Context

- Skykomish is one of King County's smallest school districts; the levy's dollar amounts (roughly $165K-$192K/year) reflect a very small tax base compared with other county school measures [S1].
- Voters approved the predecessor replacement EP&O levy at the August 2, 2022 primary ($142,561-$150,684/year, estimated $0.61/$1,000, collections 2023-2026), which this measure replaces [S3].
- No independent news coverage of the 2026 measure from established outlets was located; evidence beyond the pamphlet is limited to official election records [S1][S2][S3].

## Axis notes

- Tax appetite vs. school service level: the levy funds operations the state does not (sports, activities, transportation, maintenance, technology); rejection would remove roughly $165K-$192K/year from a small district's general fund [S1].
- Continuity vs. relief: as a replacement levy at a lower estimated rate than the expiring one, the practical question is continuing an existing tax burden rather than adding a new one, though total dollars collected rise each year [S1][S3].
- One-sided record: with no con committee and no located news coverage, voters have only the district's and supporters' framing; the main counterweight is general property-tax sensitivity [S2].
