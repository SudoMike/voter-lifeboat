## What this office does

One of two House seats for LD 41 (Eastside: Bellevue, Mercer Island,
Newcastle, parts of Renton/Issaquah/Sammamish); votes on state law, budget,
and taxes.

## Race dynamics

Two candidates on the primary ballot; both advance to the general
regardless of primary result, so research depth is light.

- **My-Linh T. Thai** (Prefers Democratic Party) — incumbent since 2019,
  House Deputy Majority Leader, pharmacist, first refugee elected to the
  Washington Legislature.
- **Michael Rosen** (Prefers Independent Party) — attorney, investor, and
  licensed nutritionist from Mercer Island; first-time candidate running on
  fiscal accountability.

## Differentiating axes

- Incumbent Democrat with leadership position vs. Independent challenger
  critical of state spending growth and recent tax increases.
- Fiscal posture is the clearest divide: Thai emphasizes protecting access
  to care, behavioral health, and education investment; Rosen argues state
  spending has doubled while outcomes worsened and demands fiscal
  discipline.
- Experience contrast: legislative/committee record and school-board
  background (Thai) vs. private-sector legal/investment background (Rosen).
