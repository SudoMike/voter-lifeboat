## What this office does

A Washington State Representative serves a two-year term in the state House,
one of two representatives per legislative district. Representatives write
and vote on state law and the state operating/capital/transportation
budgets, covering issues such as housing, education funding, health care,
taxes, public safety, and transportation. The 46th LD covers northeast
Seattle (including Lake City and the University District area) and nearby
communities.

## Race dynamics

- Only two candidates filed, so **both advance from the primary to the
  November general election regardless of the primary result**. The primary
  here is effectively a preview of the general.
- Incumbent Darya Farivar (Prefers Democratic Party) faces first-time
  candidate Rodney 'Star' Thornley (Prefers Independent Party) in a district
  that routinely elects Democrats.

## Issue axes that actually differentiate these candidates

1. **Experience and record vs. outsider status:** Farivar is a sitting
   legislator with passed bills (grocery/pharmacy covenant ban, caregiver
   workforce expansion) and committee leadership; Thornley has no elected
   experience and runs on being new to politics.
2. **Party alignment vs. independence:** Farivar runs within the Democratic
   coalition with labor, party, and elected-official endorsements; Thornley's
   core message is rejecting party-line politics and prioritizing direct
   constituent responsiveness.
3. **Policy specificity:** Farivar's platform centers on disability equity,
   food/health access, and civil-rights work; Thornley offers a short list of
   distinct proposals (state citizenship for undocumented residents,
   three-year college path, fiscal accountability) without detailed record or
   institutional backing.
4. Conventional left-right policy contrast is limited — both express
   pro-immigrant and constituent-service themes; the sharper axes are
   record/experience and party vs. independent framing.
