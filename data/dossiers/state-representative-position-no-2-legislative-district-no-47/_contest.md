## The office

A State Representative is one of two lower-chamber members elected from each legislative district to two-year terms in the Washington State House. Representatives write and vote on state laws and the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets, covering issues such as taxes, housing policy, K-12 education funding, public safety, healthcare, and environmental regulation. The 47th Legislative District covers the Kent, Auburn, Covington, and Maple Valley area of south King County.

## Race dynamics

Only two candidates filed, so both advance from the primary to the November general election regardless of primary results. This is a rematch: Republican Ted Cooke challenged and lost to Democratic incumbent Chris Stearns in the 2024 general election for this same seat. Stearns is the incumbent with House leadership roles (Speaker Pro Tempore) and a broad institutional endorsement slate; Cooke is a party-organization activist and software professional without prior elected public office.

## Issue axes that differentiate these candidates

- **Taxes and state spending:** Cooke would freeze new taxes, eliminate income/property/fuel taxes, and audit and cut state programs; Stearns runs on delivered state investments in affordable housing, behavioral health, and childcare.
- **Housing approach:** Cooke opposes state-driven density and prioritizes single-family "starter homes with yards"; Stearns emphasizes expanding affordable housing, including through state programs.
- **Social policy:** Cooke would remove DEI from government hiring, restore "full parental rights in education," and "uphold the biological definition of gender"; Stearns describes reproductive and gender-affirming care as human rights and chairs the House Members of Color Caucus.
- **Experience axis:** Legislative incumbency and a bill-signing record (clean energy, tribal apprenticeships, problem gambling, data privacy) versus an outsider running against what he characterizes as government overreach.
- **Less differentiating:** Both candidates say they prioritize public safety and better schools, though they frame the problems and remedies very differently.
