## Background

Chris Stearns is the incumbent State Representative for the 47th Legislative District (Kent, Auburn, Covington, Maple Valley area), running for re-election to Position 2 [S1][S2]. He serves as Speaker Pro Tempore of the House and as Vice Chair of the State Government & Tribal Relations Committee, with additional service on the Capital Budget, Environment & Energy, and Rules committees [S1]; his campaign site also lists him as Vice-Chair of the Regulated Substances & Gaming Committee and Co-Chair of the Legislative Nuclear Energy Caucus [S2]. He is Navajo and an attorney (Cornell Law School JD, Williams College BA) with roughly 30 years of experience working on Native American rights at the state and federal level [S1][S2]. He previously served on the Auburn City Council, was appointed by President Clinton as the U.S. Energy Department's first Indian Affairs Director, and formerly chaired the Washington State Gambling Commission [S1][S2]. His community service includes the King County Affordable Housing Commission, the King County Mental Illness and Drug Dependency Committee, and the WA State Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women Task Force [S1].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Cites expanding affordable housing as a delivered result and continued priority [S1][S2].
- **Healthcare / behavioral health:** Supports expanding healthcare access and affordability; describes reproductive and gender-affirming care as human rights; prioritizes behavioral health investment [S1][S2].
- **Labor / economy:** Cites strengthening worker protections; supports small business and "an economy where every family has the opportunity to succeed" [S1][S2].
- **Clean energy:** Supports clean energy job expansion [S1][S2].
- **Tribal sovereignty:** Prioritizes advancing Native American rights and resources [S2].
- **Schools and public safety:** Says he will continue fighting for "strong schools, safer communities" [S1].
- **Families:** Supports investments in childcare and nutrition [S2].

## Record

- Sponsored HB 1461 (clean energy infrastructure), signed into law by Governor Ferguson [S3].
- Sponsored a Native American apprenticeship bill signed into law under Governor Inslee, supporting apprenticeship opportunities for members of federally recognized tribes [S3].
- Sponsored HB 1681, which doubled funds available for problem-gambling help; it passed the House 80-16 [S3].
- Sponsored HB 1221 on data privacy and security, which passed the Senate [S3].
- Defeated Ted Cooke, his current opponent, in the November 2024 general election for this same seat [S4].

## Endorsements

Per his pamphlet statement: Auburn Mayor Backus, Kent Mayor Ralph, Covington Mayor Smith, Congressmember Adam Smith, Governor Bob Ferguson, 47th LD Democrats, WA State Nurses Association, Aerospace Machinists 751, WA Conservation Action, and the WA State Labor Council [S1]. His campaign site additionally lists UFCW 3000, the Tulalip Tribes, State Senator Claudia Kauffman, and Representatives Debra Entenman, Janice Zahn, and Edwin Obras [S2].

## Scoring notes

- Incumbent with House leadership roles (Speaker Pro Tempore) and a record of bills signed into law; his opponent has not held legislative office [S1][S3][S4].
- Emphasizes affordable housing, behavioral health, worker protections, and tribal/civil rights, in contrast with his opponent's tax-cut and government-audit platform [S1][S2].
- This is a rematch of the 2024 general election between the same two candidates [S4].
- Carries a broad slate of institutional endorsements (labor, Democratic officials, environmental groups) [S1][S2].
