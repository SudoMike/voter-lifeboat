## What this office does

State representatives serve 2-year terms in the Washington House, voting on
state budgets, taxes, and all state law (housing, education, public safety,
healthcare, climate).

## Race dynamics

- Depth is light: two candidates, both advance from the top-two primary to the
  general election regardless of the primary result.
- Incumbent Osman Salahuddin (D) was appointed in January 2025 to fill the
  House seat Vandana Slatter left when she moved to the Senate, and is running
  to retain it. Challenger Jeffery Poppe (R) is a retired CPA making a
  fiscal-accountability case; LD 48 is heavily Democratic.

## Differentiating axes

- Party and overall direction: Democratic incumbent with broad establishment
  endorsements vs. Republican challenger.
- Taxes and spending: Poppe pledges no new taxes and fraud audits; Salahuddin
  campaigns on investments in childcare, healthcare, housing, and education.
- Campaign finance: Poppe pledges individual contributions only (no PAC/union
  money); Salahuddin has conventional labor/party support.
