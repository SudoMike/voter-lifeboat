## Background

Incumbent State Representative for the 48th LD (prefers Democratic Party) and
former Redmond City Councilmember [S1]. Appointed to the House on January 7,
2025 to replace Vandana Slatter after her move to the Senate [S3]. Redmond
native and son of immigrants; BS in Neurobiology from the University of
Washington, where he was student body president [S1][S2]. Prior work includes
cancer research at Fred Hutchinson, medical device consulting, and community
engagement for King County; board service includes Hopelink and Transportation
Choices Coalition [S1][S2].

## Positions

- Priorities listed: housing and transit infrastructure, education and
  workforce development, healthcare and behavioral health access, public
  safety and gun violence prevention, climate action, and small business and
  worker advocacy [S2].
- Pamphlet emphasis: public safety, healthcare access, strong schools,
  "common-sense solutions and economic growth" [S1].

## Record

- Says he secured childcare and healthcare investments for the district and
  "passed more laws than any legislator this year," citing bills on affordable
  housing, gun violence prevention, and immigrant protections (candidate's own
  characterization) [S1].

## Endorsements

Congresswoman DelBene; Governor Ferguson; Treasurer Pellicciotti; King County
Councilmember Balducci; mayors of Redmond, Bellevue, Kirkland, and Medina;
teachers and nurses unions; WA State Labor Council; 48th District Democrats;
Washington Conservation Action; Planned Parenthood Alliance (per candidate's
own list) [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
- Appointed incumbent with a short (about 1.5-year) legislative record and
  broad Democratic-establishment and labor endorsements.
- Contrast with opponent is primarily on taxes/spending and party direction.
