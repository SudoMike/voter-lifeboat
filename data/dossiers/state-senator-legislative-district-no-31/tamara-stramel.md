## Background
Tamara Stramel (D) is a first-time candidate for elected office; she has been a Democratic precinct committee officer since 2017 and has served eight years on the board of the 31st LD Democrats [S1]. She was a field organizer for the 2024 Sumner-Bonney Lake school bond (which she describes as the largest in state history) and previously worked as a program manager in residential treatment facilities [S1]. She holds a BA in Psychology from California State University, Bakersfield [S1]. She describes herself as a mom, organizer, and veteran spouse who has lived near Lake Tapps for eight years [S1]. Her campaign website is active with Meet/Priorities/Endorsements sections [S2].

## Positions
- **Education:** says schools are "asked to do more with less" and pledges to champion education funding [S1].
- **Infrastructure/transportation:** will advocate for the district to be prioritized in the state transportation budget and for rural infrastructure development [S1].
- **Affordability/growth:** cites families being priced out and infrastructure straining under rapid growth [S1].
- **Governance style:** pledges to represent "the whole 31st district" and to lead with honesty and compassion [S1].

## Endorsements
- Washington State Labor Council, AFL-CIO; Pierce County Democrats; and "trusted local leaders," per her pamphlet statement [S1].

## Scoring notes
- The only Democrat in the race; party is the clearest differentiator against a four-term-plus Republican legislator [S1].
- Grassroots/PTA/school-bond organizing background but no elected or legislative record [S1].
- Emphasizes education and transportation-budget advocacy where the incumbent emphasizes tax restraint [S1].
- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
