## Background
Jeff Lyon is a Libertarian candidate from Woodinville who has not held elected office; he ran for Woodinville City Council in 2025 and describes himself as "proudly unaffiliated with the political establishment" [S1][S2]. He is a disabled U.S. Navy veteran (First Class Petty Officer, Navy police), served as a Civil Affairs leader in Afghanistan (2009), worked as a Deputy Sheriff in Chesapeake, Virginia, and is a cybersecurity engineering manager — identified in primary-filing coverage as head of infrastructure security at Coinbase [S1][S2][S4]. He holds a bachelor's degree from the College of William and Mary (2005) and a master's from American Military University (2016) [S1]. He says he led a 2025 campaign that raised Woodinville voter turnout to an eight-year high [S1].

## Positions
- **Taxes:** Proposes eliminating state taxes for most families earning under $125,000; says any new income tax must get direct voter approval, completely eliminate regressive taxes, and guarantee full school funding [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Supports a constitutional amendment guaranteeing permanent K-12 funding; prioritizes career pathways in AI, robotics, healthcare, software, aerospace, and engineering [S1][S2].
- **Housing / local control:** Would eliminate regulations he says drive up housing costs; opposes top-down state zoning mandates and supports reforming the Growth Management Act to increase supply [S1][S2].
- **Direct democracy:** Supports binding citizen initiatives that cannot be overturned by legal challenge or bureaucratic delay [S2].
- **Public safety:** Supports "balanced reform" — decriminalizing victimless conduct while holding offenders accountable, citing his deputy-sheriff experience [S2].
- **Energy / environment:** Supports advanced nuclear reactors and "cleanest, most efficient" power; would require closed-loop cooling for data centers to reduce freshwater use [S2].
- **Transportation:** Argues communities should not be taxed for projects offering them no direct benefit [S2].

## Record
- No prior elected office [S1].
- 2026-cycle fundraising: $59.39 in one reported contribution (PDC data as of 2026-07-16), indicating an essentially self-organized campaign [S3].

## Endorsements
None listed on the pamphlet statement or campaign website [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- The only third-party (Libertarian) candidate in the race; platform centers on tax elimination for sub-$125k households and voter-approval conditions for any income tax [S1][S2].
- Combines a military/law-enforcement background with a senior tech-security career — the only candidate pairing cybersecurity industry experience against the incumbent's tech-policy legislative focus [S2][S4].
- Anti-establishment framing and near-zero fundraising distinguish the campaign's scale from the two Democrats' [S1][S3].
- Aligns with Republican Moon on local control and lower taxes, but diverges on decriminalization of victimless offenses and direct-democracy mechanisms [S2].
