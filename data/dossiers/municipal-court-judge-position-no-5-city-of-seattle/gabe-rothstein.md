## Background

Gabe (Gabriel) Rothstein has been a public defender since 2005 — his current
full-time work — and serves as a Judge Pro Tem in municipal and district
courts around Washington [S1]. His defense roles have included misdemeanor
and felony defense, drug-treatment-court attorney, misdemeanor supervisor,
juvenile-court unit lead, and civil-commitment unit lead; he has tried more
than 70 cases to verdict [S1][S2]. Before becoming a public defender he
volunteered with the Housing Justice Project and the Unemployment Law
Project on evictions and unemployment benefits [S1]. He chairs the
Washington State Bar Association's Character and Fitness Board, serves on
the board of the Holman Recovery Center (an inpatient substance-abuse
treatment facility), and takes evening/weekend calls on the statewide Youth
Access to Counsel Line [S1][S2]. He holds a J.D. from UCLA School of Law and
a B.A. from Emory University [S1].

## Positions

- Says every person in the courtroom — victims, defendants, and those who
  cannot afford an attorney — deserves dignity, respect, fairness, and to be
  heard; calls Seattle Municipal Court "the people's court" [S1].
- States release decisions should be based on flight risk and public safety
  rather than ability to afford bail [S2].
- Prioritizes treatment over punishment in substance-abuse and
  mental-health cases, while balancing victim safety with accountability;
  says he believes in "treatment, accountability, recovery, redemption, and
  second chances" [S1][S2].

## Record

- Judge pro tempore since 2024 in Seattle, Burien, Redmond, Kent, and
  Shoreline courts and Everett Municipal Court, presiding over small-claims
  trials, vehicle-impound hearings, and criminal and jail calendars [S2].
- Two decades representing thousands of indigent clients, many of them both
  survivors of abuse and defendants in the criminal legal system [S1].
- Bar ratings per his site: "Exceptionally Well Qualified" from QLaw and
  from the Latino Bar Association (the latter rated for Superior Court);
  Cardozo Society and Joint Asian Judicial Evaluation Committee ratings
  listed as pending. Self-reported; not independently verified [S2].

## Endorsements

- King County Democrats (independently confirmed on the organization's 2026
  endorsement list) [S3].
- Per his pamphlet statement: 32nd and 46th District Democrats, SEIU 925
  Public Defense Chapter, Seattle Municipal Court Judges Vaddadi and Shadid,
  Court of Appeals Judges Smith, Coburn, and Bowman, King County Superior
  Court Judges Sutton, Robertson, and McKee, and dozens of other judges and
  attorneys [S1]. His site adds Supreme Court Justice Sheryl Gordon McCloud
  and County Executive Girmay Zahilay [S2].

## Campaign finance

## Scoring notes

- Twenty-year public-defense career contrasts directly with opponent Garmon
  Newsom's prosecution and municipal-attorney background; that
  defense-vs-prosecution split is the race's central difference [S1][S2].
- Backed by the King County Democrats and public-defense labor (SEIU 925
  chapter), while Newsom holds the Seattle Times and retiring incumbent's
  endorsements — a classic split of endorsement blocs [S1][S3]. (Nonpartisan
  office; party endorsement signals organizational support, not judicial
  philosophy.)
- Most explicit candidate in the race on bail policy (release based on risk
  rather than affordability) [S2].
- Judicial experience is recent (pro tem since 2024) but spread across six
  courts, including the misdemeanor/jail calendars this court runs [S2].
