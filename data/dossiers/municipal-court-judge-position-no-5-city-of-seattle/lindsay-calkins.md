## Note: candidate has withdrawn from the race

As of research date (2026-07-16), Lindsay Calkins's campaign website carries
an announcement that she is withdrawing from the race for Seattle Municipal
Court Judge, citing personal reasons and a decision to focus on family:
"our children are at an important stage in their lives" [S2]. Her statement
still appears in the printed voters' pamphlet [S1], so her name may still
appear on the primary ballot; voters should be aware she is no longer
actively seeking the seat [S2].

## Background

Lindsay Calkins is a trial and appellate attorney with nearly 15 years of
litigation experience, focused on criminal appeals and resentencing work
[S1]. Before law school she worked as an immigration paralegal; during law
school (University of Chicago, Law Review) she worked in the Civil Rights
and Police Accountability Clinic [S1]. She graduated with honors from
Princeton University [S1]. She has lived on Capitol Hill for over 15 years,
where she and her husband are raising three children, and volunteers with
the King County Bar Association, the First Responder Will Clinic, the Junior
League of Seattle, and her faith community [S1].

## Positions

- Said courts should be "fair, thoughtful, and grounded in the real lives"
  of the people who come before them, with careful consideration and equal
  treatment for every person [S1].
- Emphasized access to justice: municipal court is many people's first and
  only interaction with the legal system, and should prioritize language
  access and comprehensibility [S1].
