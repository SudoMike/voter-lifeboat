## Background

- Incumbent State Representative, 11th Legislative District Position 1, serving since 2021 [S1]; first elected in 2020 [S7]. The district covers Tukwila, Renton, and parts of Kent [S7].
- Attorney: former Assistant United States Attorney, former UN war crimes prosecutor, former Senior Counsel for Global Human Resources at The Nature Conservancy, and former consultant for the Seattle City Attorney's Office [S1].
- Education: BS, Cornell University; MPA, Harvard University; JD, Harvard Law School [S1].
- Community service history includes board roles with Tukwila Children's Foundation, Alliance for Gun Responsibility, Tabor 100, Habitat for Humanity Seattle-King & Kittitas Counties, and service as a Washington State Human Rights Commissioner [S1].
- Chair of the Washington State Legislative Black Caucus (since January 2023) [S4].

## Positions

- **Cost of living (stated top priority):** says his "singular focus is finding direct ways to lower costs for everyday people, from housing to healthcare" [S1]; campaign frames this as "bold, state-led solutions that drive down the cost of living" given a "gridlocked federal government" [S1][S2].
- **2026 session priorities (stated):** improving state government performance, protecting taxpayers, and strengthening public safety [S4].
- **Environment/energy:** has prioritized clean energy initiatives [S4]; the Seattle Times endorsement notes he has made environmental justice and air quality for his constituents a top priority [S8].
- **Consumer protection and refugee support** appear among his recurring legislative themes [S4].

## Record

- Vice Chair, House Consumer Protection & Business Committee; member, Environment & Energy, Transportation, and the Legislative Ethics Board [S3].
- Prime sponsor of HB 1149 (2025), "Preventing cruelty to animals," targeting organized animal fighting; passed the House 96-0 and Senate 47-1, signed May 12, 2025 (Chapter 220, Laws of 2025) [S5].
- Campaign finance: $142,259 in itemized contributions across 75 records reported to the PDC for the 2026 cycle as of 2026-07-16 — the most of any candidate in this race [S6].

## Endorsements

- Seattle Times editorial board (2026 primary): recommends Hackney, stating he "ably represents his constituents" [S8].
- King County Democrats: endorsed (dual endorsement alongside Ashley Fedan in this race) [S9].
- Washington State Labor Council: endorsed Hackney; WSLC delegates passed over challenger Ashley Fedan [S10].
- Per his pamphlet statement: Governor Bob Ferguson, Attorney General Nick Brown, the 11th District Democrats, and the State Labor Council [S1].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate in this race with a legislative record: five years in the seat, a committee vice-chairmanship, caucus leadership (Black Caucus chair), and enacted legislation [S3][S4][S5].
- Faces an intra-party challenge from Democrat Ashley Fedan; institutional endorsers (Seattle Times, WSLC, Governor, AG) have largely stayed with him, while King County Democrats dual-endorsed [S8][S9][S10].
- Emphasizes prosecutorial/legal background and cost-of-living economics rather than a healthcare-practitioner lens (his challenger's frame) [S1][S2].
- Substantial fundraising lead over both opponents in this race [S6].
