## What this office does

A Washington State Representative is one of two House members (Position 1 and Position 2) elected from each legislative district to two-year terms in the state House of Representatives. Representatives write and vote on state law, adopt the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets, and set statewide policy on issues like housing, K-12 education funding, healthcare programs, taxes, public safety, and transportation. This is a partisan office decided by a top-two primary (August 4, 2026): the two highest vote-getters advance to the November general election regardless of party.

## Race dynamics

- District 11 covers Tukwila, Renton, and parts of Kent [S1] — a Democratic-leaning South King County district.
- Three candidates: two-term-plus Democratic incumbent David Hackney (attorney, first elected 2020), Democratic challenger Ashley Fedan (nurse anesthetist, Navy veteran, and party activist), and Republican Christian Rombough (Army veteran and union ironworker) [S1][S3].
- The primary contest of note is intra-party: local media flagged this as a race to watch because Hackney drew a challenger from within his own party [S1]. Institutional endorsers split only partially — King County Democrats dual-endorsed both Democrats, while the Seattle Times and the Washington State Labor Council backed the incumbent (see candidate dossiers).
- Fundraising is lopsided: Hackney ~$142k, Fedan ~$57k (with more individual contribution records), Rombough $0 on file with the PDC as of 2026-07-16 [S2].
- With three candidates in a top-two primary, both Democrats and the Republican are competing for two general-election slots; a Democrat-vs-Democrat general is plausible given district lean and campaign activity.

## Differentiating issue axes

1. **Record vs. fresh perspective (the real axis):** Hackney runs on five years of legislative record (committee vice-chair, Black Caucus chair, enacted bills); Fedan runs on frontline healthcare expertise and the absence of nurses in the legislature. Their stated policy goals (affordability, healthcare access, housing) overlap heavily, so voters largely choose between profiles, not platforms [S1][S3].
2. **Healthcare emphasis:** Fedan makes healthcare access and reproductive care provision her core identity; Hackney frames healthcare as one component of a broader cost-of-living agenda [S3].
3. **Party/ideology:** Rombough offers the only cross-party contrast — anti-waste, local control, public safety — though he notably breaks with GOP norms by pledging to defend trade unions [S3].
4. **Campaign viability/infrastructure:** endorsement depth and fundraising differ sharply among all three and may matter to voters weighing effectiveness [S2].
