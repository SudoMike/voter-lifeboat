## Background

Scott Edwards, 60, is a tax and constitutional litigator in private practice: at Lane Powell (which combined with Ballard Spahr) since 2010, where he chaired the practice group 2010-2024 and served on the firm board; previously at Perkins Coie (1999-2010) and Bogle & Gates (1996-1999) [S1][S3]. He has been an adjunct professor at the University of Washington School of Law since 2002 [S1]. He grew up in Anchorage, Alaska, where his father served as Alaska attorney general and U.S. Attorney; he holds an economics degree magna cum laude from Princeton and a UW law degree with honors [S1][S2][S3]. He has never held judicial office [S1].

## Positions

- Says the Supreme Court's role is "to apply the constitution and laws as written, not rewrite them," and campaigns on taking "politics out of the courtroom" [S1].
- Core stated principles: independence from political pressure, rule of law through "clear, principled reasoning," and due process with equal treatment [S2].
- Criticizes the current court as "one of the most progressive courts in the country" and argues recent appointments have created judicial imbalance that lets "policy outcomes" influence decisions [S3].
- Emphasizes that justices should apply the law "predictably, as written, and without favoring any political agenda" [S1].

## Record

- More than 30 years representing clients at all levels of Washington's court system, including more than a dozen Washington Supreme Court cases, largely in tax and constitutional matters [S1].
- Professional recognitions (self-reported): Chambers USA ranking in Tax (2020-2025), Best Lawyers in America (Tax Law 2012-2026; Litigation & Controversy - Tax 2024-2026), Super Lawyers in Tax (2012-2024) [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

- Gil Brewer, former senior assistant director of tax policy at the Washington Department of Revenue [S3].
- Fundraising: about $53,000 raised as of early July 2026 [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Only Position 1 candidate whose career is entirely in private appellate/tax litigation; he has no judicial or quasi-judicial service, unlike both opponents [S1][S3].
- Substantial Washington Supreme Court advocacy experience (a dozen-plus cases before the court) and two decades teaching at UW Law [S1].
- The most explicit critic of the current court's ideological direction in this race; his practice includes tax-law issues of the kind the court is expected to face [S3].
- Fundraising is a quarter of the incumbent's but roughly double the other challenger's [S3].
