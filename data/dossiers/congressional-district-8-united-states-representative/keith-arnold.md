## Background

- Democratic-preference candidate; no prior elected office [S1].
- Perennial candidate for this seat: he reports running for U.S. Representative in the 8th District in every cycle from 2006 through 2024 [S3]; in the 2024 top-two primary he was eliminated, with Kim Schrier and Republican Carmen Goers advancing [S5].
- Career federal employee: worked at the IRS in St. Louis (1999–2004), then the Washington State Department of Licensing, and since 2010 as an accounting technician at NOAA [S3].
- Describes himself in the pamphlet as a "former (involuntarily) federal employee, despite statutes": he says he was terminated from his federal job for running for Congress, and he disputes the termination, arguing Washington's top-two primary is a nonpartisan election under the federal statute limiting political activity by federal employees, and that the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit misinterpreted the law [S1][S2].
- B.S. in Accountancy, University of Illinois [S1][S3].

## Positions

- **Ranked-choice voting:** His central issue; he calls it "the best democracy defense" and devotes most of his pamphlet statement to explaining how it prevents vote-splitting [S1][S2].
- **Judiciary:** Argues that courts overturning legislative or executive actions ("legislating from the bench") violates the Constitution's ex post facto clause and should require legislative panel approval [S1].
- **Economic policy:** Supports a living wage "for the law-abiding," a progressive tax system on higher incomes, universal healthcare, and fair free global trade [S1].
- **Energy/environment:** Supports clean, green, renewable energy sources [S1].
- **Immigration:** Supports enforcement focused on abusive employers [S1].
- **Foreign policy:** Opposed starting the Iraq and Afghanistan wars and the bank bailout; supports "UN-focused world unity" and supporting troops' economic futures [S1].
- **Abortion:** Describes himself as "ProLife" with exceptions for rape, incest, and significant health risk [S1].

## Scoring notes

- The only other Democratic-preference candidate besides the incumbent, but with no fundraising found in FEC records for the 2026 cycle and a decade-long history of primary eliminations in this district [S4][S5].
- His platform is idiosyncratic rather than party-line: progressive economics and universal healthcare combined with a pro-life stance and unconventional judiciary theories [S1].
- Campaign materials focus heavily on his personal legal dispute over his federal employment and on ranked-choice voting rather than a broad governing agenda [S1][S2].
