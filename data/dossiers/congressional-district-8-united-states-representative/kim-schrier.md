## Background

- Incumbent U.S. Representative for Washington's 8th Congressional District, serving since 2019; seeking a fifth term in 2026 [S3][S5].
- Pediatrician in Issaquah for 17 years before Congress; pediatric residency at Stanford (2000), M.D. from UC Davis (1997), B.A. in astrophysics from UC Berkeley (1991) [S1].
- Serves on the House Committee on Energy & Commerce, with subcommittee assignments on Health and on Energy; previously on Commerce, Manufacturing, and Trade [S1].
- Community involvement listed: Green River habitat restoration, Mountains to Sound Greenway, volunteering at the King County Clinic [S1].

## Positions

- **Cost of living:** Introduced legislation to suspend the federal gas tax through the end of the year; frames lowering household costs as her central priority [S1].
- **Healthcare:** Says she has passed measures to cap insulin costs, give Medicare authority to negotiate prescription prices, and cap out-of-pocket costs for seniors; wants to "crack down on insurance company abuses" and criticizes the current Republican majority for healthcare cuts paired with tax cuts [S1]. Campaign site lists healthcare and women's health as core issue areas [S2].
- **Agriculture:** Positions herself as an advocate for 8th District family farms — reversing India's apple tariffs, opposing the Albertsons–Kroger merger, and pursuing foreign market access in the Farm Bill [S1].
- **Housing:** Says she has introduced affordable-housing legislation [S1]; sponsored the Permanent Housing Affordability Act (H.R. 8127, 119th Congress) [S3].
- **Energy/climate:** Supports spurring clean-energy development [S1]; campaign lists environment as a priority [S2].
- **Governing style:** Presents herself as an independent, bipartisan legislator who works with both parties; cites holding over 130 town halls [S1][S2].

## Record

- Sponsored 104 bills since taking office in 2019, including 22 in the current (119th) Congress — among them the SCREEN for Type 1 Diabetes Act, the Permanent Housing Affordability Act, the National Prescribed Fire Act, the Improving Child Care for Working Families Act, and the Kids' Access to Primary Care Act [S3].
- Her campaign states she has "passed 18 bills into law, signed by both Democratic and Republican presidents" [S5]; congress.gov does not record any of her sponsored bills as enacted as standalone public laws, so this count evidently reflects provisions enacted through larger legislative packages [S3].
- Says she secured over $60 million in Community Project (earmark) funding across the district, including a school-based health clinic in Graham, veterans' housing in Ellensburg, Highway 2 improvements, food bank expansions, wildfire resiliency in North Central Washington, and body cameras plus a rapid DNA system for King County law enforcement [S1].
- Campaign finance: raised about $2.45 million this cycle and held about $3.29 million cash on hand as of March 31, 2026 — far more than any challenger in the race [S4].

## Endorsements

- Planned Parenthood Action Fund, Washington State Labor Council, League of Conservation Voters, and mayors across the district including Mike Poirier (Wenatchee), Rich Elliott (Ellensburg), Dan Rankin (Darrington), and Nancy Backus (Auburn) [S1].
- Campaign site references past editorial endorsements from The Everett Herald and The (Tacoma) News Tribune [S2].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate in the race with a federal legislative record; her four terms and committee work are the incumbency axis every challenger runs against [S3][S5].
- The only Democratic-preference candidate with an active, funded campaign; her fundraising exceeds the combined challenger field by more than an order of magnitude [S4].
- Emphasizes bipartisanship and district-service deliverables (earmarks, town halls) rather than ideological positioning [S1].
- Healthcare is her signature issue, grounded in her career as a pediatrician [S1][S2].
