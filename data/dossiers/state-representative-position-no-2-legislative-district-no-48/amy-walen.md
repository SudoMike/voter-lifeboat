## Background

Incumbent State Representative for the 48th LD since 2019 (prefers Democratic
Party); previously Kirkland Mayor (2014-2018) and Kirkland City Councilmember
(2009-2019) [S1]. Chairs the House Consumer Protection & Business Committee
[S2]. 20+ years as an owner and CFO in family businesses; law degree (LLB) and
BA in political science from the University of Queensland [S1]. In November
2025 she unsuccessfully challenged Sen. Vandana Slatter in the special
election for the LD 48 Senate seat, then filed to retain her House seat [S3].

## Positions

- Self-describes as a "pragmatic governing Democrat who works across the
  aisle," championing public safety, affordability, balanced budgets, and
  fiscal responsibility [S1].
- Affordability: tax relief for low-income families and small businesses,
  housing investment, missing-middle housing [S2].
- Public safety: "balanced approach" investing in community-focused law
  enforcement plus expanded mental health and crisis response [S2].
- Healthcare: protecting reproductive healthcare access [S1][S2].
- Environment: clean energy, low-carbon fuel standards, better transportation
  options [S2].
- Accountability: says she prioritizes combating wasteful spending, citing the
  regional homelessness authority [S2].

## Record

- Says she voted no on "the income tax" (the capital gains tax framing is the
  candidate's own) [S1].
- Says she "stood up to the federal government by banning ICE from wearing
  masks and backfilling money cut from Planned Parenthood" (candidate's own
  characterization) [S1].
- Consumer protection work including ending "zombie debt" collection
  practices; advocated Washington's plastic bag ban while in Kirkland city
  government [S2].

## Endorsements

Bellevue Police Guild; Alliance for Gun Responsibility; former Gov. Gregoire;
Congressman Adam Smith; King County Councilmember Claudia Balducci (per
candidate's own list) [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
- Incumbent with a 7-year House record and committee chairmanship; positions
  in the moderate/business-oriented lane of the Democratic party (no-vote on
  income/capital-gains tax, police guild endorsement, fiscal-responsibility
  framing) [S1][S2].
- Lacks the local Democratic-organization endorsements in this race; those
  went to her challenger [S1].
- Recent intraparty history: lost the 2025 special Senate election to
  Slatter, who now endorses her opponent [S3][S1].
