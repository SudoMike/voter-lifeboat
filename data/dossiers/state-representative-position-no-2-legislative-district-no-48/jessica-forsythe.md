## Background

Redmond City Councilmember since 2020 (prefers Democratic Party); Council
President 2022-23 and Vice President 2024-25; chaired the Alternative Crisis
Response Subcommittee and served on the Tenant Protections Subcommittee,
Growth Management Policy Board, and as Eastrail co-chair [S1]. Union member
and Executive Director of Emerge WA, a nonprofit training women and non-binary
leaders; 20 years as a small business owner; BFA in Visual Communication and
Design from Kent State University [S1][S2]. Filed in November 2025 to
challenge incumbent Rep. Amy Walen [S3].

## Positions

- Climate: co-authored Redmond's Climate Emergency Declaration; campaigns on
  climate and environmental resiliency [S2].
- Housing: affordable housing and renter protections; cites winning tenant
  protections and securing $7.5M for housing and human services on the
  council [S1][S2].
- Education: equitable K-12 funding, citing Washington's national ranking [S2].
- Health: public health, behavioral health, and reproductive rights [S2].
- Economy: small business support; championed pandemic support for small
  businesses [S1][S2].
- Says she will bring an "inclusive and pragmatic approach" and design-thinking
  methodology to Olympia [S1][S2].

## Record

- Municipal record only (no state legislative record): balanced billion-dollar
  municipal budgets, tenant protections, financial transparency improvements,
  Redmond's Environmental Sustainability Action Plan [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

48th District Democrats; King County Democrats; Washington State Labor
Council; Insurance Commissioner Kuderer; Senator Slatter; Planned Parenthood
Alliance Advocates; Redmond Firefighters; 60+ elected officials (per
candidate's own list) [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
- Challenger from the progressive/labor lane against a moderate Democratic
  incumbent; holds the local Democratic-organization and state labor council
  endorsements in this race, plus Sen. Slatter's [S1][S3].
- Record is municipal (Redmond council) rather than legislative; emphasis on
  climate, renter protections, and education funding differentiates her from
  the incumbent's fiscal-moderation framing [S1][S2].
