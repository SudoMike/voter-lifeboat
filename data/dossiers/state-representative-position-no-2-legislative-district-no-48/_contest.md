## What this office does

State representatives serve 2-year terms in the Washington House, voting on
state budgets, taxes, and all state law (housing, education, public safety,
healthcare, climate).

## Race dynamics

- Depth is light: two candidates, both advance from the top-two primary to the
  general election regardless of the primary result.
- Democrat-vs-Democrat intraparty contest in a heavily Democratic Eastside
  district: incumbent Rep. Amy Walen (State Representative since 2019, former
  Kirkland mayor) is challenged by Redmond City Councilmember Jessica
  Forsythe (per NPI's Cascadia Advocate, Dec 2025).
- Backstory: Walen unsuccessfully challenged Sen. Vandana Slatter in the
  November 2025 special election for the LD 48 Senate seat; Slatter now
  appears on Forsythe's endorsement list (per NPI's Cascadia Advocate and the
  candidates' pamphlet statements).

## Differentiating axes

- Moderate/business-oriented vs. progressive lane: Walen self-describes as a
  "pragmatic governing Democrat who works across the aisle," highlights her
  no vote on "the income tax," balanced budgets, and Bellevue Police Guild
  endorsement; Forsythe emphasizes climate action, renter protections,
  education funding equity, and union membership.
- Record type: 7-year state House record and committee chairmanship (Walen)
  vs. municipal record on Redmond City Council (Forsythe).
- Endorsement split within the party: local Democratic organizations and
  labor council back Forsythe; Walen lists Gov. Gregoire, Congressman Smith,
  and public-safety/gun-responsibility groups.
