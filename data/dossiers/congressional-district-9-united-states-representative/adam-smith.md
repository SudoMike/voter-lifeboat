## Background

- Incumbent U.S. Representative for WA-9, serving since January 1997; previously a Washington State Senator, 1991-1996 [S1][S5].
- Former prosecutor for the City of Seattle (1993-1995); J.D. from University of Washington (1990), B.A. from Fordham University (1987) [S1].
- Grew up in SeaTac in a union household; raised his two children in the district [S1].
- Ranking Member (and past Chairman) of the House Armed Services Committee; member of the New Democrat Coalition, Congressional Progressive Caucus, and Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus [S1][S5].

## Positions

- **Housing/affordability:** Campaign priorities include "Lower Your Rent & Build More Housing"; pamphlet statement says "we must bring down the cost of housing and make homeownership achievable again" (says he will) [S1][S2].
- **Healthcare:** Says the healthcare system "needs to be restructured to create a path to a single-payer plan like Medicare for All" (says he will) [S1].
- **Economy/labor:** Supports growing "from the middle out," protecting unions, and expanding job training and workforce development; campaign lists "Build Pathways to High Paying Jobs" and "Put People Before Profits" [S1][S2].
- **Abortion:** Campaign lists "Protect Our Right to Abortion" as a priority [S2].
- **Environment:** Campaign lists "Protect Puget Sound for Future Generations" [S2].
- **Trump administration:** Campaign frames the race around "Stand Up to Trump & for Washington" and "rebuild[ing] our representative democracy" [S2].
- **Israel/defense:** Per KUOW (2024), Smith notes he supports positions AIPAC opposes, including the Iran nuclear deal and UNRWA funding, despite receiving AIPAC donations; he has characterized left-flank challenges as appealing to "the far-left of our constituency" and emphasizes housing, crime, and homelessness as top constituent concerns [S6].

## Record

- Primary sponsor of 7 bills enacted into law, including the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021 (H.R. 6395, 116th) and the Promoting Affordable Housing Near Transit Act (H.R. 3680, 117th) [S5].
- Recently introduced bills include the Behavioral Health Crisis Care Centers Act of 2025 (H.R. 5859), the Pre-Apprenticeship Wrap-around Support Services Fund Act of 2025 (H.R. 5806), the Career and Technical Education Access Act (H.R. 5531), and the CHARGE Act of 2025 (H.R. 6281); his sponsored bills concentrate in health (29%), transportation/public works (17%), and labor/employment (14%) [S5].
- Voted Yea on the FY2023 National Defense Authorization Act (H.R. 7900, 2022) and Yea on the rule providing for consideration of 2024 emergency supplemental appropriations (H.Res. 1160) [S5].
- Missed 6.4% of roll-call votes from 1997 to June 2026, versus a 2.1% median among currently serving representatives [S5].
- Won reelection in 2024 with 65.44% of the vote against Melissa Chaudhry [S7].
- Campaign finance (2026 cycle, through 2026-03-31): $1,128,650 in receipts, including $707,737 from individuals and $420,250 from PACs/other committees; $852,275 spent; $847,128 cash on hand [S4]. Per KUOW, in the 2024 cycle his donors included AIPAC and defense contractors [S6].

## Endorsements

- Gov. Bob Ferguson, former Govs. Christine Gregoire and Gary Locke, Attorney General Nick Brown, State Treasurer Mike Pellicciotti, Insurance Commissioner Patty Kuderer [S3].
- Over 20 state legislators including Senate Majority Leader Jamie Pedersen; mayors of Auburn, Bellevue, Federal Way, Issaquah, Renton, SeaTac, and Newcastle; former Seattle Mayor Bruce Harrell; King County Council Chair Sarah Perry [S3].
- Labor unions including the International Association of Machinists, construction trades councils, and public-sector unions; Sierra Club; Planned Parenthood Action Fund; Moms Demand Action Gun Sense Candidate designation [S3].
- The Stranger endorsed the other King County incumbents (Jayapal, DelBene, Schrier) in July 2026 but endorsed Kshama Sawant, not Smith, in WA-9 [S7].
- Smith himself appears as an endorser of Democratic candidates elsewhere in the pamphlet (Jesse Salomon, Mia Gregerson) [S8].

## Scoring notes

- 29-year incumbent and top Democrat on House Armed Services; the only candidate in the race with a federal legislative record.
- Says he supports a "path to" single-payer healthcare, contrasted with challengers who demand immediate universal programs funded by new taxes on the wealthy.
- Accepts PAC and AIPAC money ($420K in committee contributions this cycle), which both major challengers explicitly refuse and campaign against.
- Backed by the state Democratic establishment (governor, legislators, mayors, major unions); challengers' support comes from left-of-party organizations and media.
- Defense posture is the sharpest dividing line: he has voted for NDAAs he helped write, while Sawant and Chaudhry center ending military aid to Israel and cutting war spending.
