## Background

- 2024 Democratic candidate for this same seat: advanced from the primary with roughly 20% of the vote and received 90,000+ votes in the general, losing to Adam Smith, who won 65.44% [S1][S6][S7]. She describes herself as the first Muslim candidate in Washington state history to compete in a federal general election [S3][S6].
- Nonprofit grants and research professional: produced weekly human-geography research briefs for a U.S. State Department working group distributed to 4,000+ experts and ambassadors; grants strategist supporting a $3.2M congressional award for a 27-unit permanently affordable housing project in King County [S1][S3].
- Has worked as a USPS mail carrier and in residential behavioral health, both as a union member; direct-service work includes hygiene access for unhoused and refugee families [S3].
- Her husband, Muhammad Zahid Chaudhry, a disabled U.S. Army veteran and legal permanent resident, was detained by ICE for 124 days beginning August 2025 at his citizenship interview; she represented the case pro se across four federal jurisdictions and secured his release, with the district judge calling the detention "flat out wrong" (campaign account) [S1][S3].
- B.A. in public policy (history, economics, sustainable development); graduate study in systems science [S1].

## Positions

Her platform is organized under "Care, Courage, the Constitution" [S1][S2]. All items below are campaign promises ("says she will") unless noted.

- **Government accountability:** Congressional stock-trading ban; overturn Citizens United; term limits (14 years House, 18 Senate); Supreme Court reform (18-year terms, binding ethics rules); protect whistleblowers [S2].
- **Immigration/civil liberties:** Abolish ICE and restructure DHS; close detention centers; end for-profit prisons; path to citizenship for Dreamers; defend birthright citizenship [S2].
- **Healthcare:** Universal healthcare, "guaranteed, comprehensive, as a matter of basic social infrastructure" [S2].
- **Economy:** 35% tax on wealth above $1 billion; close the carried-interest loophole; $25/hr national minimum wage (with public subsidy for small businesses); public jobs guarantee; UBI pilot programs; worker ownership (co-ops, ESOPs); antitrust enforcement; public banking [S2].
- **Foreign policy/defense:** "End U.S. complicity in genocide. Stop funding Israel"; restore war powers to Congress; audit the Pentagon; ban weapons-manufacturer donations to politicians; reduce overseas military footprint; no AI for military targeting [S2].
- **Climate:** Renewable energy at scale; fully fund FEMA; no data-center approvals without environmental review and public consent; restore USAID [S2].
- **Privacy/AI:** No AI surveillance, facial recognition in public spaces, or predictive policing; data ownership as a Fourth Amendment right; protections against algorithmic discrimination [S2].
- **Democracy reform:** Ranked-choice voting; end gerrymandering via independent commissions [S2].
- **Campaign finance:** Takes no corporate PAC money and rejects AIPAC contributions; points voters to OpenSecrets to verify [S1][S2][S4].

## Record

- Won her federal-court fight over her husband's ICE detention pro se (campaign account; the case is her signature "record" claim; Ninth Circuit case ongoing) [S3].
- 2024 campaign: The Stranger's sole endorsement in both primary and general; the 33rd LD Democrats endorsed her over Smith; the 43rd LD Democrats censured Smith and endorsed her; the 31st LD rescinded its Smith endorsement (campaign account) [S3].
- Campaign finance (2026 cycle, through 2026-03-31): $3,507 in receipts (all individual), $27,926 in disbursements, $117,363 cash on hand [S5]. The Stranger reported roughly $1,500 raised per FEC filings at the time of its endorsement meeting [S7]. In 2024 she raised about $66,000 to Smith's $1.2M+ as of mid-July [S6].

## Endorsements

- 2026: sole endorsement of the 33rd LD Democrats (second consecutive cycle); majority-but-below-60% delegate votes in the 37th and 43rd LD Democrats; Veterans & Military Families Caucus of the Washington State Democrats (second consecutive cycle) (campaign account) [S4].
- The Stranger, which endorsed her in 2024, endorsed Sawant instead in the 2026 primary; its write-up said Chaudhry told the endorsement board she would switch her affiliation to the Green Party if elected and had omitted LGBTQ+ protections from her platform to avoid alienating parts of her coalition. The Washington State Stonewall Democrats chair subsequently urged Democratic organizations to reconsider their endorsements of her [S7].

## Scoring notes

- The only challenger who has run against Smith before (2024, losing 65.44%–34.56% range per reported Smith share), giving her a measurable electoral baseline in this district.
- Runs as a Democrat but, per The Stranger's endorsement report, said she would switch to the Green Party if elected — a disputed-loyalty question unique to her in this race.
- Platform overlaps heavily with Sawant on Gaza, ICE, wealth taxes, and a $25 minimum wage; differs in emphasis on constitutional/accountability reforms (term limits, court reform, RCV) and in party label.
- Distinctive personal record: pro se federal litigation over her husband's ICE detention, which she presents as proof of capability against federal power.
- Minimal 2026 fundraising (about $3.5K in new receipts through March) compared with Sawant (~$609K) and Smith (~$1.13M).
