## Background

- Bakery worker in Seattle, age 49, and member of BCTGM (Bakery, Confectionery, Tobacco Workers and Grain Millers) Local 9; has also worked as a meat cutter and freight railroad conductor [S1].
- Member of the Socialist Workers Party since 2000; began organizing protests against police brutality and for immigrant rights in San Jose, California, about thirty years ago [S1].
- Certified on the August 2026 primary ballot for WA-9 with the party preference "Socialist Workers Party" [S3].
- Serves as an editorial volunteer for The Militant, the SWP's weekly newspaper, which is also his listed campaign website [S2][S1].

## Positions

All items are campaign statements from his pamphlet statement and campaign paper [S1][S2].

- **Foreign policy:** "US Out of the Middle East!"; opposes the U.S. "war against Iran"; "US Hands off Cuba!" — at a March 2026 Seattle rally he called, as the SWP candidate for WA-9, for ending the U.S. blockade of Cuba, quoting a National Nurses United statement [S1][S2].
- **Israel:** Says to "fight rising Jew-hating attacks worldwide and defend Israel's right to exist as a refuge for Jews," while uniting Middle East working people across nationalities — a position distinct from every other candidate in the race [S1].
- **Ukraine/Europe:** "Defend Ukraine's sovereignty! US troops and nuclear weapons out of Europe!" [S1].
- **Economy/labor:** Federally funded public works program at union wages; shorter workweek with no loss in pay; workers' control of production; amnesty for immigrants [S1].
- **Party system:** Argues billionaire families rule through both the Democratic and Republican parties and that workers need "a revolutionary political party" to fight for "workers' power" [S1].
- **Civil liberties:** Opposes "all attacks on constitutional freedom of speech and worship... due process" and "all violence against people for their political views" [S1].

## Record

- No elected-office record; identity as a repeat SWP standard-bearer is consistent with his role as a long-time party activist [S1][S2].
- No FEC campaign committee registered for the 2026 cycle (federal committees are required only above spending thresholds) [S4].

None found in Tier 1/2 sources reviewed.

## Scoring notes

- Runs on the Socialist Workers Party program of building a revolutionary workers' party; unlike Sawant's independent-socialist campaign, his emphasis is party-building and international solidarity (Cuba) rather than specific federal legislation.
- The only left candidate in the race who defends "Israel's right to exist as a refuge for Jews" while opposing U.S. Middle East wars; Sawant and Chaudhry instead center ending U.S. aid to Israel.
- Working-trade biography (bakery worker, union member) with no prior office and no registered campaign fundraising.
- His listed campaign website is the party newspaper, not a candidate site; campaign activity documented is movement-oriented (rallies, labor solidarity).
