## What this office does

One of 49 Washington State Senators; votes on state law, the state budget,
and taxation. LD 37 covers southeast Seattle (Rainier Valley, Beacon Hill,
Central District), Skyway, and part of Renton.

## Race dynamics

Open seat: incumbent Sen. Rebecca Saldaña announced in December 2025 she
would not seek reelection (Washington State Standard). Two candidates, both
preferring the Democratic Party, are on the primary ballot; with only two
candidates, both advance to the general election regardless of primary
result. Research depth is light for that reason.

- **Chipalo Street** — sitting State Representative (LD 37, since 2023),
  Microsoft technologist; endorsed by Saldaña and the 37th District
  Democrats.
- **Tatiana Brown** — first-time candidate, community organizer and
  co-chair of the WA Environmental Justice Council; rejects corporate PAC
  money; endorsed by Transit Riders Union.

## Differentiating axes

- Legislative record and institutional experience (Street) vs. grassroots
  organizing/outsider profile (Brown).
- Approach to governance: Street points to bills passed and dollars secured;
  Brown emphasizes community assemblies and participatory decision-making.
- Campaign finance posture: Brown explicitly refuses corporate/PAC money.
- Broad policy overlap (progressive taxation, housing, transit, climate),
  so the differentiators are experience and theory of change more than
  issue positions.
