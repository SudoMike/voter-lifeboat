## Background

State Representative for LD 37 since 2023; serves in Democratic leadership
and on tax and budget committees [S1]. Works at Microsoft on technology
safety and security; small business owner and former professional soccer
referee [S1]. Bachelor's and master's in computer science, Brown University
[S1]. Volunteer work includes teaching computer science at Technology
Access Foundation and board service with the Institute for Democratic
Future [S1]. Running for the Senate seat being vacated by Sen. Rebecca
Saldaña, who announced in December 2025 she would not seek reelection [S3].

## Positions

- **Taxes:** Championed the "millionaires tax" to fund education,
  healthcare access, and tax cuts for working families; wants wealthy to
  pay more [S1][S2].
- **Housing:** Passed tenant protections including rent stabilization;
  reformed zoning to increase home building [S1][S2].
- **Transit:** Campaign emphasizes preserving Graham Street and Airport Way
  light rail stations [S2].
- **Civil liberties/immigration:** Cites work restoring abortion funding,
  maintaining food assistance for immigrants, banning ICE mask-wearing, and
  stopping Flock camera surveillance [S1].

## Record

- Says he secured $131 million in state investment for the district,
  including Garfield Super Block, Skyway Homeownership, Tubman Health
  Center, Rainier Valley Food Bank, Childhaven Early Learning Center, and
  $300,000 for a reparations study [S1].
- Campaign cites passage of the millionaire's tax, rent stabilization, and
  an assault weapons ban among accomplishments [S2].

## Endorsements

Campaign lists Sen. Rebecca Saldaña, House Speaker Laurie Jinkins, Attorney
General Nick Brown, King County Executive Girmay Zahilay, and Seattle City
Councilmembers Joy Hollingsworth, Eddie Lin, Alexis Mercedes Rinck, and
Dionne Foster [S2]. Pamphlet cites "unmatched endorsements from labor and
environmental advocates" [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race with a legislative record and elected
  experience (House, 2023–present) [S1].
- Points to concrete delivered items (district funding, passed bills)
  rather than solely campaign promises [S1][S2].
- Backed by the outgoing incumbent senator and much of the Democratic
  establishment [S2].
