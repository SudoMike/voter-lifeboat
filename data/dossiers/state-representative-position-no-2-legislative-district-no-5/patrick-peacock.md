## Background
Republican-preference candidate; describes himself as "not a career politician" [S1]. 23 years of U.S. Army service as a Human Intelligence Warrant Officer and Counter-Intelligence Agent, with two Afghanistan combat tours, a Bronze Star, and a Combat Action Badge [S1][S2]. Vice Chair of the King County GOP; Senior Industrial Security Specialist at Boeing [S1][S2]. MBA (finance emphasis), Webster University; BA in philosophy, University of Washington [S1]. Family has lived in Hobart, WA since 1887; active in local history through the Maple Valley Historical Society tradition [S1][S2].

## Positions
- Taxes/affordability: opposes a state income tax; says high fuel taxes drive up costs; wants to roll back fuel taxes [S1][S2].
- Housing: reduce regulatory barriers to increase housing construction and lower costs [S1][S2].
- Public safety: supports law enforcement and accountability for repeat offenders [S1][S2].
- Education: prioritize reading, math, and practical skills; empower parents [S2].
- Economy: lower business costs to keep employers in Washington [S1][S2].
- Frames the race as a check on "one-party rule" in Olympia [S1][S2].

## Endorsements
- King County Republican Party and 5th LD Republicans [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- Military-intelligence and security career is the core of his resume; no elected experience [S1].
- Platform centers on tax opposition (income tax, fuel taxes) and deregulation, a direct contrast with the incumbent's investment-oriented agenda [S1][S2].
- Endorsements are party organizations only, versus the incumbent's labor/advocacy coalition [S1].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
