## Background
Drew Stokesbary (R, Auburn) is the incumbent and the current House Minority Leader; he has previously served as Chair of the Select Committee on Pension Policy, Ranking Member on House Appropriations, and House Minority Floor Leader [S1]. House Republicans selected him as leader in 2023 after J.T. Wilcox stepped down [S3]. He is a partner at the law firm Chalmers, Adams, Backer & Wallen, advising startups and small businesses on corporate and securities law [S1]. J.D. cum laude, Notre Dame Law School; B.S. Economics, Duke University [S1]. He co-founded the Citizen Action Defense Fund, providing pro bono legal counsel to churches and nonprofits on constitutional-rights matters [S1].

## Positions
- **Taxes/economy:** says taxes "keep increasing, by billions each year" and pledges a "stronger and more affordable economy" [S1].
- **Public safety:** wants "safer neighborhoods where laws are enforced and criminals are apprehended" [S1].
- **Education:** cites dropping test scores, attendance, and college enrollment; pledges "better schools that prepare all students for college or career" [S1].
- Campaign theme is "Let's Fix Washington" [S2].

## Record
- Sitting House Minority Leader; at the January 2026 session opening he cited billions in new 2025 taxes and fees and called the session "an inflection point" given another budget shortfall [S4].

## Endorsements
- Campaign site lists Washington Council of Police and Sheriffs, Washington State PTA, Washington State Council of Fire Fighters, Puget Sound Energy, and Washington Hospitality Association, plus prior Seattle Times and Tacoma News Tribune editorial endorsements (2024) [S2].

## Scoring notes
- Highest-ranking House Republican in the state; leadership position gives him outsized influence relative to a typical district seat [S1][S3].
- Runs on a tax-restraint, public-safety, school-outcomes critique of Democratic majorities [S1][S4].
- Faces a first-time Democratic challenger; both advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
