## Background
Stephen Szczurko-Walton (D) is a first-time candidate with no prior elected experience [S1]. He served as a U.S. Navy nuclear reactor operator from 2015-2024, with three deployments aboard USS Theodore Roosevelt, and now works as a commercial HVAC technician serving schools, hospitals, and small businesses; earlier jobs included retail and campus security [S1]. B.A. Political Science, UC Santa Barbara (2014); Master of Public Administration (2025) [S1].

## Positions
- **Campaign finance:** signature issue is passing SB 6358 to "end corporate election spending in Washington," which his website frames as a way to bypass Citizens United [S1][S2].
- **Affordability:** pledges to bring down housing, healthcare, and tax costs; website advocates universal healthcare and progressive taxation [S1][S2].
- **Climate/energy:** will "defend the Climate Commitment Act" and modernize the electrical grid; website adds maintaining diverse clean energy options [S1][S2].

## Endorsements
None listed in the pamphlet or on the campaign website homepage as of 2026-07-16 [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- The only Democrat in the race, challenging the sitting House Minority Leader; party is the primary differentiator [S1].
- Recent military and skilled-trades background; no legislative or elected record [S1].
- Most policy-specific plank is campaign-finance reform (SB 6358), an issue his opponent's statement does not address [S1][S2].
- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
