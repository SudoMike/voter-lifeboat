## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected from each legislative district for a two-year term, voting on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets.

## The race
Depth: light. Two candidates filed, so **both advance from the August 4, 2026 top-two primary to the November general election** [S1]:

- **Drew Stokesbary (R, incumbent)** — House Minority Leader, attorney, running on a "Let's Fix Washington" message about taxes, crime, and schools [S1].
- **Stephen Szczurko-Walton (D)** — first-time candidate, Navy veteran (nuclear reactor operator) and commercial HVAC technician, running on campaign-finance reform, affordability, and defending the Climate Commitment Act [S1].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Party leadership vs. outsider** — the sitting House Republican leader against a first-time Democratic challenger [S1].
2. **Tax posture** — Stokesbary campaigns against rising state taxes; Szczurko-Walton emphasizes bringing down housing, healthcare, and tax costs through different means, including progressive taxation per his website [S1].
3. **Climate policy** — Szczurko-Walton explicitly defends the Climate Commitment Act; Stokesbary's statement does not address climate [S1].
4. **Campaign finance** — Szczurko-Walton makes ending corporate election spending (SB 6358) his signature issue; not a Stokesbary theme [S1].
