## Background
Kristine Reeves (D) is the incumbent, serving as State Representative for the 30th District 2017-2019 and 2023-present [S1]. She is a Federal Way small business owner in organizational development consulting; previously Director of Economic Development for Washington's Military and Defense Sector at the state Department of Commerce, Executive Director of the Washington Military Alliance, and a statewide veterans coordinator for U.S. Senator Patty Murray [S1]. BA Washington State University; MA Gonzaga University [S1]. Her campaign website was still under construction as of 2026-07-16, showing only her slogan ("An Open Door. An Open Mind.") and donation links [S2].

## Positions
- Runs on continued delivery for the district: protecting homeless students, expanding senior food access, wildfire risk mitigation, and public-safety investment [S1] (record framing, not new promises).
- Emphasizes bipartisan work on veterans and military family issues [S1].

## Record
- Says she passed laws protecting homeless students, expanding nutritious food access for seniors, mitigating wildfire risk, and investing $100 million in local public safety [S1].
- Founded the bipartisan Veterans & Military Families Caucus; passed the Wounded Warrior Shared Leave Act [S1].
- Chairs the House Agriculture and Natural Resources Committee [S1].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites Congressman Adam Smith, mayors and city leaders, labor, nurses, teachers, police, and environmentalists [S1].
- Sierra Club Washington endorsed her in the 2026 primary [S3]; Transportation for Washington also endorsed her [S4].

## Scoring notes
- The incumbent with a multi-term legislative record and committee chairmanship; her opponent has city-council but no legislative experience [S1].
- Both candidates advance to the general; the primary functions mainly as a first head-to-head test [S1].
- Contrast axis is her program-delivery record vs. the challenger's tax-reduction/accountability message [S1].
