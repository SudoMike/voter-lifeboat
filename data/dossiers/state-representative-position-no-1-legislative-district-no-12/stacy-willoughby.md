## Background
Democratic-preference first-time candidate from Chelan challenging incumbent Brian Burnett [S1][S2]. Twenty-five years in financial services, including as a financial advisor and local bank branch manager (manages the Cashmere Valley Bank branch in Chelan) [S1][S2]. Board president of Chelan Valley Hope, a nonprofit providing rent assistance, fuel, firewood, food, and eviction-prevention services; founder of Chelan Business Women Connect; board member of the Lake Chelan Chamber of Commerce [S1][S2]. Graduate of the Leadership Institute of North Central Washington; attended Central Washington University and Skagit Valley College [S1]. Settled in the Lake Chelan area with her husband in 2017 [S2].

## Positions
- Taxes/budget: champion a state budget prioritizing "tax fairness for rural families and small businesses" [S1][S2].
- Housing: affordable housing "that serves each community"; expanding housing availability [S1][S2].
- Workforce: promote trades training for youth; concerned good-paying jobs for young people are vanishing [S1].
- Rural representation: amplifying rural communities' presence in the legislature [S2].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists: 12th Legislative District Democrats, King County Democrats, Washington State Labor Council, AFT-Washington, and Sage Leaders Washington [S1].

## Scoring notes
- First-time candidate whose profile is financial services and anti-poverty nonprofit leadership, versus the incumbent's law-enforcement career [S1][S2].
- Frames affordability (housing, tax fairness, rural economics) as the central issue; incumbent leads with public safety and local control [S1].
- Has Democratic party and labor endorsements; incumbent lists none in the pamphlet [S1].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
