## Background

Keith Scully (prefers Democratic Party) has served on the Shoreline City Council since 2016, including as Deputy Mayor (2020–22) and Mayor (2022–24) [S1]. His career spans work as a paramedic (1994–98), King County Deputy Prosecutor (1999–2005), United Nations war crimes prosecutor (2005–06), Legal Director of the environmental nonprofit Futurewise (2006–08), and intellectual property attorney (2008–present) [S1]. He holds a J.D. from NYU School of Law, an M.A. in Middle Eastern History from Tufts, and a B.A. in History from UCLA [S1]. Community roles include the Regional Crisis Response Agency Board, chairing the Regional EMS Levy Advanced Life Support Committee, King County Conservation Voters Board, Shoreline Planning Commission (member and chair), Salmon Recovery Council Board, and PTA President [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes**: supports progressive tax reform, restructuring the state tax code to mirror the federal progressive system, and overhauling business taxation to tax profit rather than revenue [S2][S3][S4].
- **Budget**: proposes addressing the state budget deficit with across-the-board cuts except education [S3].
- **Housing**: supports expanding affordable housing, including upzoning near transit [S2][S3].
- **Education**: supports funding K-12 education, with funding adjusted for urban areas [S2][S3].
- **Climate/environment**: campaigns on fighting climate change; supports electrification of fleets, homes, and businesses [S2][S3].
- **Childcare and energy affordability**: pledges to make housing, energy, and childcare affordable [S1].
- **Federal issues/rights**: emphasizes defending state rights against federal threats [S2].
- **Technology**: lists "managing AI" among platform topics [S2].

## Record

- In Shoreline, says he expanded affordable housing and transit, brought mental health professionals into the field, and built sidewalks and parks while balancing the budget [S1].
- Shoreline Planning Commission member since 2010, prior to council service [S3].
- Campaign fundraising: $61,868 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, second among the six candidates [S5].

## Endorsements

- The Seattle Times editorial board and The Stranger both endorsed him in this primary [S3][S4]; his site quotes The Stranger calling him "an easy pick in this crowded race" (7/1/26) and the Seattle Times calling him "the best choice to go the distance in tough economic times" (7/7/26) [S2].
- Sierra Club Washington endorsed him for this seat [S6].
- Pamphlet cites Snohomish County Democrats, Shoreline and South County Firefighters, Rep. Cindy Ryu, and "40+ elected officials" [S1].
- Website lists King County Councilmember Rod Dembowski, Snohomish County Councilmember Megan Dunn, Rep. Cindy Ryu, and Shoreline Firefighters (IAFF Local 1760) [S2]; the Herald adds Mountlake Terrace Mayor Steve Woodard, Shoreline Mayor Betsy Robertson, and Washington College Democrats [S3].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate endorsed by both major newspaper editorial boards (Seattle Times and The Stranger) in this primary [S2][S3][S4].
- Endorsed by the retiring incumbent, Rep. Cindy Ryu, whose departure created the open seat [S1][S2].
- Longest local-government tenure in the field (planning commission since 2010, council since 2016, including mayor) and the only candidate from the Shoreline/King County side with local elected experience [S1][S3].
- Pairs progressive tax restructuring with explicit budget-cutting proposals, a combination not matched by others in the field [S3][S4].
