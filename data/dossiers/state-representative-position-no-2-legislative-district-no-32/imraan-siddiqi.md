## Background
Imraan Siddiqi (D) is a first-time candidate with no elected experience [S1]. He is Executive Director of CAIR-Washington (Council on American-Islamic Relations), a civil rights advocate, nonprofit leader, and former small business owner (a coffee shop in Arizona) [S1][S2]. The child of immigrant small-business owners, he was born in Alabama and grew up in Georgia; BA in Business/Marketing, University of Texas Arlington; MBA, Arizona State University [S1][S2]. He describes himself as one of the original plaintiffs against the Trump administration's "Muslim Ban" and organizes coalitions with labor unions, tenant-rights advocates, and the Washington Area Immigrant Solidarity Network [S1]. He announced his campaign in January 2026, challenging incumbent Lauren Davis [S3].

## Positions
- **Affordability:** says wages aren't keeping up with bills; will fight for housing affordability and lower everyday costs [S1][S2].
- **Immigrant protection:** protecting immigrant communities facing "federal targeting" is a core plank [S1][S2].
- **Federal overreach:** pledges "a Washington that stands up to federal overreach" [S1][S2].
- Frames the race as current leaders "aren't doing enough to help" [S1].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites "Democrats, local leaders, and community organizations" [S1].
- News coverage reports endorsements from U.S. Reps. Rashida Tlaib and Ilhan Omar, state Reps. Shaun Scott, Cindy Ryu (32nd LD seatmate), and Julia Reed, UFCW 3000 (dual endorsement), and the Transit Riders Union [S3].

## Scoring notes
- Challenging a same-party incumbent; his differentiators are civil-rights/organizing background and a progressive-movement endorsement coalition vs. the incumbent's establishment-plus-labor slate [S1][S3].
- Issue emphasis centers on immigrant protection and resisting federal actions, where the incumbent centers behavioral health [S1].
- No legislative or elected record; both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome [S1].
