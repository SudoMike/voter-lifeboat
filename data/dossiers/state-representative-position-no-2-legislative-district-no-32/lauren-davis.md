## Background
Lauren Davis (D) is the incumbent, serving as State Representative since 2019 [S1]. Her career is in behavioral health: founder of the Washington Recovery Alliance, UW School of Social Work lecturer, and adolescent mental health program director; she also worked as a Gates Foundation analyst and was a Fulbright Scholar in West Africa [S1]. BA, Brown University [S1]. Community roles include advisory boards for the Latino Center for Health (domestic violence), UW Suicide Care Research Center, and the Lynnwood Community Justice Center [S1].

## Positions
- **Behavioral health/addiction:** her central focus; says she is committed to continuing work on addiction treatment access [S1][S2].
- **Domestic violence:** funded judicial training, legal assistance for survivors, and police-accompanying victim advocates [S1].
- **Immigration:** has "championed healthcare for immigrants and protections against ICE" [S1].
- **Abortion:** describes herself as "completely pro-choice" [S1].
- **Other priorities:** affordability, education funding, homelessness, and gun violence [S1].

## Record
- Passed Ricky's Law (involuntary crisis commitment for life-threatening addiction, which she championed as citizen co-sponsor before taking office) [S1][S2][S3].
- Created the Recovery Navigator Program, launched treatment programs inside jails and ERs, and spearheaded the Lynnwood Crisis Care Center [S1].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists Governors Ferguson and Gregoire; Representatives DelBene, Larsen, and Smith; councilmembers in Seattle, Shoreline, Lynnwood, Mountlake Terrace, and Edmonds; firefighter, nurse, teacher, police, and grocery-worker unions; the State Labor Council; and the 32nd District Democrats [S1].

## Scoring notes
- Seven-year incumbent with one of the Legislature's most identifiable single-issue records (behavioral health/addiction) [S1][S3].
- Endorsement base spans establishment Democrats and a broad labor slate, contrasting with her challenger's progressive-movement coalition [S1].
- Both candidates are Democrats and both advance to the general; the primary is effectively a first round of an intra-party contest [S1].
