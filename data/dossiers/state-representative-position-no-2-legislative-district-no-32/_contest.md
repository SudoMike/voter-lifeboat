## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected from each legislative district for a two-year term, voting on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets.

## The race
Depth: light. LD 32 covers Shoreline, Lynnwood, Mountlake Terrace, Edmonds, and nearby communities straddling the King/Snohomish county line [S1][S2]. Two candidates filed — both Democrats — so **both advance from the August 4, 2026 top-two primary to the November general election** [S1]:

- **Lauren Davis (D, incumbent)** — representative since 2019, behavioral-health specialist known for Ricky's Law and the Recovery Navigator Program [S1].
- **Imraan Siddiqi (D)** — Executive Director of CAIR-Washington, civil rights advocate, first-time candidate challenging the incumbent from the left/organizing wing of the party [S1][S2].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Incumbent record vs. movement challenge** — Davis runs on a substantial behavioral-health and domestic-violence legislative record; Siddiqi runs on coalition organizing, immigrant protection, and "current leaders aren't doing enough" [S1].
2. **Issue emphasis** — behavioral health, addiction, and gun violence (Davis) vs. immigrant rights, standing up to federal targeting/overreach, and housing affordability (Siddiqi) [S1].
3. **Endorsement coalitions** — Davis lists governors, members of Congress, and a broad labor slate; Siddiqi's backers include progressive members of Congress (Tlaib, Omar), a sitting seatmate legislator, UFCW 3000, and the Transit Riders Union [S1][S2].
4. **Same-party contest** — with two Democrats, the race turns on record, priorities, and coalition rather than party [S1].
