## Background
Tina L. Orwall (D) is the incumbent State Senator for the 33rd District, appointed by the King County Council in December 2024 to succeed longtime Senator Karen Keiser after roughly 15 years representing the district in the State House, where she served as Speaker Pro Tempore [S1][S3]. She is Vice Chair of the Senate Health & Long-Term Care Committee and serves on Rules, Human Services, Housing, and the Joint Committee on Veterans' & Military Affairs [S1]. A career social worker, she is UW affiliate faculty with twenty years in behavioral health, consulting, and grant writing; she holds an MSW and BS in Psychology from UW [S1].

## Positions
- **Housing:** continued work on affordable housing and strengthening homeowners' rights [S1].
- **Healthcare/behavioral health:** comprehensive healthcare, the 988 crisis line, and behavioral-health capacity [S1].
- **AI consumer protection:** cites work creating protections against "unregulated AI," including prohibiting insurers from using AI to deny care [S1].
- **Airport communities:** airport pollution mitigation for communities around Sea-Tac [S1].
- **Vulnerable populations:** protecting survivors of sexual abuse, gun violence, and suicide; backstopping programs threatened by federal cuts [S1].
- **Other:** childcare access, language access in schools, climate solutions [S1].

## Record
- Pamphlet cites this year's bills strengthening protections against child sexual exploitation, prohibiting insurance use of AI to deny care, investing in flood recovery, reducing youth homelessness, and strengthening homeowner rights [S1].
- Sponsored the 2022 Language Access law (HB 1153) for family engagement in schools and 2024 legislation against non-consensual deepfake intimate images [S2].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites "local mayors and councilmembers, unions and state leaders" without naming individuals [S1].
