## Background

Heather-Marie Wilson is a research scientist with no prior elected experience, running with no party preference [S1]. She holds a PhD in Pathology from the University of Washington and a BS in Biochemistry from the University of Nevada Reno [S1]. Her professional history includes Senior Scientist at Sigma Genetics, Staff Scientist in the Hope Heart Matrix Biology Program at Benaroya Research Institute, Research Associate in Transplantation Biology at Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center, and multiple research and teaching roles at the University of Washington [S1]. Community involvement includes serving as a USSF soccer referee, manager and board treasurer of Emerald City FC, and board member of Northwest Center Child Development [S1]. News coverage identifies her as a cell biologist and political newcomer [S3]. She has no campaign website listed and had reported no fundraising as of late June 2026 [S1][S2][S3].

## Positions

- **Taxes / economy (her central theme):** Argues that "recently added taxation on businesses and high wage earners is leading to layoffs and an exodus of tax wealth, jobs, and services from our state"; cites a claimed $500 million-plus loss in adjusted gross income to out-migration between 2022 and 2023 after the capital gains tax passed [S1]. (This is her characterization; the pamphlet is not fact-checked [S1].)
- **Budget:** Warns that as the Legislature fixes budget shortfalls it "will target Washingtonians already struggling" with existing taxes and rising costs for businesses, groceries, and housing [S1].
- **Public services / arts:** Argues lost state revenue harms schools, public services, and arts and culture communities dependent on private philanthropy [S1].
- **Governing approach:** Describes a caution-first, data-driven philosophy: "Most human-made catastrophes start with good intentions and poor foresight… Politicians should be just as cautious when making policies"; says she approaches challenges "through data, discussion, and collaboration" [S1].
