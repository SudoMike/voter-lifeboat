## Background

Javier Valdez is the incumbent State Senator for Legislative District 46 (northeast Seattle), a Democrat, and Chair of the Senate State Government, Tribal Affairs & Elections Committee [S1][S3]. He previously served as a State Representative; his legislative service dates to 2017 in the House, moving to the Senate by 2023 [S1][S3]. He has worked for the City of Seattle for over two decades, leading programs to expand opportunities for women- and minority-owned businesses [S1]. The son and grandson of farmworkers and laborers, he holds a B.A. in Business Administration from the University of Washington and an M.P.A. from Baruch College (CUNY) [S1][S2]. He has served on the board of the Washington State Hispanic Chamber of Commerce, as President of Hispanic Seafair, and as president of AFSCME Local 21C [S1]. He describes himself as one of the first Latinos in the state Senate [S1].

## Positions

- **Gun safety:** Wrote laws banning ghost guns and high-capacity magazines; says he wants to pass legislation next session prohibiting carrying guns in parks and fairgrounds [S1]. His SB 5098, restricting weapons in places where children and families gather (parks, county fairgrounds), passed the Senate in 2025 but did not receive a House vote; he has said he intends to advance it in 2026 [S4].
- **Housing and cost of living:** Says he helped reform zoning laws to allow more affordable housing and supported a cap on rent increases [S1].
- **Taxes:** Voted to tax millionaires and the very wealthy to fund free school lunches, small-business tax relief, and healthcare investments [S1].
- **Immigration and federal relations:** Says he is leading efforts to ban ICE agents from wearing masks, ban federal agents from using cameras to surveil immigrants, and block federal access to state voter data [S1].
- **Reproductive health:** Says he helped backfill state funding for Planned Parenthood after federal cuts [S1].
- **Equity:** Campaign priorities include expanding economic opportunity and advancing racial and social justice through policy and budgeting [S2].

## Record

- Current committee assignments: Chair, State Government, Tribal Affairs & Elections; member, Transportation and Law & Justice committees [S3].
- Sponsored SB 5098 (weapons restrictions in parks and similar public places); passed the Senate, did not receive a House floor vote [S4].
- As a House member: HB 1739, banning untraceable 3D-printed ("ghost") firearms; HB 1732, expanding hate-crime classifications; HB 2632, making false crime/emergency reporting ("swatting") a felony [S2].

## Endorsements

Per his pamphlet statement: 46th District Democrats, Washington State Labor Council, Planned Parenthood Alliance Advocates, Washington Conservation Action, One America Votes, Washington State Nurses Association, "and more" [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Incumbent with a legislative record (House since 2017, Senate since 2023) and a committee chairmanship, versus a first-time legislative candidate opponent [S1][S3].
- Signature record areas: gun regulation (ghost guns, magazine limits, SB 5098), progressive tax measures, and housing/zoning reform [S1][S4].
- Explicitly frames his candidacy around opposing Trump-administration policies on immigration enforcement and reproductive-health funding [S1].
- Carries a broad slate of Democratic-aligned institutional endorsements (labor, environmental, reproductive-rights organizations) [S1].
