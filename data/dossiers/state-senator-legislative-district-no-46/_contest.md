## What the office does

A Washington State Senator is one of 49 members of the state Senate, serving a four-year term. Senators write and vote on state laws, adopt the state operating/capital/transportation budgets, and set statewide policy on taxes, housing, public safety, education, healthcare, and elections. District 46 covers northeast Seattle (Lake City, Wedgwood, View Ridge, and surrounding neighborhoods).

## Race dynamics

Only two candidates filed, so both advance from the August primary to the November general election regardless of the primary result. Javier Valdez is the Democratic incumbent, a sitting senator and committee chair with a legislative record dating to 2017; Sandra Stephens is a first-time Republican candidate, a retired nurse with no campaign website and no public-office record. Evidence is asymmetric: Valdez has an extensive documented record, while Stephens's file is pamphlet-only.

## Differentiating issue axes

- **Gun regulation:** Valdez authored ghost-gun and high-capacity-magazine bans and is pushing restrictions on guns in parks/fairgrounds; Stephens aligns with the King County Republican Party platform and does not address guns in her statement.
- **Taxes and spending:** Valdez supports taxes on millionaires/the very wealthy to fund school lunches, healthcare, and small-business relief; Stephens frames rising taxes as the product of corruption and misdirected funds through unaudited nonprofits.
- **Reproductive rights:** Valdez emphasizes backfilling Planned Parenthood funding; Stephens identifies as pro-life (past officer of the National Association of Pro-Life Nurses).
- **Immigration and federal relations:** Valdez centers opposition to federal immigration enforcement tactics (ICE masking, surveillance, voter-data access); Stephens does not address immigration.
- **Experience:** Incumbent legislator and committee chair versus a candidate whose background is in nursing and community volunteering with no legislative experience.
