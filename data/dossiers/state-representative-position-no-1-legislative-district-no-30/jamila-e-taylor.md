## Background
Incumbent Democratic state representative for the 30th Legislative District, seeking a third term (per her campaign site; first elected 2020) [S1][S2]. Chair of the House Civil Rights & Judiciary Committee; chair of the Developmental Disability Advocacy Caucus; vice chair of the Members of Color Caucus; member of the Transportation and Early Learning & Human Services committees [S1]. Attorney: former statewide advocacy counsel managing legal-aid attorneys serving domestic-violence survivors and crime victims; ran youth violence-reduction efforts; owns a private law practice and nonprofit consultancy [S1]. JD, University of Oregon; BA, Virginia State University [S1]. Board member of BlackPast.org, Federal Way Human Services Commission, Highline College Foundation, and Girl Scouts [S1].

## Positions
- Public safety: laws to reduce gun violence, improve police training and trust, and address root causes of crime [S1].
- Affordability: resources for housing and childcare, small-business tax reductions, and expanded working-family tax credits [S1].
- Economy: works with businesses, unions, and schools to protect jobs and expand apprenticeships and career pathways [S1].
- Housing equity: campaign highlights Washington's new homeownership program and work addressing racially restrictive property deeds [S2].

## Record
- Cites passing laws on gun-violence reduction, police training/trust, small-business tax reduction, and working-family tax credit expansion [S1].
- Campaign site highlights homeownership-access legislation and remediation of discriminatory deed covenants [S2].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists: 30th District Democrats, State Labor Council, Planned Parenthood, OneAmerica Votes, Sierra Club, housing advocates, teachers, healthcare workers [S1].
- Campaign site states The Seattle Times endorsed her for a third term [S2].

## Scoring notes
- Incumbent committee chair (Civil Rights & Judiciary) with a legal-aid/victim-advocacy background; opponent is a first-time candidate [S1].
- Record spans both public-safety legislation and affordability measures (tax credits, housing), the two axes her opponent also runs on from the opposite direction [S1].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
