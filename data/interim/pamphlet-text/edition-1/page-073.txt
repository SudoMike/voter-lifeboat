Statement: North Seattle deserves a city councilmember who listens, 
responds, and delivers on our everyday needs. Seattle is a deeply 
progressive city. We have the resources, the talent, and the votes to 
tackle major challenges, but often City Hall feels disconnected from 
what people are experiencing day to day.
When the Fred Meyer in Lake City closed, my kids asked why nobody 
fought harder to save it or replace it. I didn’t have a good answer. 
Families without cars lost access to groceries and pharmacy services 
overnight, and our city had no real plan. That’s not acceptable. And 
housing costs continue to skyrocket, pushing more people into 
instability and homelessness while longtime residents struggle to stay 
in the neighborhoods they love.
We need a councilmember who shows up, listens, and focuses on 
practical solutions. My priorities are straightforward: build more 
aff  ordable housing, improve safety on Lake City Way and Aurora, 
support neighborhood businesses, strengthen gun violence 
prevention, protect immigrant communities from federal overreach, 
move us toward meeting our climate goals, and make sure ranked 
choice voting is successfully implemented in 2027 so voters trust and 
understand the system.
I live in Lake City with my husband Rob and our two kids. I work at 
FairVote Washington, where I work every day on democracy and 
coalition building across our state. From serving as PTA Advocacy 
Chair at John Rogers Elementary to organizing across Seattle, my work 
has always been about connecting people and delivering results.
I’d be honored to earn your vote.
