Everyone’s Voice Matters
People who have been to jail have voting rights. 
Your vote is one way to make your voice heard!
Before you can vote, you have 
to be registered.
Register to vote online at 
VoteWA.gov or call King County 
Elections at 206-296-VOTE (8683).
We’ll send you a voter registration 
form. Fill it out and mail it back to 
King County Elections, 919 SW 
Grady Way, Renton, WA 98057.
How do I get my ballot?
Ballots are automatically mailed 
to registered voters around 
3 weeks before Election Day. 
You can list the detention center 
address as your mailing address 
or have a loved one forward your ballot to 
you. Need help? Call us at 206-296-VOTE 
(8683). We will work with you and the 
detention center to get your ballot to you.
Get your ballot in on time.
You don’t need a stamp! But 
you do need to vote as early 
as possible. Get your ballot in 
the mail at least a week before 
Election Day.
