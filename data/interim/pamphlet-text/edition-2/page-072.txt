residents feel that our region is becoming less aff  ordable, less

frustrate willing taxpayers by asking for more and delivering too little 
in return.
King County families are being squeezed by rising housing costs, 
taxes, insurance, and everyday expenses. At the same time, 
public conﬁ  dence has declined as we continue to see worsening 
homelessness, persistent property crime, struggling business districts, 
and major questions about oversight and spending.
I believe county government must return to its core responsibilities: 
accountability, aff  ordability, public safety, and eff  ective services.  As 
your councilmember, I will focus on stronger oversight of county 
departments and taxpayer dollars. Programs should be measured by 
outcomes, not political messaging. Residents deserve transparency, 
independent auditing, and leadership willing to challenge failing 
policies without clear results, even when they come from their own 
allies.
I also believe we need a more balanced approach to public safety 
and homelessness. Compassion matters deeply, but compassion 
without accountability is not a strategy. We must prioritize treatment, 
recovery, safer public spaces, and measurable performance standards 
for the hundreds of millions spent annually across our regional 
systems.
Finally, I will bring a practical, solutions-oriented perspective shaped 
by years of leadership experience managing large teams, budgets, 
and operational change in both the private and public sectors. I’m not 
running to protect the status quo. I’m running to help restore public 
trust and ensure King County remains a place where working families, 
businesses, and future generations can thrive.
We can build a King County that is both compassionate and 
accountable. That starts with putting measurable results ahead of 
politics.
