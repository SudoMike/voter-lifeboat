## What this office does

U.S. representatives serve two-year terms, legislate and vote in the House, conduct committee oversight, and provide constituent services [S2].

## Race dynamics

Incumbent Kim Schrier faces five challengers in Washington's top-two primary: Democrats Keith Arnold and Schrier, and Republicans Andres Valleza, Bob Hagglund, Spencer Meline, and Trinh Ha appear in the official field [S1]. Several challengers have little verifiable issue material, so apparent differences must not be inferred from party preference [S1].

## What genuinely differentiates these candidates

- Schrier offers an incumbent congressional and health-policy record; Hagglund emphasizes costs, trade, and a market-oriented clean-energy approach [S1].
- Arnold offers a long, ideologically mixed issue platform centered on economic fairness and institutional reform [S1].
- Evidence is insufficient to characterize the remaining challengers across comparable policy axes without overstatement [S1].
