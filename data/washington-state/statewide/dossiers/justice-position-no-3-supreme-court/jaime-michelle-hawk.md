## Background

Jaime Michelle Hawk, 48, of Federal Way, is a King County Superior Court judge [S3][S4]. She was appointed to that court by Governor Jay Inslee in 2022 and has since won election twice [S5]. Her earlier career spans roughly 20 years: state and federal public defender in eastern Washington (including Grant and Spokane counties), civil rights work with the ACLU of Washington — she is the first ACLU of Washington attorney to become a judge — and adjunct law professor at Gonzaga University School of Law, where she earned her law degree with honors [S1][S2][S4][S5]. Raised by a single mother, she was the first in her family to attend college; before law school she worked supporting domestic violence survivors [S1]. She serves on the Washington Supreme Court Minority and Justice Commission [S1][S2].

## Positions

- Says justices must apply the rule of law "based on our constitution, our statutes … precedent … not personal views," while remaining open to reform where necessary [S4].
- Campaign priorities: upholding the rule of law, protecting constitutional rights, expanding access to justice, and maintaining an independent judiciary [S5].
- Emphasizes bringing the perspective of women and working families to the court [S1].
- Highlights work on a Supreme Court commission addressing bias elimination and court accessibility for working families [S2].

## Record

- Has presided over hundreds of criminal, civil, and family law cases on the Superior Court, as well as appeals from lower courts; her pamphlet states she has never had a trial overturned for legal error [S1].
- As a federal public defender, handled trials and appeals defending indigent clients against federal prosecution [S1][S2].
- Appointed by Senators Murray and Cantwell to their federal judicial selection committee; selected by the American Bar Association to observe Guantanamo Bay military hearings and to assist families separated at the border [S1].

## Endorsements

- Governor Bob Ferguson, Congresswoman Pramila Jayapal, Justice Helen Whitener, retired Justice Mary Yu, Spokane Mayor Lisa Brown, and Court of Appeals Chief Judge Cecily Hazelrigg [S1][S4][S5].
- More than 100 current and retired judges and justices; the Washington State Labor Council, National Women's Political Caucus, and local/county Democratic organizations [S1][S2].
- Fundraising: about $163,000 raised as of early July 2026, second in the race [S4].
