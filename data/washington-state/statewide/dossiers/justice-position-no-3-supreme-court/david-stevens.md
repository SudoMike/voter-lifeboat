## Background

David Stevens, 64, of Shelton, is a Mason County Superior Court judge who reached the bench by election rather than gubernatorial appointment [S1][S3]. His legal career includes service as a senior felony prosecutor (including as a Spokane County deputy prosecutor), federal public defender, chief public defender for the Colville Confederated Tribes, federal prosecutor, federal civil rights investigator with the Fair Housing Enforcement Center in Seattle, international prosecutor in Pristina, Kosovo, and justice advisor in Bamiyan, Afghanistan [S1][S3]. He is a U.S. Navy veteran who attended the University of Washington on veterans' benefits and earned his JD from the UW School of Law; he also holds a State Department certificate in Islamic jurisprudence [S1].

## Positions

- Describes himself as a "strict constructionist": courts should read statutes literally without resorting to legislative-intent analysis, and should interpret the Washington Constitution "based on its text and original meaning and not on evolving societal standards" [S2][S3].
- Says public policy "is the province of the political branches and not the courts," and that the Supreme Court is "a constitutional court, not a policy-making body" [S1][S2].
- Argues the modern pattern of gubernatorial appointment of judges undermines the state constitution's design of electing judges, and that his election would bring "common sense and balance" to a court he sees as progressive-leaning [S1][S3].
- On court administration: opposes race-based jury selection, supports issuing warrants for defendants who fail to appear, and says constitutional change requires formal amendment rather than judicial reinterpretation [S2].

## Record

- Has tried more than 200 jury trials as a lawyer and judge, with 25+ years of trial and international court experience [S1][S3].
- Sitting Superior Court judge in Mason County; his campaign emphasizes that he was elected, not appointed, to the bench [S1][S3].

## Endorsements

- Backed by the Washington State, King County, and Snohomish County Republican parties [S3].
- His campaign site displays the Washington State Chiefs of Police (WACOPS) logo; his pamphlet cites support from tribes, judges, public defenders, prosecutors, attorneys, and law enforcement [S1][S2].
- Fundraising: roughly $23,000-$30,000 raised as of late June/early July 2026, the least in the race [S3].

## Scoring notes

- The race's textualist/originalist candidate: the only one who describes himself as a strict constructionist and openly criticizes the court's progressive lean [S2][S3].
- Career spans both prosecution and indigent defense plus international rule-of-law work — an unusual mix relative to Hawk (defense/ACLU) and Diaz (federal prosecutor/appellate judge) [S1].
- Only candidate in the race who reached the bench by election rather than appointment, which he makes a central argument [S1][S3].
- Holds Republican party recommendations in a nonpartisan race, opposite the Democratic-aligned endorsements of both opponents; trails badly in fundraising [S3].
