## Background

Laura Christensen Colberg is a family law attorney with 29-30 years in practice who has served for the past 18 years as a Snohomish County Superior Court Commissioner pro tem and court-appointed arbitrator, in which roles she says she has made thousands of judicial decisions [S1][S3]. She grew up in Wenatchee (high school valedictorian), earned a BA summa cum laude from Seattle Pacific University, and a JD from the University of Washington School of Law [S1][S2]. She has been a family law lecturer for the Rigos Bar Review (1997-2013) and is active in the Free Methodist church, including as denominational director of its Bible Quizzing program [S1].

## Positions

- Emphasizes impartiality and having "zero history of partisan activism"; says she would "measure all laws against the Constitution as written" [S1].
- Stresses judicial independence from the appointment pipeline, saying she "owes no favors to any governor, legislator, or party" [S2].
- Would advocate for "fast-tracking" appeals in family law matters, arguing delay harms children in crisis, and notes the Supreme Court oversees lower-court procedures and priorities in addition to deciding cases [S1].
- Argues her family law expertise is distinctive and intersects property, tax, criminal, and constitutional matters; says no justice with deep family law expertise has served since Washington legalized no-fault divorce 52 years ago [S1][S2].
- Criticizes the incumbent's gubernatorial appointment as creating a "conflict of interest" and a "public perception that the judiciary is just being stacked" [S3].

## Record

- 18 years of part-time quasi-judicial service as commissioner pro tem and arbitrator; she has not held a full-time judicial office [S1][S3].

## Endorsements

- Recommended by the Washington State Republican Party [S3].
- Fundraising: about $27,000 raised as of early July 2026, of which $14,700 was her own money [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Positions herself as the candidate with the most hands-on bench experience in this race (pro tem/arbitrator decisions), though it is part-time quasi-judicial service rather than a judgeship [S1][S3].
- Only candidate in the race with a family law specialty; her signature proposal is faster appellate handling of family law cases [S1].
- Runs as an outsider to the appointment pipeline and holds the state Republican Party's recommendation in a nonpartisan race [S2][S3].
- Smallest fundraising total in the Position 1 race, with a majority of it self-funded [S3].
