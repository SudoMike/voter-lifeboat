## Background

Colleen Melody is the incumbent Washington Supreme Court Justice in Position 1, appointed by Governor Bob Ferguson in November 2025 to succeed retiring Justice Mary Yu; she took office January 1, 2026 [S3][S4]. She is 43, a Spokane native and Ferris High School graduate, and earned her undergraduate and law degrees from the University of Washington, graduating first in her law school class [S3][S4]. Before joining the court she led the Attorney General's Office Wing Luke Civil Rights Division from its creation in 2015, and earlier spent four years as a trial attorney in the U.S. Department of Justice Civil Rights Division, where she received the U.S. Attorney General's Award for Outstanding New Employee [S3][S4]. She has also taught at the University of Washington and brought pro bono cases on behalf of immigrants and veterans [S1].

## Positions

- Emphasizes judicial independence and impartiality, and argues state courts must safeguard rights in privacy, the workplace, housing, healthcare, and education, particularly as federal law changes [S1][S4].
- Says courts must be places where victims of crime and discrimination can seek justice "with safety and dignity" [S1].
- Defends Washington's judicial-election system as providing "more accountability to voters" than lifetime federal appointments, and disputes the idea that gubernatorial appointees uniformly agree with the governors who appointed them [S4].
- Campaign priorities include protecting workplace rights, healthcare access, and civil and constitutional protections, and keeping courts accessible and trustworthy [S2].

## Record

- As head of the AG's Wing Luke Civil Rights Division: led the successful challenge to the first Trump administration travel ban; won the initial temporary restraining order against the second Trump administration's attempt to end birthright citizenship; litigated to preserve DACA status for nearly 20,000 Washington residents; and defended state courts against federal arrests of non-citizens at courthouses [S3].
- Recovered wages owed to workers and litigated pregnancy-discrimination and sexual-harassment cases [S1].
- On the Supreme Court since January 2026; has recused herself from two cases, applying judicial-conduct standards on impartiality [S4].

## Endorsements

- Every sitting Supreme Court justice (her eight colleagues), retired Justices Yu and Madsen, Governors Ferguson, Inslee, and Gregoire, Attorney General Nick Brown, hundreds of judges and elected officials, and the Washington State Labor Council, per her pamphlet statement [S1][S4].
- The Seattle Times editorial board recommended Melody for Position 1 in the 2026 primary [S5].
- Fundraising: about $205,000 raised as of early July 2026, including contributions from the Washington Education Association and former Seattle Mayor Jenny Durkan [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in this race currently serving on the Supreme Court, though her tenure is short (took office January 2026); her pre-bench record is as a career civil rights litigator, not a judge [S3][S4].
- Has by far the broadest institutional support in the race: all sitting justices, three governors, the AG, and the Seattle Times [S1][S4][S5].
- Her appointment by Governor Ferguson (her former boss as AG) is the central criticism raised by both challengers, who frame it as an impartiality/appointment-pipeline concern [S4].
- Largest fundraising total in the Position 1 race by roughly a factor of four [S4].
