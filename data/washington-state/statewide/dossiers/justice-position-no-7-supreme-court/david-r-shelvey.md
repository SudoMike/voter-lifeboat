## Background

David R Shelvey is a solo-practice attorney based in Sumner (Pierce County) who reports eleven years practicing administrative law, civil litigation, personal injury, collections, family law, estate planning, probate, and taxation [S1]. He reports no judicial experience, which his pamphlet statement characterizes as "the same as many lawyers have when they take to the bench" [S1]. He is a veteran [S1]. He holds an MBA from City University of Seattle, a J.D. and LL.M. in Taxation from Thomas M. Cooley Law School, and an LL.M. in Global Law and Economics from Antonin Scalia Law School [S1]. Before law school he worked four years as a paralegal and also worked as a tax preparer for H&R Block [S3].

He ran for Washington Supreme Court Justice Position 2 in 2024, receiving 59,676 votes (3.4%) in the four-way August primary; Sal Mungia (43.4%) and Dave Larson (36.5%) advanced to the general election [S4][S1]. His 2026 pamphlet statement opens by referencing that run and thanking those voters [S1]. His community service includes serving as volunteer president of the Tacoma Eagles (Fraternal Order of Eagles Aerie #3), serving meals for Catholic Community Services and a local synagogue, and pro bono legal advice for low-income residents, seniors, teamsters, veterans, and nonprofits [S1][S3]. He discloses that he lives with a mild form of Parkinson's disease that affects him physically [S1].

## Positions

Framed as judicial philosophy and stated approach:

- Says he approaches every decision "with respect, fairness, and impartiality—guided by sound judgment and common sense," and that he judges no one by gender, skin color, language, health condition, or religion [S1].
- States in his pamphlet that he opposes a state income tax and "won't be soft on criminals" — outcome-oriented statements presented in a nonpartisan judicial race [S1].
- Says he will "stand with American small businesses and workers" and protect the rights and freedoms of every citizen [S1].
- Says he will pursue "practical economic solutions" to ease heavy caseloads facing prosecutors and public defenders [S1].
- Campaign website emphasizes protecting "freedoms and rights," practical "in the trenches" experience, and responsiveness, with the slogan "Because I will put you first!"; it lists no detailed judicial-philosophy statements [S2].
- In his 2024 run, he identified re-evaluating past wrongful convictions as a key priority and said he wanted to be "non-biased and fair to each person" [S3].
- Deliberately does not list endorsements, stating during his 2024 run that he wants to maintain the impartiality expected of a justice [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in this contest with no judicial experience, per his own pamphlet statement; his opponent is the sitting Chief Justice [S1].
- His practice background is solo family/estate/tax law rather than appellate litigation, the Supreme Court's core work [S1][S3].
- Minimal campaign operation: PDC records for the 2026 cycle show no reported contributions and about $2,848 in expenditures; in 2024 he raised the least of the four Position 2 candidates (about $2,400) [S5][S3].
- Repeat candidate: received 3.4% statewide in the 2024 Position 2 primary and did not advance [S4].
- His pamphlet statement takes positions on policy outcomes (state income tax, treatment of criminal defendants), which contrasts with the incumbent's platform framed around judicial independence; news coverage notes the 2026 court races are drawing attention amid a pending constitutional challenge to the state's new tax on incomes over $1 million [S1][S6].
