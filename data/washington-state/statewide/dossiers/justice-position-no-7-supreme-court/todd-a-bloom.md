## Background

Todd A. Bloom is a tax and business attorney with over two decades of experience providing legal and professional services to private and publicly held companies, executives, business owners, and families, and he served as an arbitrator for FINRA from 1999 to 2020 [S1]. He spent over a decade as a tax attorney and senior manager in major accounting firms and holds CPA, CFA, and CFP designations [S1]. He served as an active-duty Navy officer from 2002 to 2013, called up from the Naval Reserve after 9/11, having earlier earned his commission from the enlisted ranks; per his WSBA bio he retired as a Lieutenant Commander in 2018 and deployed in support of military and counter-terrorism operations in the Persian Gulf, the Pacific, Southeast Asia, and South America [S1][S2]. His education includes an LL.M. from the University of Washington, a J.D. and M.B.A. from Tulane University, an M.A. from the Naval War College, a B.A. from Simpson College, and the College of Naval Command & Staff [S1][S2]. He has served on the Washington State Bar Association Board of Governors representing District 6 since 2023, and his other listed service includes a Nonprofit Finance Council and adjunct faculty positions at UW and Tulane [S1][S2]. He was originally admitted to the bar in Louisiana before relocating to the Pacific Northwest [S2]. He is challenging incumbent Chief Justice Debra Stephens for Position 7; criminal defense attorney Karim Merchant and family law attorney David Shelvey are also in the August primary [S3]. He lists no campaign website in the voters' pamphlet [S1].

## Positions

Framed as judicial philosophy and approach (this is a nonpartisan judicial race):

- Pledges to "uphold and apply the law impartially and fairly," and frames the election as a choice between retaining current justices or bringing "meaningful new perspective, experience, and common sense" to balance the court [S1].
- In a July 6, 2026 Center Square Q&A, he said judges owe loyalty to the Constitution and should defend it rather than make law, and emphasized judicial independence from those who appoint judges and resistance to political pressure [S3].
- Expressed concern about public perceptions of a partisan judiciary, the influence of endorsements, and what he called "two tiers of justice," arguing courts must enforce the law consistently [S3].
- On specific rulings: he said the court's Quinn decision upholding the capital gains tax was "wrongly decided," siding with the dissent and praising Justice Gordon McCloud's dissenting reasoning; he also criticized the Blake drug-possession decision, saying its outcome "astonishes" average citizens and undermines trust in the court [S3].
- Attributes his approach to solving "complex problems thoughtfully, collaboratively, and fairly," citing his working background (farm, grocery, and restaurant jobs; enlisted service) and his post-9/11 deployment as shaping his understanding of duty, accountability, and leadership under pressure [S1].

## Record

- Elected member, WSBA Board of Governors (District 6) since 2023; his pamphlet statement says his board service is where he was encouraged to run for the Supreme Court [S1][S2].
- FINRA arbitrator, 1999–2020, hearing securities-industry disputes [S1].
- No judicial service; no court rulings to evaluate [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

- The Washington State Republican Party issued a "recommendation" (its term for nonpartisan judicial races, rather than "endorsement") of Bloom for Position 7 on June 24, 2026 [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Professional background is in tax, business, and securities law (CPA/CFA/CFP, FINRA arbitration) rather than courtroom litigation or judicial service — a contrast with both the incumbent and opponent Merchant's criminal-defense career [S1][S3].
- Only current statewide-elected credential is bar governance: WSBA Board of Governors member for District 6 since 2023 [S2].
- Publicly criticized both the Quinn (capital gains tax) and Blake (drug possession) decisions, whereas opponent Merchant criticized Quinn but supported Blake [S3].
- Carries the WAGOP "recommendation" for Position 7; judicial races are formally nonpartisan [S4].
- Minimal fundraising: 8 reported contributions totaling about $4,700 as of mid-July 2026 per PDC data [S5].
