3 Dear Voters, Soon, it will be time to vote in the August Primary. This election is a chance for you to have a direct say in our local leadership. My goal is to make sure you have the tools to vote with confidence. Getting Your Ballot to Us If you plan to mail your ballot, please do it early. The U.S. Postal Service has changed how and when they postmark mail. Because of these changes, we can't guarantee your ballot will have a valid postmark if it's mailed within a week of Election Day. The best way to return your ballot is to use one of our 31 drop boxes. These boxes are strategically placed across the county for your convenience; a full list can be found at ThurstonWaVotes.gov and on page 4 of this pamphlet. They're also the most secure and direct route to our office. During every election, teams of two election workers empty these boxes and bring the ballots straight to our processing center. We keep a close eye on your vote every step of the way. How We Protect Your Vote When your ballot arrives, we check your signature against the one in your voter registration record. Only you can vote your ballot. Every single signature is reviewed by staff members who are trained to spot signature fraud. Once we receive your ballot, you're credited for voting so that no one else can use your name. You can see this for yourself by checking your ballot status at VoteWA.gov . Your privacy is always protected. Reflections on a Decade of Service This is my last year serving as your County Auditor. I've been serving in this role since 2013, and these years have truly been the highlight of my 30-year elections career. It's been a sincere honor to serve you. I'm so proud of the team we've built here. They show up every day to make sure your voice is heard. We've also done a lot of work to improve election processes, security and voter access for the entire state. We led the effort to secure universal vote-by-mail and same-day registration. We even convinced the state t o finally pay its fair share of election costs. As I leave this office, I’m proud that we’ve made voting more secure, accessible and equitable for all. Thank you for the trust you've put in me over the years. Respectfully Mary Hall Mary Hall Thurston County Auditor, CERA Message from Thurston County Auditor Mary Hall

Position Descriptions Office Term * Description U.S. Representative 2 year Helps create federal level laws, federal budgets, represents constituents' interests, and may impeach federal officials. State Senator 4 year Shares responsibility with state Representatives for making state laws and adopting the state budget. Also responsible for confirming appointments made by the Governor. State Representative 2 year Shares responsibility with state Senators for making state laws and adopting the state budget. Assessor 4 year Determines market value of all real and personal property to calculate property taxes. Auditor 4 year Runs elections, handles licenses and titles, keeps real estate records, provides passport services, and manages the county’s financial operations, including budgeting, accounting, and payroll. Clerk 4 year Preserves records of the opinions, decisions, and judgments of the court. County Commissioner 4 year Serves as the local government and legislative authority outside city limits. Coroner 4 year Investigates all sudden, violent, suspicious, and unusual deaths occurring in the county. Prosecuting Attorney 4 year Prosecutes criminal actions in superior, district, and juvenile courts and provides legal advice to the county. Sheriff 4 year In charge of law enforcement, jail facilities, civil and legal processing and emergency operations. Treasurer 4 year Provides banking, investment, debt and cash management services for the county and all local governments within the county, except cities. Supreme Court Justice 6 year As the highest court in the state, hears appeals and reviews decisions from the Courts of Appeals and other lower courts. Court of Appeals Judge 6 year Hears and decides appeals from Superior Courts. District Court Judge 4 year Hears civil, small claims, criminal, and traffic cases. Public Utility District Commissioner 6 year Provides utility services, water planning and sets rates for customers. The PUD develops and operates facilities for the distribution of its utility services and can levy taxes against property within the district. * Terms listed are full terms. Some offices may be up for unexpired terms. Unexpired terms will be listed with candidate statements. 8
