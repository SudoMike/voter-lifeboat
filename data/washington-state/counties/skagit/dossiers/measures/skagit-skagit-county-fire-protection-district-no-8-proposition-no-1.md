## What it does

Restores the fire district's regular property-tax levy from about $0.89 to no more than $1.25 per $1,000 for collection in 2027, permits 3% annual growth for five years, and makes the final-year amount the base for later levy limits [S1]. Funds support fire and EMS operations, staffing, apparatus, and equipment [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The initial increase is approximately $0.36 per $1,000, or $180 annually on $500,000 assessed value [S1].
- The district says the measure would add two full-time firefighters and help replace engines and water tenders [S1].

## Arguments for

- Supporters cite about 1,100 annual emergencies, a 58% call increase since 2019, reduced volunteer availability, and dedicated use of every levy dollar for emergency response [S1].

## Arguments against

- No opposition statement was filed [S1].

## Axis notes

- The tradeoff is a permanent levy-base increase and above-default five-year growth versus staffing and equipment intended to address higher call volume [S1].
