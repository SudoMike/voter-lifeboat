## What it does

Raises District 304's regular property-tax levy from about $0.09 to $0.50 per $1,000 beginning in 2027, with the 2027 amount becoming the base for later levy limits [S1]. Revenue would support nutrition, child and family programs, youth services, mental health, senior care, prevention, and local health-service availability [S1][S2].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The increase is approximately $0.41 per $1,000; the district estimates about $17 per month on a $500,000 home [S2].
- The district says funds remain with District 304 rather than PeaceHealth and would reduce reliance on shrinking, restricted grants [S2].

## Arguments for

- Supporters argue stable local funding would preserve and expand prevention and wellness programs and give local residents more control over priorities [S1].

## Arguments against

- No opposition statement was filed [S1].

## Axis notes

- The choice pairs a lasting property-tax-base increase with stable, locally controlled health and prevention funding [S1][S2].
