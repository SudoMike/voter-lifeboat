## What it does

Replaces an expiring operations levy with a four-year levy collecting up to $950,000 annually from 2027 through 2030 for class-size reduction, nurses, counselors, mental-health specialists, arts, athletics, transportation, supplies, training, and other operations not fully state-funded [S1]. Estimated rates decline from $1.24 per $1,000 in 2027 to $1.02 in 2030 [S1].

## Arguments for

- Supporters call it a no-increase replacement needed for athletics, paraeducators, staff, transportation, maintenance, and community-center costs [S1].
- They say the levy collects a fixed amount, so rising property values do not increase district revenue [S1].

## Arguments against

- Opponents argue district salaries and benefits are unsustainable and academic results do not justify continued levy funding [S1].
- They say rejection would force prioritization, a sustainable budget, and attention to parent trust and reading outcomes [S1].

## Axis notes

- The central tradeoff is continuation of local school funding versus demands for spending restraint and measurable academic improvement [S1].
