Journey of
your ballot
King County Elections mails ballots to all registered 
voters around three weeks from Election Day.
You can return your ballot to an 
oﬃcial ballot drop box, 24/7, until 
Election Day at 8 p.m. sharp. If 
returning your ballot by mail, USPS 
recommends mailing it at least 
one week before Election Day.
What happens after we 
receive your ballot back at 
Elections Headquarters?
Your ballot 
envelope is sorted.
Our mail sorting machine scans 
your ballot envelope and sends 
an image of your signature to 
be veriﬁed.

Your signature 
is veriﬁed.
The signature on your voter 
registration record is compared to 
the image of the signature from 
your ballot envelope.
Right after 8 p.m. on Election 
Night, we tabulate results and 
post them on our website for 
all to see. Results are updated 
every weekday at 4 p.m. until 
the election is certiﬁed.
Your ballot 
is scanned.
Your ballot is scanned and an 
image is stored on a secure and 
closed system until 8 p.m. on 
Election Night.
Your ballot 
is counted.
If the signatures match, your 
ballot moves on to be opened.
If your signature is missing 
or doesn’t match your 
registration record, the 
ballot will be challenged. 
We will contact you to 
resolve the issue before 
the ballot is opened.
Track your ballot each step of the way!
Opt in at kce.wiki/BallotAlerts to receive texts or 
emails as your ballot is processed and counted.
