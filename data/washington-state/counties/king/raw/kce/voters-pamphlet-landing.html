Voters' pamphlet

Learn how we make and send out voters' pamphlets, how to get a copy online, and request alternate formats.

King County publishes a Local Voters’ Pamphlet that contains candidate and measure information. King County Elections does not edit or fact-check candidate or measure statements and is not responsible for their content.

View the Local Voters' Pamphlet online

There are three ways to access the pamphlet online:

  1. View a personalized voters' pamphlet on My Voter Information. The personalized pamphlet only contains candidates and measures that are on your ballot.
    Go to My Voter Information
  2. View or download a pdf of the Voters' Pamphlet
    • Edition 1 
    • Edition 2

  3. Visit the candidates and ballot measures page for a full list of measures and candidates in the upcoming election.
    • Ballot measures
    • Candidates

Voters' pamphlet FAQs

Candidates in the local voters’ pamphlet appear in the order they will appear on the ballot.

The candidates and measures on your ballot reflect the district in which you are registered to vote. The local voters’ pamphlet may cover multiple districts and could include candidates and measures outside of your district. You can create a personalized pamphlet with My Voter Information that will contain only the candidates and measures that are on your ballot.

Districts choosing to participate in a local voters’ pamphlet are responsible for appointing committee members who agree to write statements.

What is an explanatory statement?

A ballot measure explanatory statement is prepared by the district attorney; it outlines the effect the ballot measure would have if passed into law.

Alternate local voters' pamphlet formats

Alternate formats of the Local Voters’ Pamphlet (such as an audio edition) are available upon request. Contact us for more information.
