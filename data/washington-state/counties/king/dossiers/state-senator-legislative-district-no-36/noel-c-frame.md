## Background
Noel C. Frame (Prefers Democratic Party) is the incumbent State Senator for the 36th Legislative District, serving since 2023 after seven years as its State Representative (2016–2023) [S1][S2]. She is Vice Chair of Finance on the Senate Ways & Means Committee and previously chaired the House Finance Committee [S1][S2]. She is managing partner of the small consulting business Uncommon Bridges, a former nonprofit director, and a past foster parent/caregiver to extended family [S1]. Education: bachelor's in Political Communication and master's in Political Management, George Washington University [S1].

## Positions
- Tax reform: her central issue; describes Washington's tax code as "upside-down" and regressive; says she helped pass the Millionaires Tax to fund schools, healthcare, human services, and early learning [S1][S2].
- Affordability: cites expansion of the Working Families Tax Credit (460,000 new households), senior property tax exemption (50,000 new households), a low-income energy assistance program, and sales-tax exemptions on key household items [S1].
- Housing/homelessness, education, healthcare access, public safety, child welfare, and environment are listed platform areas; also a section on opposing Trump administration actions [S2].
- Equity: emphasizes systems reform in partnership with foster youth, people with disabilities, and communities of color; cites recognition for advocacy on developmental disabilities, behavioral health, and juvenile justice [S1][S2].

## Record
- Prime sponsor of Washington's proposed wealth tax since 2021 — SB 5486 (2023, 1% on financial assets above $250 million) and SB 5797 (2025, 1% on financial intangible assets above $50 million); dubbed the Legislature's "Revenue Queen" by Washington State Standard [S3][S4].
- Helped pass the state capital gains tax and the 2026 "Millionaires Tax" [S1][S2].
- Vice Chair of Finance, Senate Ways & Means; past co-chair, Children & Youth Behavioral Health Work Group [S1].

## Endorsements
Pamphlet lists: 36th District Democrats, Working Families Party, Washington State Labor Council AFL-CIO, Washington Conservation Action [S1].

## Scoring notes
- Ten-year incumbent legislator and the Legislature's most prominent advocate for progressive tax restructuring (wealth tax, millionaires tax) [S1][S3].
- Directly opposed by her Republican challenger on the race's defining axis: Frame authored the taxes England pledges to repeal [S1][S2].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
