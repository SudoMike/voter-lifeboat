# State Senator, Legislative District No. 36 — contest overview

- depth: light
- All candidates advance: yes (two candidates in a top-two primary; both proceed to the general regardless of primary result).

State senators serve four-year terms; the 36th LD covers northwest Seattle neighborhoods (Ballard, Magnolia, Queen Anne, Greenwood area). This is the clearest partisan contrast among the light races: incumbent Democrat Noel Frame, the Legislature's leading progressive-tax architect (wealth tax prime sponsor, Millionaires Tax), versus Republican party officer Jillian England, whose platform is built on repealing the Millionaires Tax and the Climate Commitment Act. Differentiating axes: tax policy (progressive restructuring vs. repeal/cut), climate/energy (CCA defense vs. repeal plus nuclear/gas expansion), and homelessness approach (services/housing investment vs. encampment clearing and accountability). In this heavily Democratic district the primary result is not competitive suspense; both candidates advance.
