## Background

Mia Jacobson is a longshoreman with a BA from the University of Washington [S1][S3]. She has run for office twice before in overlapping territory: she received 1.8% (472 votes) in the August 2023 primary for Seattle City Council District 1 and 3.1% (5,845 votes) in the August 2025 primary for Seattle City Council Position 9 At-Large, not advancing to the general election in either race [S4]. Her campaign website, voterabbit.com, has been used across these campaigns [S2][S4]. Ballotpedia identified no endorsements for her in either prior race [S4].

## Positions

- **Direct public participation in legislation:** Her central claim is that "our local legislative process has no place for public opinion" and that residents have "no measurable impact on decisions made by our representatives" [S1]. She argues representative government has been outgrown by technology: "If we can expect to access social media 24 hours a day, we can expect to engage with the legislative process whenever we want" [S1][S2].
- **Open public forums:** Pledges to introduce "an open public forum parallel to every committee to act as a standing check against bad policy" [S1][S2].
- **Interactive map:** Proposes an interactive map where residents "drop pins of interest" to identify issues and solutions that guide legislation [S1][S2].
- **Community empowerment:** Says she would "use government to empower those in the community already doing the work" [S1].
- Her pamphlet statement and website do not take positions on the county's substantive policy areas (housing, taxes, public safety, transit); her platform is procedural, about how decisions get made [S1][S2]. The Seattle Times editorial board similarly characterized her positions as focused on "the need to hear directly from constituents when it comes to decision-making" [S3].

## Record

- No elected or governmental record. Prior campaign results: eliminated in the 2023 Seattle District 1 primary (1.8%) and the 2025 Seattle Position 9 primary (3.1%) [S4].
- Campaign finance: no itemized 2026 contributions or expenditures appear under her name in PDC datasets as of 2026-07-16, consistent with minimal or mini-reporting-level campaign activity (interpretation; absence of records is not itself a PDC determination) [S5].

## Endorsements

None found. Her pamphlet statement and website list no endorsements [S1][S2], and Ballotpedia identified none in her prior races [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Runs on process rather than policy: direct-democracy mechanisms (parallel public forums, pin-drop maps) instead of positions on county issue areas — unlike both opponents [S1][S2][S3].
- Only candidate in the race with no governmental experience of any kind (elected or staff) [S1][S4].
