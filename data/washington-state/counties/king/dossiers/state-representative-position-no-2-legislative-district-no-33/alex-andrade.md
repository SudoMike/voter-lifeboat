## Background
Alex Andrade (Alejandra Parga-Andrade) is a Burien City Councilmember, elected in 2024 to a term running through December 2027 [S1][S3]. She is a small business owner — founder of Citlali Creativo, a Burien-based creative business started in 2008 — with 25+ years of experience in retail management, HR, budgeting, operations, and project management [S1][S3]. She describes herself as a Latina, a member of the LGBTQ+ community, and a mother of two; she grew up in Eastern Washington and has lived in King County since the late 1990s [S2][S3]. Her civic involvement includes four Sound Cities Association committees, the SOMOS Seattle board, the WA State Combined Fund Drive Advisory Council, and volunteer work in education, arts, LGBTQ+ advocacy, and animal welfare [S1]. She announced her challenge to Rep. Mia Gregerson in March 2026 [S3].

## Positions
- **Government accountability / spending:** says state spending "continues to grow without enough accountability" and pledges to demand transparency in how taxpayer dollars are spent [S1].
- **Public safety and behavioral health:** supports co-response teams pairing first responders with behavioral health professionals, and "responsible policies to address large unmanaged encampments" alongside pathways to stability and recovery [S2].
- **Housing / homelessness:** favors combining housing expansion with treatment and recovery programs emphasizing "long-term stability, recovery, and accountability" [S2].
- **Economy / small business:** cites her dual perspective as business owner and councilmember; has supported minimum wage increases while acknowledging operational pressures on employers [S2].
- **Education:** emphasizes strengthening K–12, expanding early learning and childcare, and supporting multiple career pathways [S2].
- **Local control:** opposes unfunded state mandates on cities; argues cities should be able to make decisions reflecting their unique needs [S2].
- **Social policy:** pamphlet statement lists support for "pro-choice protections" and equality [S1].

## Record
- Burien City Councilmember, 2024–present [S1]. No specific council votes were documented in the sources reviewed.

## Endorsements
