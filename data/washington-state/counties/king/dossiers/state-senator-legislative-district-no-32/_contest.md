## What the office does

A Washington State Senator is one of 49 members of the upper chamber of the state legislature, elected to a four-year term (versus two-year terms for the district's two State Representatives). Senators write and vote on state law, adopt the state operating, capital, and transportation budgets, set state tax policy, and confirm gubernatorial appointments. Committee assignments matter: chairs control which bills advance. The incumbent here chairs the Senate Local Government Committee; the challenger chairs a House committee she would give up by switching chambers [S1].

## District and dynamics

The 32nd District covers Shoreline, Mountlake Terrace, Lynnwood, parts of Edmonds, and a small slice of northern Seattle, straddling the King/Snohomish county line; it is heavily Democratic [S1][S2]. This is the marquee dynamic of the race: two sitting Democratic legislators are running against each other. Rep. Cindy Ryu, in the House since 2011, is challenging two-term incumbent Sen. Jesse Salomon, saying the colleague she once endorsed is "too moderate" for the district; Salomon counters that he is a "pragmatic progressive" [S2]. Republican Ira McBee, a first-time candidate, runs on partisan balance [S1][S4]. With three candidates in a top-two primary, the most likely general-election scenarios are Democrat-vs-Democrat or one Democrat vs. McBee. Fundraising between the two Democrats is nearly even (Ryu $167k, Salomon $141k plus carryover, as of July 8); McBee has under $8k [S1]. Notably, the 32nd District Democrats organization endorsed the challenger Ryu over the incumbent [S3].

## Differentiating issue axes

- **Intra-party ideology (the central axis):** Ryu positions herself to Salomon's left ("too moderate"); Salomon embraces pragmatism — e.g., auditing homelessness spending rather than increasing it [S1][S2].
- **Housing approach:** Ryu emphasizes mixed-income/social housing, permitting streamlining, and modular construction; Salomon emphasizes supply expansion, permitting red-tape cuts through his committee, and senior property-tax relief [S1].
- **Technology policy:** Ryu wants aggressive action on AI regulation, a state privacy law, and opposes data-center subsidies; Salomon has no stated position on data-center subsidies and focuses on algorithmic-pricing regulation [S1].
- **Effectiveness/experience claims:** Salomon points to bills passed every session and committee chairmanship; Ryu points to 16 years of House seniority and argues her committee's bills die in the Senate [S1].
- **Party balance vs. one-party control:** McBee's axis against both Democrats — less spending and regulation, parental involvement in education, restoring "balance" [S1][S4].
