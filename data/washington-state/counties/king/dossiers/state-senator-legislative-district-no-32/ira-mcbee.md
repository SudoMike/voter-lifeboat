## Background

Ira McBee is a Republican first-time candidate for State Senate in the 32nd District with no prior elected experience [S1][S3]. He describes himself as an entrepreneur and international humanitarian worker with experience in twenty countries including conflict zones; he is founder and executive director of the Frontier Discovery Project and Inspire Arts Project and has owned and operated Nova McBee LLC since 2018 [S1]. He holds a BA from Boise Bible College with continuing study in public policy, economics, and community development [S1]. He is a longtime Lynnwood/Edmonds resident, active for over twenty years in church ministry, homeschool-cooperative instruction, and youth mentoring [S1][S2]. He frames his candidacy as providing "balance" against one-party control in a heavily Democratic district [S1][S3].

## Positions

- **Affordability:** Attributes rising costs to regulatory burdens and government spending rather than revenue shortfalls; wants "real affordability — not just programs for those who qualify" (says he will) [S2][S3].
- **Budget:** Supports "responsible budgeting" and reforming spending inefficiency [S1][S3].
- **Public safety:** Wants to restore safety on transit and in public spaces; supports rehabilitation for first-time offenders while strengthening penalties for repeat offenders [S2][S3].
- **Education:** Supports parental involvement, transparency, measurable outcomes over bureaucracy, and expanded pathways into skilled trades and vocational training [S1][S2].
- **Government accountability:** Wants a legislature "more responsive to voters than insulated political interests" [S2].

## Endorsements

No endorsements were listed on his campaign website at the time of access; the pamphlet statement directs readers to iramcbee.com for endorsements [S1][S2].

## Financials

PDC records show 38 contributions totaling about $7,792 for the 2026 campaign — roughly one-twentieth of what either Democratic opponent has raised [S3][S4].

## Scoring notes

- The only Republican in a race otherwise defined by an intra-Democratic contest; runs on partisan balance and one-party-control critique rather than a specific legislative agenda [S1][S3].
- No governing or legislative record; his case rests on nonprofit/small-business and international humanitarian experience [S1].
- Fundraising is minimal relative to both Democratic opponents [S3][S4].
- Distinctive public-safety stance in this field: rehabilitation for first-time offenders paired with tougher repeat-offender penalties [S3].
