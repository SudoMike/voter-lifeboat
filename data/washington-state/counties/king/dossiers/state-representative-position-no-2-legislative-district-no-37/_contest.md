## What this office does

One of two House seats for LD 37 (southeast Seattle, Skyway, part of
Renton); votes on state law, budget, and taxes.

## Race dynamics

Open seat: the incumbent, Rep. Chipalo Street, is running for the
district's State Senate seat instead. Two candidates are on the primary
ballot and both advance to the general regardless of the primary result, so
research depth is light.

- **Jaelynn Scott** (Prefers Democratic Party) — Executive Director of the
  Lavender Rights Project; heavily endorsed by Democratic elected
  officials; would be the first openly transgender state legislator in
  Washington.
- **Evon McCorkle** (Prefers Independent Party) — college student and
  first-time candidate; campaign website unreachable and no substantive
  news coverage found (pamphlet-only evidence).

## Differentiating axes

- Organizational leadership and coalition track record (Scott's $16M
  jail-to-housing redirection claim, nonprofit executive experience) vs. a
  student outsider candidacy (McCorkle).
- Party preference: Democratic vs. Independent.
- Depth of institutional support: Scott has extensive endorsements;
  McCorkle states he is "not backed by powerful insiders."
