## Background

Jamie Pedersen is the incumbent State Senator for the 43rd Legislative District and has served as Senate Democratic Majority Leader since 2025 (elected to that role in late 2024) [S1][S3][S6]. He was elected to the State House in 2006, served there 2007–2013, was appointed to the Senate in December 2013, and was elected to the Senate in 2014, 2018, and 2022 [S1][S3]. He is Executive Vice President and General Counsel at McKinstry, a Seattle-based engineering and construction firm, since 2012, and previously practiced law at K&L Gates (formerly Preston Gates & Ellis) for 17 years [S1][S3]. He grew up in Puyallup and graduated from Yale College and Yale Law School [S1][S3]. He serves on the Ways & Means and Rules committees and previously chaired the Senate Law & Justice Committee for four years [S3]. He and his husband, a retired teacher, have four sons in public schools and live on Capitol Hill [S1]. He was the lead volunteer attorney for Lambda Legal in Washington's marriage equality case [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes:** Describes himself as the architect of the state's new "Millionaires Tax" — a 9.9% tax on household income over $1 million, signed by Governor Ferguson — alongside a capital gains tax, paired with tax cuts for working families and small businesses [S1][S2][S3][S6]. Told the Seattle Times he wanted to use some of the new revenue to reduce property taxes, but House leadership disagreed [S5]. Per The Urbanist, he has expressed support for rolling back the estate tax increase passed in the prior session and opposes seatmate Rep. Shaun Scott's "Well(th) Washington Fund" payroll-tax proposal as not politically feasible [S7].
- **Schools:** Says he will prioritize "amply funding" public schools; claims over $1 billion in new public school funding, plus funding for universal school lunches [S1][S2]. Says his personal preference would be to "rebalance toward more investment in public schools" over backfilling federal cuts to other programs [S5].
- **Immigration / civil liberties:** Cites legislation banning ICE agents from hiding behind masks, restricting camera surveillance for immigration enforcement, and protecting sensitive voter information [S1][S2].
- **LGBTQ+ rights:** Long record leading on marriage equality, LGBTQ+ civil rights, and transgender privacy protections [S1][S2].
- **Housing / transit:** Committed to tackling housing affordability and building the full voter-approved light rail system; cites past work on renter protections [S1].
- **Reproductive rights:** Cites restoring state money to Planned Parenthood after federal cuts [S1][S2].
- **Climate / guns:** Cites climate action legislation, paid family leave, and gun violence prevention among accomplishments [S1][S2].

## Record

- Elected to the House in 2006; Senate since December 2013; Senate Majority Leader since the 2025 session [S1][S3][S6].
- Main architect and lead sponsor of the 2026 income tax on high earners (9.9% on household income above $1 million), the state's first income tax of this kind [S6][S7].
- Led/championed marriage equality legislation and LGBTQ+ civil rights bills; known also for supporting marijuana legalization and state gun-control measures [S1][S7].
- Ran unopposed in 2022, winning 98% of the general-election vote [S6].
- Campaign finance: approximately $387,840 in reported contributions across 828 records for the 2026 election cycle, per PDC data — roughly four times his nearest opponent [S4]; the Washington State Standard reported about $345,000 raised as of late June 2026 [S6].

## Endorsements

- Seattle Times editorial board (primary endorsement: "Pedersen has earned another term") [S5].
- Labor unions including SEIU 775, SEIU Healthcare 1199NW, Teamsters 117, SEIU 925, and UFCW 3000, per his campaign site [S2]; pamphlet cites labor unions, Sierra Club, Planned Parenthood Alliance Advocates, and every Democratic State Senator [S1].
- Notable endorsements he did not receive: the 43rd District Democrats and Seattle Mayor Katie Wilson both endorsed challenger Hannah Sabio-Howell [S6].

## Scoring notes

- Incumbent and Senate Majority Leader — the race's central question is whether his insider power and incremental deal-making record serves the district better than a challenger's movement-style progressivism [S5][S6][S7].
- His signature achievement (the millionaires income tax) is simultaneously attacked from the left as too incremental (delayed revenue, embedded corporate tax break later removed by the House) and from the right/center as part of historic overspending [S5][S7].
- Distinguishing positions vs. Sabio-Howell: supports rolling back the estate tax increase, opposes the Well Washington Fund payroll tax, and emphasizes political feasibility over maximal revenue [S7].
- Corporate ties are a live issue: he is general counsel of a large construction/engineering firm and accepts the kind of PAC money his main opponent refuses [S3][S7]; his fundraising lead is roughly 4:1 [S4][S6].
- Loss of Seattle's clout in Olympia if the Majority Leader is unseated is an argument made on his behalf by the Seattle Times [S5].
