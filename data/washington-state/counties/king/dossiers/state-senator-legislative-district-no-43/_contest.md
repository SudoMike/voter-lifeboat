## What the office does

A Washington State Senator serves a four-year term in the 49-member upper chamber of the Legislature, voting on the state operating/capital/transportation budgets, taxes, and all state law — K-12 funding, housing and landlord-tenant law, healthcare, transit funding, public safety, and civil rights. Senators also shape legislation through committee assignments; the incumbent here is additionally the Senate Majority Leader, controlling the majority caucus's agenda [S2][S4].

## The district and race dynamics

The 43rd is entirely within Seattle — Capitol Hill, the University District, Fremont/Wallingford, Belltown, South Lake Union, Eastlake, Montlake, Madison Park, Laurelhurst, and the downtown core — the densest legislative district in the state, where roughly three in four residents rent [S2][S3][S5]. It is routinely described as the most progressive district in Washington [S2][S5].

This is a Democrat-on-Democrat challenge from the left and one of the most closely watched legislative races of the cycle: Senate Majority Leader Jamie Pedersen (in the Legislature since 2007, unopposed with 98% in 2022) faces first-time candidate and labor communicator Hannah Sabio-Howell, plus scientist Heather-Marie Wilson, who states no party preference, has no reported fundraising, and is not treated as competitive in coverage [S2][S5]. The August 4 top-two primary will almost certainly send both Democrats to November [S2]. Notable dynamics: the 43rd District Democrats and Seattle Mayor Katie Wilson endorsed the challenger over the sitting Majority Leader, while the Seattle Times endorsed Pedersen; commentators link the insurgency to the national post-Mamdani intra-Democratic fight [S2][S4]. Pedersen holds roughly a 4:1 fundraising advantage (~$345–388K vs ~$81–95K) [S2].

## Differentiating issue axes

- **Scope of tax progressivity:** Pedersen's signature is the new 9.9% millionaires income tax and capital gains tax, with openness to estate-tax rollback and opposition to a new payroll/wealth tax; Sabio-Howell treats the millionaires tax as a floor and wants a wealth tax and the Well Washington Fund with no rollbacks; Wilson opposes the recent tax increases outright as driving out jobs and wealth [S1][S3][S4].
- **Insider effectiveness vs. movement change:** whether the district benefits more from having the Majority Leader's seat at the table, or from a new-generation organizer refusing corporate PAC money — the sharpest framing contrast in the race [S2][S3][S4].
- **Housing/renter policy intensity:** both Democrats back building more housing, but they diverge on rent stabilization stringency, statewide social housing, and speed of upzoning; the challenger's renter identity vs. the incumbent's homeowner/corporate-counsel profile is an explicit theme [S3][S5].
- **Police accountability and ICE enforcement:** Sabio-Howell wants to restore rolled-back use-of-force/pursuit standards and go further on constraining ICE; Pedersen points to enacted anti-ICE-masking and surveillance limits [S1][S3].
- **Experience and governing style:** 20 years of legislative record and coalition deal-making versus no elected experience and an explicitly confrontational posture toward corporate interests [S4][S5].
