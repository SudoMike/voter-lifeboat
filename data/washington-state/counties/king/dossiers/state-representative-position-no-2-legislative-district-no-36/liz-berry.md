## Background
Liz Berry (Prefers Democratic Party) is the incumbent State Representative for the 36th Legislative District, Position 2, serving since 2021, and chairs the House Labor Committee [S1][S2]. She previously was executive director of a large regional nonprofit protecting the rights of consumers, workers, and patients, and served as Legislative Director for Congresswoman Gabby Giffords, working on policies for military families and clean energy [S1]. She is a past board member of Pro-Choice Washington, serves on the ArtsFund board, and is a PTA parent of two Seattle Public Schools students [S1]. Education: BA, American University; Executive Certificate in Public Leadership, Harvard [S1].

## Positions
- Gun safety: her signature issue; passed legislation requiring a permit to purchase a firearm [S1] (see Record).
- Labor: as Labor Committee Chair, says she has protected workers from wage theft and unfair noncompete agreements while supporting union jobs [S1].
- Taxes/affordability: supported tax cuts on everyday essentials while "making our tax system fairer" to fund schools, childcare, housing, and health care [S1].
- Climate/environment: leading on reducing plastic waste and expanding recycling [S1].
- Transportation: supports the Ballard Light Rail project [S2].
- Reproductive rights: lifelong reproductive-justice advocate; has worked to protect and expand abortion access [S1].

## Record
- Prime sponsor of HB 1163 (2025), Washington's permit-to-purchase firearm law: five-year permits through the Washington State Patrol with safety-training requirements, signed by Gov. Ferguson in May 2025, effective May 2027 [S3].
- Chairs the House Labor Committee; pamphlet cites wage-theft and noncompete legislation [S1].
- Participated in the 2026 House floor debate on the tax-code rebalancing package, which she calls "a historic step" [S2].

## Endorsements
Website says she is endorsed by "hundreds of elected leaders and community members," naming Rep. Pramila Jayapal, Gov. Bob Ferguson, and AG Nick Brown [S2].

## Scoring notes
- Sole candidate on the primary ballot; advances to the general regardless of result.
- Third-term incumbent with a concrete legislative signature: the 2025 permit-to-purchase gun law (HB 1163) [S3].
- Committee leadership (Labor Chair) anchors a labor-rights-focused record [S1].
