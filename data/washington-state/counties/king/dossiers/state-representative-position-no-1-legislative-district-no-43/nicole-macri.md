## Background

Incumbent State Representative for LD 43 since 2017 [S1]. Deputy Director
at DESC (Downtown Emergency Service Center), helping oversee a $150 million
budget for supportive housing, integrated healthcare, and social services
for people who have experienced homelessness [S1]. Past president of the
Washington Low Income Housing Alliance [S1]. MPA, UW Evans School; BA in
sociology, Rutgers [S1]. Received the Human Right to Housing Award from the
National Homelessness Law Center [S1].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Championed laws to prevent rent gouging; 2026 campaign
  focuses on increasing renter protections [S1][S3].
- **Taxes:** Cites "historic tax fairness" measures funding schools and
  childcare, lowering costs for working families and seniors, and cutting
  small business taxes [S1].
- **Healthcare:** Making healthcare affordable; expanding access to
  affordable health insurance [S1][S3].
- **Climate:** Tackling climate change; climate-resilient infrastructure
  [S1][S3].
- **Public safety/civil rights:** Work on gun violence reduction, the
  fentanyl crisis, and protecting immigrant and LGBTQ+ residents from
  federal overreach; states she is voting no on all statewide initiatives
  on this ballot [S1].

## Record

- Lead campaigner for HB 1217, the rent stabilization bill limiting
  excessive rent increases [S3].
- Described in local coverage as a "housing champ"; has drawn criticism
  from some progressives for favoring achievable incremental housing gains
  over further-reaching interventions given Senate opposition [S3].
