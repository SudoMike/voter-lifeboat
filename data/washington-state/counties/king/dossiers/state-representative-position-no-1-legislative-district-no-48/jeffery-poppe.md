## Background

Retired CPA and financial systems consultant with 40 years of experience
(prefers Republican Party); work included auditing a billion-dollar bank fraud
for the FDIC, audits for the LA County Grand Jury, and consulting for Fortune
500 companies, the federal government, California, and large California
counties [S1][S2]. Former director of the San Diego Taxpayers Association and
a homeowner association; delegate to Washington and national Republican
conventions [S1]. BS from University of Cincinnati (magna cum laude); MBA in
Finance from San Diego State University [S1]. Has lived in the 48th District
for 16 years [S2]. No prior elected public office.

## Positions

- No new taxes: says Washington "has a spending problem, not an income
  problem" and pledges to reject new taxes [S1][S2].
- Fraud and efficiency: pledges to spearhead fraud audits and push for more
  effective, efficient government [S1][S2].
- Campaign finance: pledges to accept only individual contributions, no PAC or
  union money [S1][S2].
- Transparency: wants state spending and performance metrics publicly
  accessible [S2].

## Endorsements

King County Republican Party; 48th Legislative District Republicans and GOP
precinct committee officers (per candidate's own site) [S2].

## Scoring notes

- Both candidates advance to the general regardless of primary outcome.
- Only Republican in the race; platform is almost entirely fiscal
  (taxes, audits, transparency) rather than a broad policy agenda.
- First-time state candidate with an auditing/finance professional background;
  no legislative record.
