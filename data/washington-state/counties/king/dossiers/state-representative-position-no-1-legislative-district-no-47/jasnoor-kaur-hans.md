## Background

Jasnoor Kaur Hans is a Democrat and political newcomer running her first race [S4]. She grew up in Kent, graduated valedictorian from Kentridge High School, attended Green River Community College and the University of Washington (B.A. in Political Science and Law, Societies, and Justice), and earned a Master of Legal Studies from Seattle University School of Law, where she served as the Master of Legal Studies representative in the Student Bar Association [S1][S2]. She works in family law practice and legal advocacy, has been an English-language tutor, and was a substitute paraeducator in the Kent School District supporting special-education and English-language-learner students [S1][S2]. Her community work includes government relations and legislative advocacy with the Washington Autism Alliance, immigration case support for Kids in Need of Defense, and independent human-rights research and public academic presentations on the Sikh genocide and international accountability [S1].

## Positions

- **Housing:** Expand the state Housing Trust Fund, fund right-to-counsel for tenants facing eviction, and support community land trusts [S2].
- **Schools:** Equitable funding within districts, more counselors and mental-health staff, and lower school bond passage thresholds [S2].
- **Public safety:** Address root causes through stable housing, mental-health services, and youth programs rather than increased sentencing [S2].
- **Healthcare:** Lower out-of-pocket costs, hold insurance and hospital pricing accountable, and expand community mental-health care [S2].
- **Rights:** Defend civil rights, reproductive freedom, immigrant protections, language access in state services, and due process [S2].
- **Affordability/taxes:** Says she will lower costs and make the tax system fairer, strengthen good-paying jobs, and ensure artificial intelligence creates opportunity rather than uncertainty [S1].
- Frames the race against "career politicians" who do "the minimum" while working families struggle [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Local-roots narrative (Kent upbringing, Kent School District paraeducator) mirrors the incumbent's South King County ties while arguing for generational change [S1][S2].
