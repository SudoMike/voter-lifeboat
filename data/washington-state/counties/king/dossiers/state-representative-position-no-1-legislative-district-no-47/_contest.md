## What the office does

A Washington State Representative is one of two House members (Position 1 and Position 2, both elected district-wide) for each of the state's 49 legislative districts, serving two-year terms in the 98-member House. Representatives write and vote on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets; core state responsibilities include K-12 school funding (the state's "paramount duty"), housing and land-use law, the tax code, public safety and policing standards, healthcare programs, and higher education. The legislature is part-time (105-day sessions in odd years, 60-day in even years).

## Race dynamics

- Four candidates: incumbent Democrat Debra Entenman (in the seat since 2018; re-elected 2024 with 56%), Democratic newcomer Jasnoor Kaur Hans, "Cascade Democrat" Logan Evans (lost a 2025 Kent City Council primary), and Libertarian Cobi Clark (lost a 2025 Auburn council race) [S1][S3]. No Republican filed; Clark is endorsed by the 47th District Republicans, positioning him as the de facto right-of-center option [see cobi-clark.md S2].
- Top two advance from the August 4, 2026 primary to the November 3 general [S3].
- Fundraising is modest and unusually inverted: challenger Hans leads (~$37.0K), then Evans (~$20.3K) and Clark (~$19.5K), with incumbent Entenman last (~$16.2K) as of mid-July 2026 [S2].
- The 47th is one of King County's competitive swing districts, with contested races up and down the ballot this cycle (Kauffman vs. Soltys for Senate; a Stearns-Cooke rematch for Position 2) [S3].

## Differentiating issue axes

1. **Public safety and addiction policy — the sharpest split:** Entenman's record pairs new funding (HB 2015, $100M) with police-accountability measures; Hans explicitly rejects sentencing increases in favor of root causes; Evans proposes a three-strikes policy with six months of mandatory rehab; Clark emphasizes civil liberties alongside safety [S1].
2. **Housing approach:** supply-side deregulation from developer experience (Clark) vs. public tools — Housing Trust Fund, tenant right-to-counsel, land trusts (Hans) vs. high-rise transit-oriented development (Evans) vs. incumbent's Housing Committee seat and safety-net emphasis (Entenman) [S1].
3. **Tax philosophy:** Clark opposes any state income tax and runs on fiscal restraint; Evans stresses efficiency of existing dollars; Hans wants a "fairer" tax system; Entenman sponsored a local-option tax for public safety [S1].
4. **Incumbency vs. change:** Entenman is the only candidate with a legislative record and committee seniority; Hans and Evans both run explicitly against "career politicians," from different angles (progressive newcomer vs. results-first centrist) [S1][S3].
5. **Party identity:** a Democrat, a self-styled "Cascade Democrat," and a Libertarian backed by local Republicans give primary voters an unusually wide label spectrum for a nominally Democratic-leaning seat [S1][S3].
