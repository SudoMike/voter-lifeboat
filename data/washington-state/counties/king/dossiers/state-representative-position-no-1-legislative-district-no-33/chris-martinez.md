## Background

Chris Martinez is a Republican candidate for State Representative, 33rd District, Position 1 [S1]. He is an appointed Republican precinct committee officer with no prior elected office [S1][S2]. He describes himself as a lifelong blue-collar worker — automotive technician, industrial painter, handyman, and laborer — who graduated high school in 2010 and earned a computer-based accounting certificate in 2012 [S1]. His campaign site recounts a troubled youth including juvenile incarceration and jail time as an adult, followed by a religious conversion and marriage in 2019, after which he became a homeowner and paid off his debts [S2]. He emphasizes charitable giving and tithing as his community service [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes/spending:** Wants to reduce wasteful spending and regulation and lower taxes, especially business and occupation (B&O) taxes (says he will) [S2].
- **Energy/climate:** Would work to eliminate the Climate Commitment Act, which he blames for high energy costs; pamphlet pledges to "abolish energy-killing taxes" [S1][S2].
- **Public safety:** Supports increased law-enforcement presence and holding criminals accountable; opposed SB 5974 concerning elected sheriffs [S1][S2].
- **Constitutional rights:** Emphasizes Second Amendment rights, religious liberty, and First Amendment protections; opposes HB 2165, SB 5925, and HB 2320, which he characterizes as constitutional violations [S1][S2].
- **Education/family:** Opposes what he calls "woke ideologies" in schools without parental consent; supports parental rights and "traditional family values" [S1][S2].
- **Framing:** Describes officials in Olympia as "Marxist and Socialist disguised as Democrats" and calls for replacing career politicians with limited-government leaders [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

Endorsed by the King County Republican Party, per his pamphlet statement and the KCGOP logo on his campaign site [S1][S2].

## Financials

PDC records show 31 contributions totaling about $5,535 for the 2026 campaign [S3].

## Scoring notes

- The most detailed conservative platform in the race: names specific legislation he opposes (SB 5974, HB 2165, SB 5925, HB 2320) and targets the Climate Commitment Act for repeal [S2].
- Combines fiscal restraint with explicit social conservatism (parental rights, traditional family values, religious framing), unlike fellow Republican Darryl Jones's economics-only message [S1][S2].
- Holds the only party-organization endorsement (King County GOP) among the two Republicans in the race [S1].
- First-time candidate with no legislative record, facing an incumbent with passed bills; fundraising is modest (~$5.5k) but he is the only challenger with any itemized PDC filings [S3].
