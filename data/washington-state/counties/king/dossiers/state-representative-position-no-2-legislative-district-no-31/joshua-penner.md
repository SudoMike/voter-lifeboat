## Background
Joshua Penner (R) is the incumbent first-term State Representative, serving since January 2025, and sits as Assistant Ranking Member on Appropriations plus the Finance and Early Learning & Human Services committees [S1][S3]. He was Mayor of Orting for 8 years and an Orting councilmember for 6 years, and formerly chaired the Pierce County Regional Council [S1]. He is a Marine Corps Iraq War veteran (radio operator), works in executive strategy at Penner & Co, and co-founded PNW Vets, a large regional veteran support network [S1]. Harvard University ALM; BA St. Martin's University [S1].

## Positions
- **Affordability/accountability:** pledges to keep fighting to lower living costs and "demand accountability"; campaign tagline is "Pragmatic Leadership. Compassionate Outcomes. Real Accountability." [S1][S2].
- **Child welfare:** led efforts to modify the "Keeping Families Together Act," arguing its removal standards prevented caseworker intervention in dangerous situations [S2].
- **Disability care:** opposed closure proposals affecting Residential Habilitation Centers (SB 5167); pamphlet cites saving Buckley's Rainier School [S1][S2].
- **Transit oversight:** challenged Sound Transit-related legislation (SB 6309) on zoning bypass and financial accountability grounds [S2].

## Record
- Pamphlet cites saving Buckley's Rainier School, preventing early-education cuts, protecting care facilities for fentanyl-exposed newborns, and cutting red tape to fund White River and Fairfax bridge repairs [S1].

## Endorsements
- His campaign site cites a Tacoma News Tribune editorial endorsement from his 2024 run; no 2026 endorsement list was posted as of 2026-07-16 [S2].
