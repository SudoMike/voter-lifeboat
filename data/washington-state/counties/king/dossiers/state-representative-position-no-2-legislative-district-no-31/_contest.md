## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected from each legislative district for a two-year term, voting on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets.

## The race
Depth: light. Two candidates filed, so **both advance from the August 4, 2026 top-two primary to the November general election** [S1]:

- **Joshua Penner (R, incumbent)** — first-term state representative, former Orting mayor (8 years), Marine Corps Iraq veteran [S1].
- **John Bielka (D)** — first-time candidate, civil engineer and public works director with 30+ years of infrastructure and budget experience, cattle farmer near Orting [S1].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Elected experience vs. technical/professional experience** — Penner offers a mayoral and legislative record; Bielka offers hands-on infrastructure and budget management as a public works director [S1].
2. **Party** — Republican incumbent vs. a Democrat who campaigns on working "across the aisle" and Second Amendment support (an atypically moderate profile) [S1].
3. **Accountability framing** — both stress accountability: Penner via oversight of state agencies and programs; Bielka via transparent budgeting and "results, not rhetoric" [S1].
4. **Issue overlap** — both emphasize affordability, infrastructure, public safety, and schools, making experience and party the sharper contrasts [S1].
