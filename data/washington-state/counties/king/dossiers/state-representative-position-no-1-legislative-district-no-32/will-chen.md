## Background

Will Chen (prefers Democratic Party) is an Edmonds City Councilmember, first elected in 2021 and now in his second term [S1]. He is a licensed CPA, retired small business owner, and former Fortune 500 auditor with experience in auditing, budgeting, strategic planning, and compliance [S1]. He immigrated from China roughly 30 years ago, holds accounting degrees from the University of Iowa and an MBA from Western Washington University, and built an accounting firm serving local and international clients [S1][S2][S3]. On the council he has served as Council President Pro Tem and Finance Committee Chair [S2]. He co-founded the Asian Service Center serving South Snohomish County, sits on the Lynnwood Food Bank board, is a former president of the Seattle Bing Kung Family Association, and created the Second Chance Scholarship at Edmonds College [S1][S3].

## Positions

- **Affordability**: calls affordability "the defining challenge facing families"; says working families, seniors, and young people are being priced out and pledges to fight for affordable communities [S1][S2].
- **Taxes/fiscal**: told KUOW he wants to remove the 1% property tax cap so revenue can match inflation and cost-of-living increases; describes himself as a social liberal who is financially "careful" [S4].
- **Housing/homelessness**: supports housing and homelessness solutions that combine affordable housing with wraparound services [S3].
- **Public safety and community health**: names public safety as a core priority [S1][S2].
- **Education/workforce**: emphasizes strong public schools and workforce development pathways [S2].
- **Environment**: supports environmental protection including tree protections [S3].
- **Governance**: pledges "responsible government that works for every part of our district," with emphasis on fiscal responsibility [S1][S2].

## Record

- Elected to Edmonds City Council in 2021; re-elected November 2025 with 88.3% of the vote [S1][S6].
- Chaired the council's Finance Committee; during Edmonds' 2024 budget difficulties he brought forward cost-reduction proposals, including reducing city hall operations to 32 hours per week and restructuring police department leadership [S6, per Herald coverage of the finance committee].
- Campaign fundraising: $52,361 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, fourth among the six candidates [S5].

## Endorsements

- Over 15 endorsers listed on his site, including former U.S. Representative Brian Baird, Snohomish County Councilmember Jared Mead, and mayors/councilmembers from Edmonds, Lynnwood, Mountlake Terrace, Mukilteo, Newcastle, Monroe, Bellevue, Renton, and Mercer Island [S2].
- The Herald lists Mountlake Terrace Mayor Steve Woodard, Lynnwood Mayor George Hurst, and Snohomish County Councilmember Jared Mead [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the field with a CPA/auditing background; leads with fiscal-management credentials rather than legal or advocacy experience [S1][S3].
- One of three sitting city councilmembers in the race (with Nand and Scully), with a specific finance-committee record in Edmonds [S2][S6].
- Willing to name a specific revenue change (lifting the 1% property tax cap) while framing himself as fiscally cautious [S4].
- Endorsements skew toward local mayors and councilmembers rather than legislators, labor, or newspapers [S2][S3].
