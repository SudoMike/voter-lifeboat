## Background

Lisa Rezac (prefers Republican Party) is the only non-Democratic candidate in this six-way primary [S1][S3]. She was born and raised in Edmonds [S2][S3]. She served as chair of the LD32 Republican Party (2023–2025), vice chair of the King County Republican Party (2025–2026), and precinct committee captain (2024–present) [S1]. Professionally she is a fundraising auctioneer who says she has raised over $1 million for nonprofits, schools, and community organizations and led over 150 charitable team-building events [S1]. She holds a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Washington and completed Columbia Business School's Leader as Coach program [S1]. Her pamphlet describes her as "a former voting Democrat of 25 years" [S1]. She ran for this same seat in 2024, losing to incumbent Cindy Ryu with about 31% of the vote [S3].

## Positions

- **Taxes/affordability**: campaign slogan "Less Waste. Lower Taxes. Better Results."; opposes new taxes and fee increases and advocates tax relief [S2][S3].
- **Government accountability**: pledges to expose "waste, fraud, and mismanagement of taxpayer dollars"; supports independent audits of major state programs, stronger fraud detection, and "zero tolerance for misuse of taxpayer funds" [S1][S2][S3].
- **Education**: campaigns on "restoring excellence in education" and parental rights in education [S1][S3].
- **Public safety**: prioritizes safer communities and support for law enforcement [S1][S3].
- **Regulation**: supports reducing regulation [S3].
- **One-party control**: argues one-party control in Olympia "is failing the people it serves" [S1].

## Record

- No elective office record; her leadership roles have been within Republican party organizations [S1].
- 2024 general election: lost to Rep. Cindy Ryu in this district with roughly 31% of the vote [S3].
- Campaign fundraising: $4,058 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, the least of any candidate in this race [S4].

## Endorsements

- Her pamphlet points to electlisarezac.com for endorsements, but no endorsements were listed on the site when accessed [S1][S2]. The Herald likewise found none listed and reported she did not respond to interview requests [S3].

## Scoring notes

- The only Republican in the race; in a district where she took about 31% against Ryu in 2024, she is positioned differently from the five Democrats rather than competing within their lane [S1][S3].
- Platform is organized around taxpayer accountability, lower taxes, and parental rights, in contrast to the Democratic candidates' revenue-and-services platforms [S1][S2][S3].
- Minimal fundraising ($4,058) compared with $33,000–$115,000 for the Democratic candidates [S4].
- Did not respond to the Herald's interview requests, so less independent coverage of her positions exists than for opponents [S3].
