## Background

Jenna Nand (prefers Democratic Party) is an Edmonds City Councilmember (2022–present) and transactional attorney who founded the business law firm Fortuna Law PLLC in 2015 [S1][S4]. She was appointed to the council in September 2022 to fill the vacancy left by Laura Johnson's resignation, after multiple prior applications, and describes herself as the first woman of color elected to the Edmonds City Council [S1][S4][S5]. She grew up in Edmonds and Shoreline in a union household, raised by immigrant parents from Fiji who worked as public school teachers; she is a graduate of Edmonds College, the University of Washington, and the University of Minnesota Law School (JD, magna cum laude) [S1][S2]. Her community service includes pro bono work with the Northwest Immigrant Rights Project, board service with the 32nd District Democrats and the Edmonds Chamber of Commerce, the Edmonds Mayor's Climate Protection Committee, and Bunanza Rabbit Rescue [S1]. She spent about a decade as a caregiver for her terminally ill father and is currently undergoing treatment for breast cancer [S2][S4].

## Positions

- **Taxes**: supports repealing the 1% property tax cap and has floated a flat state income tax; calls for "progressive revenue" to fund public safety and affordable housing [S2][S3].
- **Housing**: supports affordable homeownership, including state land trusts [S1][S3].
- **Healthcare**: pledges to protect access to Apple Health (Medicaid) and state health programs from federal threats [S2].
- **Education**: supports statewide school funding, lowering tuition, and subsidized college costs; proposes funding community college job training [S1][S2][S3].
- **Homelessness**: argues the crisis requires dedicated funding for social services, saying "building more housing is not enough" [S2].
- **Labor**: campaigns on union jobs [S1].
- **Climate**: supports climate resiliency measures, citing increasingly common extreme weather events [S1][S2].

## Record

- Appointed to Edmonds City Council September 2022; subsequently elected; has served as Council President Pro Tem [S4][S5].
- Campaign fundraising: $33,346 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, fifth among the six candidates [S6].

- Website lists Dave Somers, State Senator Vandana Slatter, former Seattle City Attorney Pete Holmes, and WEA-PAC [S2].
