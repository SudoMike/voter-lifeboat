## Background

Danica Noble (prefers Democratic Party) is an attorney with roughly 20 years at the Federal Trade Commission, most recently in the Bureau of Competition's Technology Enforcement Division; she also served as a technical advisor on competitive markets for USAID in Ukraine and clerked for the U.S. Senate and House Judiciary Committees [S1][S2]. She is a first-time candidate [S3][S4]. For the last four years she worked on the FTC's antitrust case against Meta [S3]. She chairs the Washington State Bar Association's Antitrust, Consumer Protection, and Unfair Business Practices Section, and previously served on the Washington State PTA Advocacy Committee and as PTA advocacy chair at Seattle public schools (Broadview-Thomson K-8, Robert Eagle Staff, McDonald Elementary); she was a director of NORML Women of Washington from 2011–2021 [S1]. She is a mother of three public school students, attended Whitman College and Georgetown Law, and was a Community Fellow at the UW Center for an Informed Public [S1].

## Positions

- **Antitrust/economy**: campaigns on challenging monopoly power, corporate consolidation, and exploitative business models in tech, healthcare, and groceries; frames this as lowering consumer prices [S1][S2].
- **Technology/AI**: proposes "common-sense guardrails" on technology targeting children and workers, including restricting educational-tech data collection, regulating AI in classrooms, and preventing surveillance pricing [S1][S2][S3].
- **Education**: prioritizes fully funding public schools; supports banning cellphones in public schools; favors moving away from per-student head-count funding models [S2][S3][S4].
- **Cost of living**: pledges to lower living costs for working families through economic reform [S2].
- **Budget transparency**: advocates budget transparency and examining state liability in lawsuit settlements as a cost-cutting measure [S4].

## Record

- No elective office record; professional record is federal enforcement work, including the FTC v. Meta antitrust litigation [S1][S3].
- Campaign fundraising: $114,947 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, the most of any candidate in this race [S5].

## Endorsements

- Pamphlet cites the Washington State Labor Council, Snohomish County Democrats, and Representatives Kagi, Taylor, Fosse, Kloba, Donaghy, Shavers, and Parshley [S1].
- Website adds King County Councilmember Teresa Mosqueda, State Senator Claire Wilson, Seattle City Attorney Erika Evans, Port Commissioner Selena Killin, and Snohomish County Councilmember Megan Dunn [S2].
- The Herald lists Reps. Shelley Kloba and Mary Fosse, Snohomish County Councilmember Megan Dunn, Snohomish County Democrats, and the Washington State Labor Council [S3]. (Snohomish County Democrats also appear on Scully's pamphlet list; multiple endorsements are possible [S1][S3].)

## Scoring notes

- Top fundraiser in the race by a wide margin (roughly double most rivals) despite being a first-time candidate [S5].
- The field's only candidate whose core platform is antitrust/consumer-protection enforcement and tech/AI regulation, backed by direct FTC litigation experience [S1][S3].
- Strongest labor-institution backing in the race (Washington State Labor Council) plus a bloc of sitting and former state legislators [S1][S2].
- No local elected record, unlike Chen, Nand, and Scully; her differentiator is subject-matter enforcement expertise rather than municipal governance [S3][S4].
