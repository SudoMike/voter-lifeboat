## Background

Chris Bloomquist (prefers Democratic Party) is a first-time candidate, a recruiting executive and small business owner who founded a climate-focused recruiting company [S1][S2][S3]. He lives in Seattle and holds a BS in Computer Engineering from Iowa State University [S1][S3]. He is Latino American; his mother emigrated from Chile after escaping authoritarianism [S1] (his campaign site describes his father as the Chilean immigrant and his mother as a fourth-generation farmer — the pamphlet and site differ on this detail [S1][S2]). His civic activity includes testifying in support of legislation to "demask ICE," former board service with the KD Hall Foundation supporting BIPOC girls' education, PTA advocacy for school funding in Olympia, coaching youth sports, job-search seminars for WorkSourceWA, founding PNW Climate Week's career fair, and building housing with Habitat for Humanity [S1]. KUOW reports he was recruited to run by state Rep. Lauren Davis (LD32 Position 2) [S4].

## Positions

- **Education**: prioritizes fully funding public schools, citing insufficient legislative will historically; supports detaching school funding from student head counts [S3][S4].
- **Taxes**: supports the state's new millionaires tax but opposes raising taxes on working families [S4].
- **Climate/economy**: campaigns on growing Washington's green economy, clean energy investment, and solar/wind development [S2][S3].
- **Homelessness/public safety**: pledges evidence-based approaches to homelessness; has led first-responder roundtables on making Aurora Avenue safer [S1].
- **Technology**: says he will author AI legislation that protects children and centers renewable energy [S1].
- **Civil rights**: champions civil rights and support for immigrant communities; states "women's rights are paramount" [S1][S2][S3].
- **Fiscal accountability**: pledges to scrutinize spending and expects government to deliver results [S1].

## Record

- No elective office record; first-time candidate [S1][S3].
- Campaign fundraising: $58,163 in contributions reported to the PDC as of 2026-07-16, third among the six candidates [S5].

## Endorsements

- Pamphlet cites LD32's sitting legislators Senator Jesse Salomon and Representative Lauren Davis, plus Senator Javier Valdez and the Lynnwood Mayor [S1].
- Website lists Sen. Jesse Salomon, Sen. Javier Valdez, Rep. Lauren Davis, Rep. Alicia Rule, Lynnwood Mayor George Hurst, former Shoreline Councilmember Susan Chang, and Lynnwood Councilmember David Parshall [S2].
- The Herald adds the Washington Council of Police and Sheriffs and environmental/progressive organizations [S3].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate endorsed by both of LD32's sitting legislators (Sen. Salomon and Rep. Davis), and was recruited to run by Davis [S1][S4].
- Distinct law-enforcement endorsement (Washington Council of Police and Sheriffs) uncommon among the Democratic field [S3].
- First-time candidate whose lane is green-economy/workforce development rather than municipal governance (Chen, Nand, Scully) or federal enforcement (Noble) [S2][S3].
- Third in fundraising, ahead of both sitting Edmonds councilmembers [S5].
