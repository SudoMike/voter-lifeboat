## Background

Ted Cooke is a Republican candidate from Covington for State Representative, 47th Legislative District, Position 2 [S1][S3]. He works as a senior software tester, team lead, and certified project manager, and holds a Bachelor of Theology from Beacon University [S1]. His party experience includes serving as King County GOP 2nd Vice Chair (2020-2022) and as an elected precinct committee officer since 2009; he also served on the Timberlane HOA board in Covington (2010-2011) [S1]. He has not held elected public office; he previously ran for this seat and was defeated by incumbent Chris Stearns in the November 2024 general election [S3]. His volunteer history includes an education nonprofit (Portable Academic Lapdesk) and organizations including Vine Maple Place Women's Shelter, Union Gospel Mission, and the Maple Valley Food Bank [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes / spending:** Says he will freeze all new taxes and eliminate existing taxes on income, property, and fuel; would order audits of every state office and program and cut waste [S1].
- **Government structure:** Says he would end public-private partnerships and legislative commissions he characterizes as unaccountable; describes state government as overreaching in taxation, regulation, and mandates [S1][S2].
- **Housing:** Says he will prioritize "starter homes with yards" over high-density apartments, which he characterizes as forced on the district by Olympia [S1].
- **Homelessness:** Says he will pursue solutions to help homeless individuals get off the street and back to productive life [S2].
- **Government hiring / social policy:** Says he would replace DEI with merit in government hiring and would "uphold the biological definition of gender" [S1].
- **Education:** Supports restoring "full parental rights in education" and a "classical American education"; says schools should focus on student success, "not on indoctrination" [S1][S2].
- **Public safety:** Names public safety as a top priority; supports funding police to make neighborhoods, parks, schools, and shopping areas safe [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

His campaign site lists a personal testimonial from the late Donald Tilly, a retired Paccar audit manager and U.S. Marine [S2]. No organizational or elected-official endorsements were found in the pamphlet or on the campaign site [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes

- Challenger with party-organization and community-volunteer experience but no prior elected public office, facing the incumbent he lost to in 2024 [S1][S3].
- Platform centers on tax elimination/freezes, state audits, and cutting programs, contrasting directly with the incumbent's record of state spending on housing and behavioral health [S1].
- Takes explicit social-policy stances (DEI removal, parental rights, "biological definition of gender") that sharply differentiate him from his opponent's support of gender-affirming care [S1][S2].
- No institutional endorsements identified, versus a broad endorsement slate for his opponent [S1][S2].
