## Background
Zach Hall (D) is the incumbent State Representative for Legislative District 5, Position 1. The King County Council appointed him in June 2025 to fill the House seat vacated by Victoria Hunt, who moved to the State Senate after the death of Sen. Bill Ramos [S4][S5]. He is a first-term legislator serving as Vice Chair of the House Environment & Energy Committee and a member of the Transportation and Local Government committees [S1][S3]. A lifelong Issaquah resident, he served six years on the Issaquah City Council and chaired the Eastside Fire & Rescue Board [S1][S2]. He previously worked as a legislative assistant in the WA House and as a consultant for cities and state agencies, and holds an MPA from the UW Evans School [S1]. He serves on the Friends of the Issaquah Salmon Hatchery Board [S1].

## Positions
- **Energy costs:** Prioritizes lowering energy costs and modernizing the power grid [S1][S2].
- **Housing:** Supported reforms he describes as fixing the housing crisis [S1][S2].
- **Transportation:** Prioritizes safety and congestion improvements on SR 18 (including the widening project) and light-rail infrastructure [S1][S2][S3].
- **Education:** Supports fully funding public schools [S1][S2].
- **Health care / childcare:** Supports expanding access to affordable childcare and healthcare, and behavioral-health services [S1][S2][S3].
- **Public safety:** Emphasizes supporting first responders, citing his Eastside Fire & Rescue board chairmanship [S1].

## Record
- **HB 2296 (2026):** sponsored and passed a bill enabling utilities to finance residential energy-efficiency upgrades repaid gradually on monthly utility bills [S3].
- **HB 2575 (2026):** sponsored and passed a bill streamlining environmental reporting requirements while preserving protections [S3].
- Voted for the new "millionaire earner" income tax enacted this year [S5].
- 2026-cycle fundraising: $44,039.35 across 86 reported contributions — the highest in this race (PDC data as of 2026-07-16) [S6].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet: Eastside Firefighters, WA Labor Council, WA Education Association, Planned Parenthood Alliance Advocates, Alliance for Gun Responsibility, Congresswoman Kim Schrier, State Sen. Victoria Hunt, State Rep. Lisa Callan, and local mayors [S1].
- Campaign site adds: Governor Bob Ferguson, King County Executive Girmay Zahilay, Affordable Housing Council, WA Conservation Action, WA State Nurses Association [S2].

## Scoring notes
- Appointed, not yet elected, to the seat — this primary is his first electoral test for the position, though he has won two Issaquah council elections [S4][S5].
- Only candidate in the race with a state legislative record (two passed bipartisan bills in his first session) [S3].
- His vote for the new millionaire-earner income tax is the sharpest single policy contrast with Republican Michelle Bennett, who opposes an income tax [S5].
- Holds the broadest institutional endorsement slate (governor, congresswoman, labor, education, firefighters) and the largest war chest in the field [S1][S2][S6].
- Positioned as a consensus-building mainstream Democrat, with Warmerdam running to his left and Bennett to his right [S1][S5].
