## Background
Aimee Warmerdam (D) is a progressive first-time legislative candidate from Issaquah [S3]. Her elected experience is as a Precinct Committee Officer in the 5th LD [S1]. She works in retail sales, with prior work in warehouse labor, computer repair, food service, and hospitality; she describes her education as "no formal higher education, apprenticeship in organizing" [S1]. She grew up in a low-income Habitat for Humanity home in Snoqualmie [S2]. She volunteers with the Seattle Democratic Socialists of America, Whole Washington, and Progressive Victory; her volunteer work includes utility-fee advocacy for renters (with the Puget Sound Tenants Union), reviewing legislation for Rep. Shaun Scott, universal-healthcare advocacy, and canvassing for progressive candidates [S1][S2].

## Positions
- **Health care:** Supports single-payer universal healthcare via the Whole Washington initiative [S1][S2].
- **Labor / economy:** Champions a $26 minimum wage, a 32-hour workweek, and guaranteed paid time off for all workers [S1][S2][S3]; supports a millionaire's tax, a payroll tax on large companies, state-owned banking, and mandatory paid vacation [S2][S3].
- **Housing:** Supports new social housing, streamlined permitting, multi-family zoning, disincentives for property speculation, and tiny-house villages for homeless residents [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Supports fully funding public schools, teacher raises, free school meals, eliminating community-college tuition, and expanded after-school programs [S1][S2].
- **Transit:** Supports expanding public transit into smaller communities and demands Sound Transit deliver all ST3 projects as approved, including Issaquah light rail [S1][S2].
- **Environment:** Would reverse cuts to the Department of Natural Resources; opposes AI data-center expansion on energy and environmental grounds [S1][S2].
- **Taxes:** Would pay for her program through progressive taxation on "the ultra rich and largest corporations" [S1].

## Record
- No prior elected office beyond PCO; record is as a volunteer organizer and advocate [S1].
- Refuses corporate PAC money [S1]. 2026-cycle fundraising: $3,679.46 across 10 reported contributions (PDC data as of 2026-07-16) [S4].

## Endorsements
None listed in the pamphlet or on the campaign site [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- The clear left flank of the race: only candidate backing single-payer healthcare, a $26 minimum wage, a 32-hour workweek, and social housing — running against a mainstream Democratic incumbent [S1][S3].
- Differentiates on class background and organizing experience rather than governmental or professional credentials [S1][S2].
- Explicitly anti-"corporate duopoly" framing and corporate-PAC refusal; smallest verified fundraising among the two Democrats ($3.7k vs Hall's $44k) [S1][S4].
- Her opposition to AI data-center expansion is a distinct environmental/energy position not taken by any opponent [S2].
