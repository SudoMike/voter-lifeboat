## Background
Michelle Bennett (R) is Mercer Island's police chief and has worked in Western Washington law enforcement since 1990 [S2]. Her career includes serving as police chief in Maple Valley, Sammamish, and Mercer Island, and as Planning and Operations Major in the King County Sheriff's Office, plus deputy service in North Bend [S1]. She retired from the Edmonds police department in February 2025, then returned to active duty on Mercer Island, serving as interim chief from July 2025 before being appointed permanent chief by the city manager [S3][S4]. She has held leadership roles including President of the King County Police Chiefs/Sheriffs and the WA State FBI National Academy Associates, and instructs at Northwestern University and Bellevue College [S1]. She holds a Doctorate in Education from Seattle Pacific University and an M.S. and B.A. from Central Washington University [S1]. She co-created a "Take a Stand Against Bullying" curriculum used in multiple states and founded community programs including the Sammamish Healthy Communities Coalition and youth explorer/volunteer programs [S1].

## Positions
- **Taxes / budget:** Opposes an income tax [S2]; criticizes Olympia for "the largest tax increase in state history" while families face rising costs, and says she will ensure state spending delivers measurable results [S1].
- **Public safety:** Says police "have been left without clear tools to protect victims and neighborhoods"; pledges to fight for safer communities drawing on her career as a police chief in the district [S1].
- **Education:** Pledges to fight for "stronger schools," citing her education doctorate [S1].
- **Governance:** Frames her candidacy as "common-sense leadership" and respect for taxpayers [S1].

## Record
- Three decades in law enforcement leadership, including chief posts in three cities within or near the district; permanent Mercer Island chief appointment effective January 2026 after an interim period from July 2025 [S1][S2][S3].
- No legislative voting record (has not held legislative office) [S1].
- 2026-cycle fundraising: $21,351.88 across 81 reported contributions — second-highest in this race (PDC data as of 2026-07-16) [S5].

## Scoring notes

- Deepest public-safety résumé in the field by a wide margin (35 years, multiple chief positions), which she pairs with an education doctorate — her two campaign pillars [S1].
