## Background

Josh Schaer is an Assistant Attorney General with the Washington State
Attorney General's Office and a Judge Pro Tem in King County courts, with
part-time judicial service since 2008 [S1][S2]. He reports 25 years of
courtroom experience spanning public defense, civil practice, and appellate
advising at the AGO [S1][S2]. He holds a J.D. from the University of
Washington School of Law, a B.A. with honors from the University of
Washington, and an A.A.S. from Bellevue College [S1]. He has lived on the
Eastside since 1988; his campaign biography recounts a family fire that
shaped his view of community assistance [S1][S2]. He previously served eight
years on the Issaquah City Council [S2].

## Positions

- Says he will uphold the rule of law, adhere to ethical standards, and
  conduct proceedings with clarity and compassion [S1].
- Campaign themes: fair and impartial justice, broad legal expertise,
  judicial integrity, and community leadership focused on equitable outcomes
  [S2].

## Record

- Part-time judicial service (pro tem) since 2008 in King County courts
  [S1][S2].
- Eight years as an elected Issaquah City Council member [S2].
- States he holds "Exceptionally Well-Qualified" ratings from the King
  County Bar Association, Latino/a Bar Association of Washington, QLaw, and
  the Joint Asian Judicial Evaluation Committee; self-reported and not
  independently verified (KCBA had not published a public 2026 ratings list
  at access time) [S1][S2].
- Community service listed on his site: mock-trial coach at Skyline High
  School, Sunday-school teacher, Kiwanis member, youth soccer coach,
  professional mentor [S2].

## Endorsements

- Per his pamphlet statement: 1st LD Democrats (sole endorsement);
  Amalgamated Transit Union Local 587; Attorney General Nick Brown; King
  County Executive Girmay Zahilay; former Deputy Executive Fred Jarrett;
  Senators Lisa Wellman and Manka Dhingra; Representatives My-Linh Thai and
  Davina Duerr; Supreme Court Justices Theo Angelis and Colleen Melody and
  retired Justices Bobbe Bridge and Mary Yu; King County judges, Eastside
  councilmembers, and 100+ attorneys [S1].

## Campaign finance

- PDC summary (accessed 2026-07-16): about $14,489 raised, $13,039 spent —
  the smallest war chest of the three candidates [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Longest span of part-time judicial service in the race (pro tem since
  2008, per his statement), though not full-time judicial work [S1].
- Professional background is government civil/appellate practice (Assistant
  Attorney General) plus earlier public defense — a different lane from
  Trasen (defense/appellate/ALJ) and Tse (prosecution/private civil) [S1][S2].
- Only candidate in the race with prior elected office (Issaquah City
  Council) [S2].
- Self-reports "Exceptionally Well-Qualified" from four bar associations,
  including KCBA — the broadest self-reported rating claim in the race after
  Trasen's "five associations" claim; none of the candidates' rating claims
  could be independently verified [S1][S2].
