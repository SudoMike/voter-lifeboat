## Background

First-time candidate; community organizer in the 37th Legislative District
[S1]. Co-chair and community representative on the Washington Environmental
Justice Council; collaborative governance strategist at People's Economy
Lab; formerly led the Campus Sustainability Fund at UW Seattle [S1][S2].
MPA and MPH from the University of Washington; BS in anthropology from
Virginia Commonwealth University [S1]. Community work includes food access,
immigrant family support, and leadership mentoring with Sage Leaders and
Standing for Democracy [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes/economy:** Progressive revenue so "the wealthiest pay their fair
  share"; worker rights and solidarity-economy initiatives [S1][S2].
- **Housing:** Affordable housing a top priority [S1][S2].
- **Climate:** Climate justice; helped shape advocacy around the Climate
  Commitment Act and HEAL Act, and co-chairs the Environmental Justice
  Council implementing the HEAL Act [S1][S2].
- **Governance:** Wants community voices in policymaking via
  government-funded community assemblies, which she helped lead [S1][S2].
- **Public services:** K-12 and higher-education funding, public health,
  alternative public safety approaches, reliable transit [S1][S2].
- **Campaign finance:** Rejects corporate donations, PAC, fossil fuel, and
  defense contractor money; small-dollar funded [S2].

## Endorsements

Transit Riders Union endorsed Brown for the LD 37 Senate race (June 2026)
[S3]. (The 37th District Democrats endorsed her opponent [S3].)

## Scoring notes

- Outsider/organizer profile: no elected experience, contrasting with an
  opponent who is a sitting legislator [S1].
- Distinctive emphasis on participatory governance (community assemblies)
  and environmental justice implementation experience [S1][S2].
- Explicit refusal of corporate/PAC money differentiates her campaign
  finance posture [S2].
