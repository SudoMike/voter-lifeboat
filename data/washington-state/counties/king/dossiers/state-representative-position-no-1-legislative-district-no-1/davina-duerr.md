## Background
Incumbent Democratic state representative for the 1st Legislative District, first appointed in 2019; chairs the House Local Government Committee [S1]. Served on the Bothell City Council 2016–2022, including as Deputy Mayor 2016–2020 [S1]. Licensed architect (describes herself as the only one in the legislature); has served on the Eastside Transportation Partnership, WSDOT Executive Advisory Group, Sound Transit BRT Elected Leadership Group, PSRC Transportation Policy Board, and the Sound Cities Association board [S1]. Bachelor of Architecture, Syracuse University [S1]. Ten years on the Bothell Landmarks Preservation Board (eight as chair); co-founded the Northshore Schools Foundation M.I.L.K. Money campaign for students experiencing homelessness [S1].

## Positions
- Housing: says affordable homes near jobs are central to quality of life; emphasizes making it cheaper and faster to build housing [S1].
- Transportation: emphasizes accessible transportation; cites securing $532 million for the 1st District in 2025, including $464.5 million for the I-405 corridor project [S1].
- Environment: cites work phasing out super-pollutant refrigerants and strengthening landfill regulation enforcement [S1].
- Education: names strong public education as a priority [S1].

## Record
- States seven of her bills became law last term, including bills to lower the cost of building affordable housing, phase out super-pollutant refrigerants, close a growth-management loophole, streamline housing permits, and give the Department of Ecology enforcement authority over landfill regulation [S1].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists: Washington State Labor Council, Washington Conservation Action, Sen. Stanford, Rep. Kloba, and several mayors [S1].
- Sierra Club Washington 2026 primary endorsements list her for House Position 1, LD 1 [S2].
