## Background
Republican-preference candidate; no prior elected experience listed [S1]. Immigrant from Taiwan (arrived 1989); operated a hair salon for eight years starting in 1981 and owned/operated a downtown Redmond restaurant for twenty-six years [S1][S2]. Volunteer election observer at King County Elections since 2022 [S1].

## Positions
- Affordability/taxes: opposes tax increases; says her focus is "lower taxes"; campaign messaging includes "Stop Tax Increases" [S1][S2].
- Public safety: prioritizes "safer neighborhoods" [S1][S2].
- Parental rights: names parental rights as a core priority [S1][S2].
- Small business/economy: wants an economy where small businesses thrive; frames rising taxes, housing costs, and "government overreach" as the main threats to affordability [S1][S2].

## Endorsements
- King County Republican Party [S1][S2].

## Scoring notes
- First-time candidate running on affordability, public safety, and parental rights; contrasts with an incumbent who runs on a legislative record [S1].
- Small-business background (salon, restaurant) is the basis of her economic platform [S1].
- Campaign materials state priorities but do not detail specific policy mechanisms [S1][S2].
- Two-candidate race; both advance to the general regardless of primary result.
