## Background

Miriam Mboya is a King County government employee ("Program and Operations
Professional") who has worked inside county government for the past seven
years, in roles touching budgeting, operations, contracting, and community
partnerships [S1][S2]. She immigrated from Kenya at age six and grew up in
Skyway, within District 2; she describes lived experience with
homelessness, food insecurity, mental health struggles, the foster care
system, and having an incarcerated parent [S1][S2]. She holds a B.A. in
political science with a specialization in legal studies and an M.A. in
communications [S1]. This is her first run for elected office (no prior
elected experience found) [S1][S2].

## Positions

- **Housing:** "Massively expand investment in affordable housing,"
  protect renters from exploitative practices, expand early housing
  support services for youth, and create pathways for seniors to stay in
  their homes [S2].
- **Behavioral health/public safety:** Prevention over enforcement:
  deeper investment in mental health and addiction services (citing King
  County Crisis Care Centers), expanded diversion programs tailored to
  neighborhoods, and addressing the school-to-prison pipeline through
  restorative pathways rather than incarceration [S1][S2].
- **Economic opportunity:** Expand job access, education/training, and
  apprenticeships; support small businesses; economic stability for
  working families [S1][S2].
- **Immigrants/refugees:** Ensure families can access county services
  without fear [S2].
- **Government operations:** Simplify access to county services and
  improve responsiveness; emphasizes stewardship and transparency of the
  county's roughly $20 billion biennial budget; frames her approach around
  "Harambee" (collective action) and community-led governance [S1][S2].

## Record

- No elected or legislative record; her relevant experience is seven years
  as a King County employee working on budgeting, operations, and
  contracting (self-described; no independent detail found) [S1][S2].
- Campaign fundraising: about $43,945 in reported contributions as of
  mid-July 2026 — roughly a fifth of what each of her two opponents has
  raised [S3].

## Endorsements

- Three suburban city councilmembers listed on her site: a Shoreline
  councilmember and deputy mayor, a Tukwila councilmember, and a Kent
  councilmember [S2]. No union, party-organization, or newspaper
  endorsements found as of mid-July 2026.

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate in the race without prior elected office; positions
  herself as a first-time, lived-experience candidate against two sitting
  officeholders [S1][S2].
- Distinct claim to internal King County operational knowledge (budgeting,
  contracting, operations) from seven years as a county employee, versus
  her opponents' external elected experience [S1].
- Platform overlaps heavily with her opponents on housing, behavioral
  health, and prevention-first safety, but with fewer concrete funding
