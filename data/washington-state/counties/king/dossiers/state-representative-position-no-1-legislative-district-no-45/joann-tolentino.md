## Background

JoAnn Tolentino is a Woodinville resident who taught private music lessons for roughly 35 years and worked in Governance, Risk, and Compliance software sales before retiring [S1][S2]. Her elected experience is five years as a precinct committee officer [S1]. She attended Wenatchee Valley College and Seattle Pacific University as a Music Therapy major [S1]. She describes herself as a teacher, mother, and grandmother, has been married 46 years, raised four children, and adopted a child from foster care [S1][S2]. She has volunteered in grassroots civic activities and worked summers with incarcerated youth [S1]. She was a candidate for Northshore School District school board, District 4, in the November 2025 general election [S3].

## Positions

- **Elections:** Supports voter ID requirements, paper ballots, and bipartisan election oversight, framed as "secure elections" [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Wants to empower parents and prioritize academic excellence [S1]; supports an arts-integrated curriculum emphasizing reading and math, parental involvement, and opposes critical race theory and gender ideology in classrooms [S2].
- **Housing:** Says she will fight regulations she views as driving up housing costs [S1] and supports housing affordability through zoning reform [S2].
- **Energy:** Champions affordable energy [S1], including fossil fuels and nuclear power [S2].
- **Homelessness/behavioral health:** Calls for "proven recovery solutions" for homelessness [S1] and treatment-focused mental health and addiction programs [S2].
- **Public safety:** Supports "full support for our law enforcement" [S1].
- **Government accountability:** Emphasizes transparency, fiscal accountability, and eliminating government waste [S1][S2].
- **Immigration:** Supports legal immigration with what her site calls humane processing [S2].

## Endorsements

- King County Republican Party, per her pamphlet statement [S1].

## Scoring notes

- First-time legislative candidate running against a nearly 20-year incumbent; presents herself as "not a politician" [S1].
- Background is in music education and compliance work rather than law or government [S1][S2].
- Platform centers on election-security measures, parental rights in schools, deregulation of housing and energy, and support for law enforcement [S1][S2].
