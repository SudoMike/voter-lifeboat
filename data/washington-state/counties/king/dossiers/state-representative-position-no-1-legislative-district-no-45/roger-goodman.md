## Background

Roger Goodman is the incumbent State Representative for the 45th Legislative District, Position 1, serving since 2007 [S1][S3]. He chairs the House Community Safety Committee and the Statute Law Committee, and serves on the Civil Rights and Judiciary Committee and the Early Learning & Human Services Committee [S1][S3]. Before the Legislature, he was an attorney in private practice, worked at the King County Bar Association (2000-08), directed the Washington State Sentencing Guidelines Commission (1998-2000), and served as a congressional chief of staff and legislative director (1988-1995) [S1]. He holds an AB from Dartmouth College, a JD from George Washington University, and an MPA from Harvard University [S1]. Community roles include the Hopelink Board of Directors, president of Kirkland's Lakeview Neighborhood Association, Rotary Club, and the King County Behavioral Health Advisory Board [S1].

## Positions

- **Public safety:** Says his priorities are improving public safety, reducing gun violence, and helping people in crisis [S1]; his campaign site emphasizes police accountability reform [S2].
- **Behavioral health:** Cites passing laws to expand drug and mental health services and describes strengthening responses to mental illness as a focus [S1][S2].
- **Taxes and cost of living:** Says he voted to reduce the tax burden on working people and small businesses and to provide free school meals and more affordable childcare [S1].
- **Education:** Pledges continued support for K-12 funding [S1] and expanding higher education opportunities [S2].
- **Housing:** Supports affordable housing investments [S1][S2].
- **Climate/transportation:** Supports climate action [S1]; campaign site cites carbon-emissions legislation and transportation investments framed around social equity and environmental sustainability [S2].
- **Reproductive rights:** Pledges to champion reproductive rights [S1].

## Record

- Sitting legislator since 2007; current chair of the House Community Safety Committee [S1][S3].
- States he passed laws to protect survivors of crime, expand drug and mental health services, and improve law enforcement accountability and training [S1]; his campaign site highlights Washington's police accountability reform package as a signature accomplishment [S2].
- Campaign site also cites pandemic relief for small businesses, tenants, and schools, and legislation to cap carbon emissions [S2].

## Endorsements

None verified for the 2026 race at time of research; his campaign site has an endorsements section but specific organizational endorsements were not confirmed [S2].
