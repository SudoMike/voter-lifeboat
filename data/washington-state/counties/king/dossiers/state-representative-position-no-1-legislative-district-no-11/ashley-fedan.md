## Background

- Registered nurse for over 25 years and Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA); owns a small business providing anesthesia care, including gender-affirming care, abortion, and reproductive care [S1].
- Navy veteran; served as a Lieutenant Commander in the Navy Nurse Corps [S1]; joined the Navy Nurse Corps in 2012 and retired from the Navy Reserve at the same rank as her grandfather [S2].
- Began her nursing career in 1999 in Cleveland Clinic's cardiothoracic ICU; worked as a travel critical-care nurse, including at University of Washington Medical Center and Harborview Medical Center [S2].
- Democratic Party activist: Precinct Committee Officer in the 11th LD, 2024 Democratic National Committee delegate, and former chair of the 28th Legislative District Democrats [S1][S4].
- Education: BSN, Case Western Reserve University; MS, York College of Pennsylvania; Doctor of Education in Interdisciplinary Leadership, Creighton University [S1].
- Community service: Pro-Choice Washington PAC member, APRNs of Washington State board member, past president of the Washington Association of Nurse Anesthesiology, medic for Southend Indivisible [S1].

## Positions

- **Healthcare:** will "fight for accessible healthcare"; notes no nurses currently serve in the legislature and argues a nurse's problem-solving perspective is needed in Olympia [S1]; emphasizes healthcare access for vulnerable populations [S2].
- **Cost of living:** cites working people "squeezed by housing and childcare costs, healthcare bills, and education debt" [S1]; campaign priorities are cost of living, healthcare access, and defending "human dignity" against federal government actions [S2].
- **Housing/education/jobs:** supports affordable housing, K-12 investments, career training, and well-paying jobs [S1].
- Campaign finance: $56,840 in itemized contributions across 177 records reported to the PDC for the 2026 cycle as of 2026-07-16 [S3].

## Endorsements

- King County Democrats: endorsed (dual endorsement alongside incumbent David Hackney) [S5].
- Campaign website lists: The Stranger, 11th LD Democrats, King County Democrats, Washington Stonewall Democrats, and nursing organizations [S2]. Pamphlet statement claims endorsements from "Nurses, State Employees, Elected Officials, 11th LD Dems, County Dems, Planned Parenthood, more" [S1].
- Washington State Labor Council delegates passed her over, endorsing incumbent Hackney instead [S6].

## Scoring notes

- Runs as a same-party (Democratic) challenger to a Democratic incumbent; her differentiator is professional rather than sharply ideological — a frontline healthcare practitioner's perspective versus the incumbent's legal/prosecutorial background [S1][S4].
- Only candidate in the race framing representation of nurses in the legislature as a distinct gap [S1].
- Secured a dual endorsement from King County Democrats but not from WSLC or the Seattle Times, which stayed with the incumbent [S5][S6].
- Larger small-donor base than the incumbent by contribution count (177 vs. 75 itemized records) but roughly 40% of his dollar total [S3].
