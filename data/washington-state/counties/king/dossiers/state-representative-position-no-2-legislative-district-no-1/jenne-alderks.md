## Background
Jenne Alderks is the current Deputy Mayor of Bothell, first elected to the Bothell City Council in 2021 and re-elected in 2025; she previously served on the city's Planning Commission [S1][S4]. She works as a human services specialist leading trauma-informed systems-change efforts in behavioral health programming (in Human Services for Snohomish County per filing coverage), and was formerly a self-employed birth and postpartum doula for over a decade [S1][S4]. She holds a Masters of Public Affairs (2024), a Public Leadership Credential from Harvard Kennedy School (2021), a Masters of Early Childhood Education (2008), and a B.S. in Human Development (2005) [S1]. She is a board member (Vice-President) of North Urban Human Services Alliance and of Lake City Partners Ending Homelessness, and a former LGBTQIA+ youth group leader [S1].

## Positions
- **Affordability / health care:** Says she will fight for affordability and supports guaranteeing universal healthcare coverage [S1][S2].
- **Housing / homelessness:** Supports expanding housing opportunities; cites board service with a homelessness-services nonprofit [S1][S2].
- **Public safety:** Supports crisis-response programs and community court approaches, citing her Bothell record [S2].
- **Transportation:** Supports investing in reliable transit and improved transportation infrastructure [S1][S2].
- **Civil rights:** Emphasizes protecting rights, freedoms, and human dignity of all Washingtonians [S1][S2].
- **Parks / environment:** Cites preserving parks and green spaces as a council priority she would continue [S1][S2].

## Record
- On Bothell City Council: campaign cites strengthening public safety through crisis response and community court initiatives, expanding housing opportunities, preserving parks/green space, and improving transportation connections (campaign's own characterization) [S2].
- 2026-cycle fundraising: $11,462.45 across 40 reported contributions (PDC data as of 2026-07-16) [S3].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites endorsements by the 1st LD, Snohomish County, and King County Democrats, plus local elected leaders [S1].
- Campaign site states she received the 1st LD Democrats' sole endorsement [S2].

## Scoring notes
- The Democratic challenger to a 9-year Democratic incumbent (Kloba); her claim of the 1st LD Democrats' sole endorsement is the race's most notable dynamic [S1][S2].
- Differentiates on local-government executive experience (Bothell Deputy Mayor) and a human-services/behavioral-health professional background rather than a legislative record [S1].
- Policy platform is broadly progressive and overlaps substantially with the incumbent's (affordability, transit, housing, civil rights); universal healthcare is her most distinct stated commitment in this field [S1][S2].
