## Background
Shelley Kloba is the incumbent State Representative for Legislative District 1, Position 2, serving since 2017 (first elected 2016) [S1][S3]. She is Vice-Chair of the House Technology, Economic Development, and Veterans Committee and a member of the Consumer Protection & Business and Environment & Energy Committees [S1][S3]. She serves as an ex-officio member of the WA State Gambling Commission, sits on the National Conference of State Legislatures AI, Cybersecurity, and Data Privacy Task Force, and founded the House Technology caucus [S1]. Before the Legislature she was a Kirkland City Councilmember (2013–2016), Kirkland Park Board member, Lake Washington Schools Foundation trustee, and WA State PTA Legislative Director [S1][S3]. She has been a licensed health care provider (massage practice) for over 20 years and holds a B.S. in Kinesiology from the University of Illinois [S1].

## Positions
- **Taxes:** Says she will work to "fix our regressive tax code" and cut costs for working families [S1]; proposes closing "tax loopholes that do not serve the public interest" and redirecting the revenue to schools [S2].
- **Technology / privacy:** Cites work to improve data privacy, rein in data brokers, and reduce harms of AI to consumers; sponsor of the People's Privacy Act to give individuals control over personal data [S1][S3].
- **Education:** Supports fully funding K-12 education, with district flexibility to attract teachers and reduce class sizes [S1][S2].
- **Transportation:** Supports improving transportation infrastructure; favors a multi-strategy congestion approach (housing near job centers, telecommuting, better bus frequency, rail planning, highway incident response, hard-shoulder running for buses at peak) [S1][S2].
- **Climate / energy:** Supports green-jobs creation, renewable-energy investment, waterway monitoring funding, and workforce retraining [S1][S2].
- **Health care / civil rights:** Says she will work to increase access to behavioral health care and protect civil rights [S1].

## Record
- Passed 2019 legislation creating a voluntary statewide self-exclusion program for people with gambling disorder [S3].
- Sponsor of the People's Privacy Act (data privacy) [S3].
- Described in her official caucus bio as playing an instrumental role in developing Washington's legal cannabis regulatory framework, focused on child protection and patient access [S3].
- 2026-cycle fundraising: $38,488.94 across 88 reported contributions (PDC data as of 2026-07-16) [S4].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists: Snohomish County Democrats, Sierra Club, Washington State Labor Council, Washington Education Association PAC, Washington Alliance for Gun Responsibility, Planned Parenthood Alliance Advocates [S1].
- Campaign site adds elected officials including House Speaker Laurie Jinkins, Sen. Derek Stanford, Reps. Davina Duerr, Gerry Pollet, Roger Goodman, Mary Fosse, Strom Peterson, Greg Nance, Alex Ramel, and local officials from Kenmore, Bothell, Lake Forest Park, Kirkland, and Everett; organizations including AFT Washington [S2].

## Scoring notes
- Only candidate in this race with a state legislative record (9+ years), including passed legislation on gambling self-exclusion and a signature focus on data privacy/AI consumer protection [S1][S3].
- Faces an intra-party challenge: fellow Democrat Jenne Alderks claims the 1st LD Democrats' sole endorsement, while Kloba retains extensive labor, environmental, and elected-official endorsements [S1][S2].
- Leads the field in institutional endorsements and is second in reported fundraising within the district behind seatmate Duerr's Pos. 1 race ($38.5k vs. challenger Alderks's $11.5k) [S4].
- Frames tax policy as making the code less regressive, in direct contrast to opponents proposing tax elimination (Lyon) or property-tax relief (Moon) [S1].
