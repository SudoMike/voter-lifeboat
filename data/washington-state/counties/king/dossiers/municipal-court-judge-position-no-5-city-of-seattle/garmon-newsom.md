## Background

Garmon Newsom II is a Seattle Municipal Court Pro Tem Judge and former
Burien City Attorney [S1]. Over a 30-plus-year career in criminal and civil
law he has been a felony and misdemeanor prosecutor, municipal attorney,
civil litigator, and member of the King County Ethics Board [S1]. His
campaign site details earlier prosecution work at the Bronx County District
Attorney's office and the New York City Special Narcotics Prosecutor's
office, service as Chief Prosecutor for the City of Renton, and more than
75 civil and criminal trials [S2]. Raised in Detroit as the son of a
first-generation immigrant mother, he holds a B.A. in political science from
Morehouse College (1988) and a J.D. from George Washington University Law
School (1992) [S1][S2].

## Positions

- Says he will advance restorative justice and deliver "fair, practical, and
  equitable outcomes" that build trust and safety [S1].
- Advocates diversion programs that are "evidence-based, effective, and
  accountable," with courts working collaboratively with community partners
  to break cycles of violence, addiction, and repeated system involvement
  [S1].
- Emphasizes access to justice for poor, marginalized, and underrepresented
  communities, and commits to "listen, be fair, and model civility and
  respect" [S1][S2].

## Record

- Pro tem judicial service at Seattle Municipal Court; prior public-sector
  legal leadership as Burien City Attorney and Renton Chief Prosecutor
  [S1][S2].
- States he is rated "Exceptionally Well-Qualified" by the Latina/o Bar
  Association and the Joint Asian Bar Association [S1]; his site adds the
  Cardozo Society of Washington State [S2]. Self-reported; not independently
  verified.
- Community involvement: youth mentorship, coaching, volunteerism [S1].

## Endorsements

- Seattle Times editorial board recommendation for this position (June
  2026) [S2][S3].
- Per his pamphlet statement: retiring Position 5 incumbent Judge Willie
  Gregory (endorsing Newsom "to succeed him"), retired Justice Mary Yu, the
  32nd Legislative District Democrats, and Seattle Municipal and King County
  judges including Judges Chess, Shah, Gipe, York, and Poydras and retired
  Judges McCullough, Kondo, and McKenna [S1]. His site claims three
  Washington Supreme Court justices (including two retired) and 24+ judges
  [S2].

## Campaign finance

- PDC summary (accessed 2026-07-16): about $76,705 raised, $23,936 spent —
  by far the largest fundraising total in this race (opponent Rothstein:
  ~$35k) [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Career prosecutor and municipal-government lawyer, contrasting directly
  with opponent Gabe Rothstein's 20-year public-defense career; this
  prosecution-vs-defense split is the central axis of the race [S1][S2].
- The only candidate in the race endorsed by the retiring incumbent (Judge
  Willie Gregory) and by the Seattle Times editorial board [S1][S3].
- Substantially out-fundraising the field (~$77k vs ~$35k) [S4].
- Both finalists campaign on treatment, diversion, and dignity; Newsom
  frames it through restorative justice and community partnership, so the
  practical difference lies more in background than in stated philosophy
  [S1].
