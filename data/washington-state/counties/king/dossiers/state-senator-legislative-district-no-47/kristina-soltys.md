## Background

Kristina Soltys is a Covington City Councilmember, first elected in 2019 by defeating a fourteen-year incumbent, and re-elected unopposed in 2023 [S1][S2][S3]. She immigrated from Ukraine at age five and has lived in South King County for more than 20 years [S1][S2][S3]. Professionally she has worked as a diagnostic medical sonographer, loan originator, and choir and piano teacher; she is a mother of three [S1][S3]. She holds an AA from Bellevue College and RDMS certification [S1]. Her community service includes the Covington Chamber of Commerce, the Covington Storehouse Food Bank, organizing a Ukraine medical supply relief effort, and PTA involvement [S1]. She also serves on the Sound Cities Association Public Issues Committee and the South King Housing and Homelessness Partners Executive Board [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes / fiscal:** opposes recent state tax increases, including B&O tax hikes and gas taxes, and opposes an income tax; argues the state has "a spending problem rather than a revenue shortage" and calls for voter input on taxation [S2].
- **Public safety:** supports fully funded law enforcement and increased officer staffing, paired with addiction treatment resources [S2]. Frames her platform as "results, accountability, and common-sense solutions — not partisan games" [S1].
- **Housing:** favors increasing housing supply through permitting reform and market incentives rather than state mandates [S2].
- **Economy:** seeks B&O tax reform to support the district's industrial park, which her campaign describes as the second-largest on the West Coast [S2].
- **Transportation:** prioritizes SR-516 corridor improvements and reducing commute times [S2]; cites fighting traffic congestion on the council [S1].

## Record

- On the Covington City Council: launched the "Clean Team" public-spaces program, supported full police funding including an additional officer, and helped pioneer a community resource officer program for residents facing homelessness or mental health crises [S1]. (Self-reported.)
- Her campaign states that during her six years on the council, crime in Covington fell 24% and burglary fell 56% [S2]. (Campaign claim; not independently verified at this research depth.)

## Endorsements

Per her campaign website: the Washington Senate Republican Campaign Committee, the King County Republican Party, and King County Councilmember Reagan Dunn [S2].

## Scoring notes

- Challenger with municipal (city council) experience running against a sitting state senator; her record is executive/local rather than legislative [S1][S2].
- Distinct from her opponent chiefly on fiscal approach: opposition to recent state tax increases and emphasis on spending restraint, versus the incumbent's emphasis on state investments and renter protections [S1][S2].
- On housing, prefers permitting reform and market incentives over state mandates, where her opponent pairs supply with renter protections [S2].
- Both candidates emphasize affordability, public safety funding, and SR-516/transportation improvements, so overlap on stated goals is substantial [S1][S2].
