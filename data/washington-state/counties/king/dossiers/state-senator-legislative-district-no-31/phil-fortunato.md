## Background
Phil Fortunato (R) is the incumbent, serving as 31st District State Senator since 2017; he was elected 31st District State Representative in 2016 and served as a 47th District State Representative 1998-2000 [S1][S3]. His professional background is in environmental training and consulting, international trade, and owning a small contracting business; he was founding director and first president of the PNW Erosion Control Association [S1]. He studied landscape architecture at Rutgers University's Cook College of Agriculture [S1].

## Positions
- **Taxes/spending:** attributes the state's financial challenges to "excessive spending and the unchecked growth of government programs"; pledges "responsible, disciplined use of your tax dollars" [S1].
- **Gun rights:** will "continue to stand firmly against efforts to erode our Second Amendment rights" [S1].
- **Government accountability:** campaign centers on resisting government overreach, safeguarding parental rights, and addressing crime and homelessness [S2].
- **Constituent engagement:** says he regularly seeks feedback through district surveys and town halls [S1]; has held 2026 town halls in Edgewood/Milton, Bonney Lake/Sumner, Buckley, and Orting [S2].

## Record
- Sitting state senator since 2017; won re-election in 2022 to the term expiring in 2026 [S3][S1]. No specific bills are highlighted in the pamphlet statement or on the campaign homepage [S1][S2].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites "law enforcement leaders, local elected officials, community leaders, and neighbors across the district" without naming individuals [S1].
