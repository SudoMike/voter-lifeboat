## What this office does
A Washington State Senator is the district's single member in the 49-seat Senate, elected to a four-year term. Senators write and vote on state law and budgets, confirm gubernatorial appointments, and shape tax, housing, public-safety, education, and transportation policy.

## The race
Depth: light. LD 31 spans southeast King County and northeast Pierce County (Auburn, Bonney Lake, Sumner, Buckley, Orting areas) [S2]. Two candidates filed, so **both advance from the August 4, 2026 top-two primary to the November general election** [S1]:

- **Phil Fortunato (R, incumbent)** — senator since 2017, previously a state representative; runs on Second Amendment rights, spending restraint, and opposing government overreach [S1][S2].
- **Tamara Stramel (D)** — Democratic precinct committee officer, PTA board member, and school-bond organizer near Lake Tapps; runs on education, infrastructure, and transportation-budget priority for the district [S1].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Party and role of government** — a straightforward R-vs-D contest: Fortunato frames state spending growth as the core problem; Stramel argues schools and infrastructure are underfunded [S1].
2. **Incumbency vs. fresh representation** — nearly three decades of intermittent legislative service vs. a first-time candidate with grassroots organizing background [S1].
3. **Gun policy** — Fortunato pledges to oppose erosion of Second Amendment rights; Stramel does not raise the issue in her statement [S1].
4. **Education funding** — Stramel organized the Sumner-Bonney Lake school bond and centers school funding; Fortunato centers tax restraint [S1].
