## Background
Joe Fitzgibbon (Prefers Democratic Party) has been State Representative for the 34th Legislative District since 2010 and has served as House Majority Leader since 2022 [S1]. He chaired the House Environment & Energy Committee from 2014 to 2022 and now also sits on the House Appropriations and Rules committees [S1][S3]. Raised in Burien in a Navy family and now living in West Seattle, he previously worked as a legislative assistant and environmental policy consultant and served on the Burien Planning Commission [S1][S3]. Education: BA, History and Political Science, Principia College [S1]. Past board service includes the Democratic Legislative Campaign Committee (2019–2025) and Futurewise (2016–2019) [S1].

## Positions
- Climate/energy: says he will continue advancing clean energy and emission reductions; championed the Climate Commitment Act and Clean Fuel Standard [S3]; pamphlet describes passing "the nation's strongest climate laws" [S1].
- Housing: supports expanding affordable housing, building near transit, modernizing local planning, and homelessness prevention while limiting displacement [S3].
- Taxes/budget: says he led passage of the "millionaires' tax" paired with tax cuts for working families and small businesses, and helped stabilize the state budget [S1].
- Transportation: promotes public transit, ferry service, and pedestrian/bicycle safety infrastructure [S3].
- Civil rights: defends reproductive freedom, voting rights, and racial and environmental justice; frames part of his record as defending Washington from federal attacks [S1][S3].

## Record
- House Majority Leader since 2022; nine years chairing House Environment & Energy [S1][S3].
- Championed the Climate Commitment Act (cap-and-invest) and Clean Fuel Standard [S3].
- Pamphlet says he led passage of the millionaires-tax reform package and secured investments in education, healthcare, transit, public safety, and the environment [S1].
