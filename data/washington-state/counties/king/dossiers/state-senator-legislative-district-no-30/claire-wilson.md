## Background
Incumbent Democratic state senator for the 30th Legislative District (Federal Way area) [S1][S2]. Served on the Federal Way School Board 2011–2019 [S1]. 35-year educator and administrator working on early learning, family engagement, and governance; former WIC project director; small business owner [S1]. BS in food and nutrition / child and family studies, Washington State University; MA in adult education, University of Northern Colorado [S1]. Volunteer roles include South King County Tool Library, WA State School Directors Association leadership, Citizens for Federal Way Schools, and PTA [S1].

## Positions
- Children/families: describes early learning, childcare, and youth services as her core focus [S1][S2].
- Housing/homelessness: cites delivering resources for affordable housing serving seniors, low-income families, and people exiting homelessness [S1].
- Public safety: voted for law-enforcement pay raises and additional training, and funding for drug treatment and recovery [S1].
- Taxes: says she worked to reduce the tax burden on workers and small businesses while funding K-12 [S1].
- Reproductive rights, gun responsibility, and environmental sustainability are named priorities on her site [S2].

## Record
- States she leads Senate efforts to expand services for young people, and sponsored and passed "landmark investments" in affordable childcare and early learning [S1].
- Cites securing over $7.5 million for the city of Pacific after flooding [S1].
- Sponsored legislation on medically accurate sexual-health education in schools, per her campaign site [S2].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet lists: firefighters, Planned Parenthood, teachers, nurses, Alliance for Gun Responsibility, Children's Campaign Fund, State Labor Council, Machinists [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Endorsement base spans labor, education, and advocacy organizations [S1].
