## What the office does
The single state senator for the 30th Legislative District (Federal Way, Algona, Pacific, Milton area, south King County). Four-year term in the Washington State Senate; votes on state budgets and legislation and confirms gubernatorial appointments.

## Race dynamics
Two candidates — incumbent Democrat Claire Wilson and Republican first-time candidate Michael Rutland — so both advance from the primary to the November general election regardless of primary results. Research depth is intentionally light for this reason.

## Differentiating axes
- Record vs. change: Wilson runs on a legislative record in early learning, childcare, housing, and district funding; Rutland runs on cost-of-living relief, tax opposition, and bringing "balance" to one-party control.
- Education framing: Wilson emphasizes expanding services and funding; Rutland emphasizes academic standards, CTE, and parental involvement while opposing "political agendas" in schools.
- Endorsements: Wilson lists labor, education, and advocacy organizations; Rutland lists none.
