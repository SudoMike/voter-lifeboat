## Background

Pramila Jayapal is the incumbent U.S. Representative for Washington's 7th Congressional District, serving since 2017; she was a Washington State Senator from 2015 to 2017 [S1]. Before elected office she worked at the global-health nonprofit PATH and founded and directed OneAmerica, which her campaign describes as the largest immigration advocacy organization in Washington State, leading it for 12 years [S2]. She holds a BA in English and Economics from Georgetown University and an MBA from Northwestern University [S1]. Her campaign notes she is the first South Asian American woman elected to the U.S. House [S2]. She sits on the House Judiciary, Foreign Affairs, and Budget committees, is Ranking Member of the Immigration Subcommittee, and is Chair Emerita of the Congressional Progressive Caucus after chairing it for six years [S1][S2]. She co-chairs the Monopoly Busters Caucus and the Trans Equality Task Force [S1].

## Positions

- **Healthcare**: Leads the Medicare for All Act, framing universal healthcare as "a right, not a privilege" [S1][S2].
- **Housing**: Says she has worked to build affordable housing and sponsors legislation to "guarantee housing as a human right" [S1]; sponsored the Housing Is a Human Right Act of 2025 (H.R. 4457) and the Housing not Handcuffs Act of 2025 (H.R. 4182) [S3].
- **Taxes/economy**: Backs an ultra-millionaire wealth tax, a higher federal minimum wage, and expanded workers' rights [S1]; sponsored the Ultra-Millionaire Tax Act of 2026 (H.R. 8085) and the Empowering App-Based Workers Act (H.R. 6646) [S3].
- **Immigration**: Campaigns on "humane immigration reform" and opposition to what she calls "lawless ICE and CBP actions" [S1]; sponsored the ICE Out of Our Faces Act (H.R. 7363), the Stop ICE from Kidnapping U.S. Citizens Act (H.R. 4456), and the Dignity for Detained Immigrants Act (H.R. 6397) [S3].
- **Democracy/ethics**: Leads bipartisan bills to ban stock trading by members of Congress and limit government surveillance, and supports Supreme Court reform and campaign-finance reform [S1].
- **Foreign policy**: Authored a War Powers Resolution to end U.S. hostilities with Iran [S1]; sponsored H.Con.Res. 89 directing removal of U.S. armed forces from hostilities with Iran, and a bill to sunset the 2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force (H.R. 6751) [S3].
- **Climate/education**: Says she fights climate change and leads legislation to make higher education tuition-free (College for All Act) [S1][S2].
- **Antitrust/consumer costs**: Founded the Monopoly Busters Caucus to target consolidation and price-gouging [S1].
- **Campaign finance**: States she has never taken corporate PAC money [S1].

## Record

- Sponsored 193 pieces of legislation over her House career per congress.gov data [S3].
- Recent 119th-Congress sponsored bills include: REDACT Act (H.R. 9679), Fresh Bucks for Fresh Produce Act (H.R. 9581), LIFT the BAR Act (H.R. 9432), All Students Count Act of 2026 (H.R. 8975), Ultra-Millionaire Tax Act of 2026 (H.R. 8085), U.S. Farmworker Protection Act (H.R. 7541), ICE Out of Our Faces Act (H.R. 7363), Sunset for the 2001 AUMF Act (H.R. 6751), Empowering App-Based Workers Act (H.R. 6646), Dignity for Detained Immigrants Act (H.R. 6397), Housing Is a Human Right Act of 2025 (H.R. 4457), and a resolution supporting a Transgender Bill of Rights (H.Res. 1058) [S3].
- Constituent services claims: over 130 town halls held; over $25 million in federal benefits recovered for constituents; over $70 million in new federal investments brought to the district (campaign statement, not independently verified) [S1].
- A Daily (UW) column notes that, despite a generally pro-Palestinian stance, she voted in favor of funding Israel's Iron Dome missile-defense system [S6].
- Campaign finance (FEC, 2026 cycle through 2026-03-31): receipts of about $2,014,218 and disbursements of about $2,053,009; roughly $887,298 in itemized and $829,020 in unitemized individual contributions, plus $234,500 from other political committees [S4].

## Endorsements

- Pamphlet lists: Washington State Labor Council, WFSE, UFCW 3000, NEA/WEA, Washington State Building & Construction Trades Council, Planned Parenthood, Sierra Club, and King County Democrats [S1].
- Fuse Washington endorsed her for CD 7 in its 2026 primary endorsements [S5].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race with a federal legislative record (nine years in the House) and committee/caucus leadership positions [S1][S3].
- Runs well to the left of the national Democratic median (Medicare for All, wealth tax, tuition-free college) while being challenged from further left by Kirkland and from the right by Sheth [S1][S6].
- Overwhelming resource advantage: roughly $2 million raised this cycle versus under $10,000 for any opponent [S4].
- Broad institutional labor and advocacy-organization endorsements; no comparable endorsements found for opponents [S1][S5].
