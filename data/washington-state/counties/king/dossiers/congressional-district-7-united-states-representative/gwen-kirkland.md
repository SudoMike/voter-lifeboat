## Background

Gwen Kirkland (prefers Democratic Party) is a political and labor organizer who moved to Washington from Missouri in 2023, which she attributes to escaping anti-transgender discrimination and seeking healthcare access [S1]. She works in grocery, has served as a union strike captain, and says she organized grocery-store coworkers to strike and advocated against corporate grocery mergers [S1]. She attended Ozarks Technical Community College and graduated from Oklahoma Christian University [S1]. She has not held elected office [S1]. FEC records list her as Gwenevere Josephine Kirkland, a Democratic challenger in WA-7 who filed her statement of candidacy on May 14, 2026, with the committee "Get Gwen in Congress" (she is her own treasurer); as of the records reviewed she is flagged as not having raised funds [S2].

## Positions

- **Healthcare**: Supports universal healthcare; says she has personally knocked thousands of doors for it [S1].
- **Education**: Supports free education for all [S1].
- **Public ownership**: Supports publicly owned utilities and publicly owned grocery stores [S1].
- **Labor**: Emphasizes union organizing, strikes, and opposition to corporate grocery mergers [S1].
- **Democratic Party reform**: Argues the Democratic Party "has zero interest in fighting the billionaires" and says her aim is to "take over the Democratic Party first"; frames incumbent leadership as failed [S1].
- **Accountability**: Says the result of her time in office "will see Trump and the Epstein Class in prison," citing her experience as a survivor of childhood sexual assault [S1].
