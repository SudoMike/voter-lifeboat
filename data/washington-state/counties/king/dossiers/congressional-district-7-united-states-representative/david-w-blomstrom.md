## Background

David W. Blomstrom is a perennial Seattle-area candidate running under the "Fifth Republic Party" preference, a party that appears to consist of Blomstrom himself [S3][S4]. He holds a BS in ecology from Western Washington University (Huxley, 1983) and attended the University of Alaska Fairbanks [S1][S3]. Ballotpedia lists U.S. Navy service from 1974 to 1977 and work as a wildlife biologist, teacher, writer, and website designer [S3]. He ran for Seattle School Board District 3 in 1999, 2007, and 2015 (defeated in the primary each time he appears in results), and for Governor of Washington in 2016 and 2020 [S3]. He submitted no elected experience, professional experience, or community service information to the pamphlet [S1]. No FEC candidate registration or campaign-finance filings were found for him as of 2026-07-16; his website footer states "Paid for by David Blomstrom for Congress" [S2][S5].

## Positions

- **Antisemitic platform**: His pamphlet statement opens with the term "Jewarchy" (his coinage from a 2016 pamphlet statement), and his campaign website organizes its issue list around claims of "Jewish corruption," stating "We will never fix any major problems until we deal with our Jewish problem" [S1][S2]. A Daily (UW) column reports that his website praises Adolf Hitler and that he self-published a 2020 ebook titled "Jew Flu: The Coronavirus Conspiracy" [S4].
- **Israel/Iran**: Describes himself as "staunchly pro-Iran and anti-Israel"; favors cutting all ties with Israel and withdrawing recognition of it as a sovereign state [S1].
- **Environment**: Calls the environment his "#1 issue" and his website's lead theme is "Earth First," putting "the natural world... before corporate profits" [S1][S2].
- **Economy**: Proposes replacing "gangster capitalism" with a Chinese-style "mixed economy" [S1].
- **Military/healthcare**: Proposes "downsizing our obese military so we can have free health care" [S1].
- **Labor**: Says workers deserve transparent, accountable unions and alleges uninvestigated signature forgery by a local corporation [S1].
- **Education**: Criticizes Seattle public schools and "derelict school principals," an issue he says he first raised in his 1999 campaign [S1].
