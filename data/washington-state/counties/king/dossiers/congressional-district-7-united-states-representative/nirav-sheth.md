## Background

Nirav Sheth (prefers Republican Party) is a first-time officeholder candidate who immigrated from India, arriving in the U.S. in 2008 to study computer and electrical engineering [S1][S2]. He served in the U.S. Marines from 2011 to 2014 (honorable discharge), working as an aircraft mechanic and military police officer, and volunteered as an intelligence liaison with U.S. Border Patrol in Yuma, Arizona during his enlistment [S1][S2]. He was a police officer with the Lakewood, WA police department starting in 2017, resigning due to partial hearing loss [S1]. He is Chief Financial Officer of Sheth Groups (Meineke franchises, 2017–present) and co-owned the Bistro Baffi restaurant from 2018 to 2025 [S1]. His website says he overcame homelessness as a college student before becoming a Pierce County business owner [S2]. He holds an associate degree in electrical engineering and computer science, with additional training in airframe mechanics, business administration, and human-resource management [S1]. FEC records show he first registered as a House candidate in July 2023 for the 2024 cycle in Washington's 10th district and is now filed as a challenger in the 7th district for 2026 [S3].

## Positions

- **Public safety**: Campaigns on "bringing law & order back to Seattle"; supports funding law enforcement and emergency services [S1][S2].
- **Housing/homelessness**: Says his goal is "sustainable & affordable housing to solve homelessness" and "Real Solutions to help homeless people" [S1].
- **Cost of living/economy**: Cites higher cost of living and energy costs as signs the district is "at a breaking point"; emphasizes economic growth and small-business support [S1][S2].
- **Energy**: Supports "Energy Independence" and investment in electric-grid resilience [S1][S2].
- **Fiscal**: Promises "military-grade accountability" in Washington, D.C. and careful stewardship of tax dollars, citing budget management in the Marines and business [S1].
- **Infrastructure**: Supports investment in transportation systems [S2].
- **Immigration**: His website promotes legal immigration pathways and "fairness, opportunity, and diversity" [S2]; a Daily (UW) column reports he has posted content opposing the constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship, describing him as balancing right-wing anti-immigration rhetoric with recognition of immigrants' role in the workforce [S4].
- **Education**: Emphasizes collaboration with parents and educators on "critical thinking skills and independent thought" [S2].

## Record

- No prior elected office [S1].
- Campaign finance (FEC, 2026 cycle through 2026-06-30): receipts of about $6,811 (roughly $250 itemized and $661 unitemized individual contributions), disbursements of about $5,525; he serves as his own treasurer [S3].

## Endorsements

No endorsements listed on his website or found in coverage reviewed [S2].

## Scoring notes

- The only Republican-preference candidate in the race; a Daily (UW) column characterizes him as the likely consolidation point for the district's Republican-leaning voters (historically roughly 15% of the vote against Jayapal) [S4].
- Emphasizes public safety, policing experience, and cost-of-living relief, contrasting with both Democratic-preference candidates' healthcare- and labor-focused platforms [S1][S2].
- Military and law-enforcement background is unique in this field [S1].
- Minimal fundraising (under $7,000) against an incumbent who has raised about $2 million this cycle [S3].
