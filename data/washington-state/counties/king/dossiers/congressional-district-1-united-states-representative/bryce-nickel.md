## Background

Bryce Nickel, 41, lives in Marysville and works as a paraeducator in the
Marysville School District [S1][S2]. He describes himself as a
developmentally disabled self-advocate who has experienced homelessness and
poverty firsthand [S1]. He holds a Bachelor of Science in Business
Administration from Central Washington University and lists postgraduate
studies in Accessibility and ITAM [S1].

He is a repeat candidate: he ran for the Washington House (District 38,
Position 1) in 2024 and for Everett City Council (District 7) in 2023,
losing in the primary both times [S1][S4]. He also lists a prior candidacy
for a PUD Board seat, and service as a precinct committee officer and party
delegate [S1].

His civic and professional affiliations center on disability and housing
advocacy: committee member of the Snohomish Coordinated Entry Advisory
Board and Housing Hope Policy Committee; board member of Homes and Hope
Community Land Trust, Northwest Community Land Trust Coalition, and the
Autism Society of Washington; fellow of the Institute for a Democratic
Future; and volunteer roles with Xbox Autism initiatives, Washington Autism
Alliance, Take This, and Special Olympics Washington [S1].

His listed campaign web presence is a Linktree page (linktr.ee/brycenickel)
[S1]; it could not be retrieved during research, and no standalone campaign
website was found. As of July 2026 he had not filed a campaign financial
report, and a search of the FEC candidate database found no registration
under his name [S2][S3].

## Positions

- **Healthcare:** Supports Medicare for All / a universal healthcare model,
  framing healthcare as a human right and opposing insurance-profit-driven
  care [S1][S2].
- **Housing/homelessness:** Supports fully funding Section 8 and building
  large-scale affordable housing; says housing should not be treated as a
  speculative commodity [S1]. Told the Herald he would also reduce taxes
  and fees to encourage builders to develop more housing [S2].
- **Economic security:** Supports a universal basic income and a federal
  jobs guarantee [S1]. States that artificial intelligence and automation
  should be nationalized so the technologies benefit the public rather than
  shareholders [S1]; told the Herald he would tax automation/AI companies
  and raise corporate tax rates to fund programs [S2].
- **Disability policy:** Wants to remove income caps on Social Security
  disability income, increase funding for support personnel such as
  paraeducators, and increase federal matching funds for home care services
  [S2].
- **Immigration:** Says ICE is overfunded; wants to "support immigration as
  much as possible while still policing criminal activity" [S2].
- **Climate:** Describes it as a lower priority for his campaign but
  supports environmental regulations and access to air conditioning for
  disabled and low-income families [S2].
- **Labor/taxation:** Calls for strengthening unions, investing in public
  services, and "taking on extreme wealth" [S1].
