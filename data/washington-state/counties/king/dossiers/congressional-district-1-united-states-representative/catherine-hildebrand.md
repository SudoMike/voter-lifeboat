## Background

Catherine Hildebrand, 34, is a travel agent from Mountlake Terrace running as a
Democrat ("Prefers Democratic Party") in the seven-candidate WA-01 primary
challenging incumbent Suzan DelBene [S5]. She owns a small travel agency and
has worked as a certified personal trainer for nine years [S1]. This is her
first government campaign [S1]. She was born and raised in Seattle and now
lives just north in Snohomish County [S4].

She holds a BA from Gonzaga University (magna cum laude, 2014) and an MM from
the Seattle Film Institute (2016) [S1]. She currently serves as President of
the Seattle Civic Band, served several years on the parish council of St.
Matthew Catholic Church in Seattle, and has volunteered in various roles
across the Seattle Archdiocese [S1]. She describes herself as one of the
"young progressives" running for office nationwide [S1].

Her stated top priorities are climate change, urbanization, affordable
housing, and universal health care [S5].

## Positions

All positions below are campaign statements ("says she will"); as a
first-time candidate she has no legislative record.

### Health care
- Supports Medicare for All, framing health care as "a human right, not an
  industry"; opposes medical patents and profit-driven pharmaceutical and
  insurance companies [S3].
- Supports implementing a universal health care system [S5]; says people
  should be assured that if laid off they can still afford medicine, and that
  a doctor visit should not risk bankruptcy [S1].

### Government reform
- Supports term limits in all branches of government [S1][S3].
- Characterizes lobbying as "bribery" and calls for eliminating it, citing
  its role in tax code complexity and fragmented health insurance coverage
  [S3]; calls for ending "multi-million dollar political campaigns" [S1].
- Calls for enshrining "rights of the people over companies" in the
  Constitution [S1].

### Climate, energy, and environment
- Would increase EPA funding and enforcement, expand protected land across
  the country, and impose stricter environmental protections [S3][S5].
- Supports investing in renewable energy and rebuilding the national grid
  [S3].
- Supports increased spending on public transportation and interstate train
  travel over highway expansion to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, saying
  "We could be creating a new project like an interstate rail system" [S5].

### Housing and affordability
- Supports federal funds to build subsidized housing to address the housing
  shortage [S5].
- Supports government-run grocery stores and expansion of food programs such
  as SNAP to address affordability [S5].
- States that housing, food, clean air, and safe water are rights [S1][S4].

### Education
- Proposes increased Department of Education funding for school districts,
  federal student loan forgiveness, and free higher education at public
  colleges and universities [S3].

### Guns
- Supports mandatory firearm registration, owner licensing with testing,
  safe-storage requirements, background checks for all sales, and bans on
  ghost guns and assault weapons implemented through buyback programs [S3].

### Immigration
- Supports major immigration reform to simplify the process so immigrants
  can "come here safely and live, work, and participate in society" [S3];
  opposes current enforcement practices, calling them "barbaric" [S5].

### Foreign policy and defense
- Would substantially reduce defense spending [S5].
- Her pamphlet statement opposes what she describes as "an unauthorized war
  that has only brought death and disaster" [S1].

## Endorsements

No endorsements found. The Everett Herald reported no endorsements as of
July 15, 2026 [S5], and her campaign website lists none [S2].
