## Background

Benjamin Kincaid (Prefers Democratic Party) is a first-time candidate for U.S. Representative in Congressional District 1, challenging incumbent Suzan DelBene in the August 2026 top-two primary [S1][S3]. He has no elected experience and presents that as an asset, arguing that "every career politician in this race has had years in office to solve the problems facing this district" and that the problems remain [S1]. He describes himself as "a working American with a blue collar job and a regular paycheck" [S1]. His listed education is Iowa Central Community College [S1]. On his campaign website he identifies as a "moderate reform Democrat" emphasizing "practical solutions first" and measurable results over ideology, and says he does not take corporate PAC money [S3]. His stated rationale for running centers on scrutiny of the incumbent's twelve-year record, including the claim that her net worth has more than doubled in office [S3]. The campaign states it holds no public rallies or door-to-door canvassing, citing security concerns [S3].

## Positions

### Housing, homelessness, and energy
- Proposes federal investment in "next generation housing manufacturing" that he says would build homes faster, cheaper, and with 70% less energy consumption than conventional construction, treating the housing crisis, the energy crisis, and rising electricity demand from AI and data centers as one connected problem [S1].
- Frames homelessness as three distinct crises — addiction, severe mental illness, and economic hardship — arguing each requires a different policy response, with "real accountability rather than endless spending with no results" [S1][S4].

### Taxes and economy
- Would eliminate federal income tax and filing requirements for Americans earning $61,000 or less; his website says this covers roughly 50–60 percent of U.S. tax filers while representing about 3–4 percent of federal individual income tax revenue [S1][S4].
- Opposes what he calls H-1B visa abuse and calls for accountability from major tech companies on layoffs and worker displacement [S4].
- Opposes tariffs on Chinese cars exceeding 100 percent, arguing they function as prohibitions and harm consumer choice [S4].
- Proposes a "Bellingham to Anchorage" (B2A) railway infrastructure project [S4].

### Healthcare
- Supports a "real world pilot program to test universal healthcare before any national rollout" and opposes the government forcing an end to private health insurance [S1]; on his website this takes the form of a proposed "Public Option Healthcare Innovation and Pilot Act" (POHIPA) [S4].

### Public safety and guns
- Proposes a federal Secure Storage Law targeting the roughly 200,000 firearms he says are stolen every year and fuel street crime [S1].
- Website proposals include a "Stalking Victim Protection & Accountability Act" and a "Nurse Assault Prevention & Accountability Act" [S4].

### Gender and women's spaces
- States that "men should not be in women's sports or women's prisons" and supports "the protection of women only spaces" [S1]; his website expands this into a "women's safety and fairness" agenda including sex-based privacy protections and a review of sex offender residency laws [S4].

### Education
- Proposes a "Civic Service Academy" concept aimed at failing schools [S2] and a national security-clearance-style vetting program for teachers and school staff, along with school safety protocols, both framed as pilot/beta-test programs [S4].

### Defense and foreign policy
- Argues the defense budget can "spend less and get more and better resources" through efficiency [S4].
- Supports Ukraine, stating "every nation that loves freedom should support Ukraine" [S1].
- States "Taiwan is not part of China" [S1].
- Supports "the people of Iran against the Regime" [S1], with a website "Plan for a Free Iran" opposing gender apartheid [S4].
- States "zero tolerance for antisemitism" [S1] and proposes a White House Office to Combat Antisemitism [S4].

### Ideological framing
- States "Socialism doesn't work" [S1] and criticizes the incumbent's alignment with what he calls "Democratic Socialists" as "too extreme and too fringe" [S2].
- Campaign slogan: "People Over Politics" [S1].

## Scoring notes

- First-time candidate with no elected or governmental record; explicitly campaigns on outsider status and a blue-collar, non-millionaire identity against a long-serving incumbent [S1][S3].
- Runs to the incumbent's right on several social issues within the Democratic primary (women's sports/spaces, homelessness accountability, anti-socialism framing) while proposing a large working-class tax exemption and a public-option healthcare pilot [S1][S4].
- Distinctive policy signature is pilot-program incrementalism: test universal healthcare, school programs, and other ideas before national rollout [S1][S4].
