## Background

Hunter Gordon, 28, is a home care aide from Redmond running as a Democrat ("Prefers Democratic Party") for U.S. Representative in Washington's 1st Congressional District [S1][S4]. He is a first-time candidate and a union member [S1][S2]. For more than five years he has worked as a home care aide, caring for his brother, who has Down syndrome and depends on Medicaid, and supporting another client with long-term care needs; he cites this experience with the healthcare system as his reason for running [S1]. He holds a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Western Washington University, where he participated in LEAD (Learning, Environment, Action, Discovery), a graduate student-led habitat-restoration program in northern Washington [S1]. He filed his statement of candidacy with the FEC on November 17, 2025 [S3]. He is challenging incumbent Rep. Suzan DelBene (D) in a seven-candidate August 4 primary; press coverage describes DelBene as heavily favored in the Democratic-leaning district [S4].

## Positions

### Healthcare
- Supports Medicare for All / a publicly funded universal healthcare system [S1][S2][S4].
- Opposes any further cuts to Medicaid and Medicare, citing Trump-administration moves to cut Medicaid; says restoring cuts alone is not enough: "We can't just be fighting to simply restore these cuts that Donald Trump has done" [S1][S4].

### Taxes and the economy
- Calls for a "billionaires tax" with rigorous tax enforcement against the top 1% [S1]; on his website frames this as making "billionaires illegal" through wealth taxes on wealth over $1 billion [S2], and told the Everett Herald he wants "to tax the billionaire class out of existence" [S4].
- Supports a much higher federal minimum wage tied to productivity [S1]; his website specifies $25/hour [S2], and he notes the federal minimum has been $7.25 since 2009 [S4].
- Supports stronger union protections [S2] and an end to "the corporate takeover of our politics" [S1].

### Housing
- Supports national rent stabilization, major public housing investment, and limits on Wall Street ownership of single-family homes [S1]; his website calls for national rent control and corporate taxes to fund affordable-housing construction [S2]; he would prohibit speculative home purchases by investment groups [S4].

### Technology
- Would seek to hold big tech accountable, regulate AI, and halt data center construction while protecting tech workers and creating union jobs [S1]; supports breaking up tech monopolies, naming Apple, Google, Meta, Amazon, and Microsoft [S2].

### Foreign policy
- Calls for halting all U.S. funding and military aid to Israel, characterizing Israel's actions in the conflict as genocide [S2][S4].

### Immigration
- Supports abolishing ICE [S2][S4], advocating "humane immigration that understands these are human people" [S4].

### Climate and transit
- Supports clean energy investment and expanded public transit [S4].

### Reproductive rights and LGBTQ issues
- Would fight to codify Roe, defend gender-affirming care, and protect bodily autonomy [S1]; website lists protecting trans rights and abortion access [S2].

### Democracy and accountability
- Names impeaching President Trump as a top priority [S2][S4]; also calls for reforming the Democratic Party [S2].

### Campaign finance
- States his campaign accepts no corporate PAC money, no AIPAC contributions, and no donations from big tech companies [S1].

## Record

No public-office record; first-time candidate [S1]. Campaign finance (FEC, coverage through March 31, 2026): total receipts $68,971.60, of which $23,471.60 was individual contributions and $44,250 candidate loans; disbursements $51,291.23; cash on hand $17,680.37; outstanding candidate-loan debt of $44,250 [S3].

## Endorsements

Per the Everett Herald, Gordon has been endorsed by AIPAC Tracker, an organization that opposes candidates who receive funding from pro-Israel groups, and by Voice of the Mountain, a progressive community newspaper in Marysville [S4]. No endorsements are listed on his campaign website as of 2026-07-16 [S2].

## Scoring notes

- Runs to the left of incumbent DelBene: Medicare for All, wealth taxes aimed at eliminating billionaires, a $25 federal minimum wage, national rent control, abolishing ICE, and ending all U.S. aid to Israel [S1][S2][S4].
- Frames the race around personal caregiving experience: five-plus years as a home care aide for a brother on Medicaid, contrasted with a healthcare system he says is "designed to take your money and wear you down" [S1].
- Explicitly contrasts himself with DelBene's wealth and tech background, describing her on his website as "the 9th richest member of Congress" and a former tech executive [S2]; refuses corporate PAC, AIPAC, and big-tech money [S1].
- Small campaign treasury largely self-funded via candidate loans ($44,250 of $68,972 total receipts) [S3], in a race press coverage says heavily favors the incumbent [S4].
