## Background

Sandra Stephens is a retired registered nurse running for State Senator in Legislative District 46; she prefers the Republican Party [S1]. She graduated in 1978 with a Bachelor of Science in nursing from Oregon Health Sciences University [S1]. Her career included work as a Med-Surg nurse in hospitals, mental health home care centers, stroke centers, clinics, and community health centers, and sixteen years as charge nurse at UW Northwest Hospital [S1]. Her only listed elected experience is election to the National Association of Pro-Life Nurses for two terms (1988–1992) [S1]. She describes herself as a grandmother who raised seven children, and lists community service including hospital boards, feeding the homeless, volunteering at youth prisons, and Salvation Army Emergency Response Teams [S1]. She lists no campaign website [S1].

## Positions

- **Social issues:** States a belief in "the sacredness of life" and individual freedom of conscience [S1].
- **Taxes and government accountability:** Attributes rising taxes and declining services to what she characterizes as theft of taxpayer funds through unaudited nonprofits channeled to politicians; describes the Washington Democratic Party as "a criminal syndicate" [S1].
- **General platform:** Says the King County Republican Party platform is "a good representation of my policies" [S1].

## Record

No legislative or other public-office record; her listed elected experience is within a professional nursing association [S1].

## Endorsements

King County Republican Party, per her pamphlet statement [S1].
