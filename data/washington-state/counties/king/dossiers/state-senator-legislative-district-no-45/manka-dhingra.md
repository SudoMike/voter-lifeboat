## Background

Manka Dhingra is the incumbent State Senator for Washington's 45th Legislative District (D-Redmond), first elected to the seat in November 2017 [S3][S5]. She was the first Sikh elected to any state legislature in the United States [S5]. She serves as Deputy Majority Leader of the Washington State Senate and chairs the Senate Law & Justice Committee; she also sits on the Ways & Means and Environment, Energy & Technology committees [S1][S4]. Before entering the legislature she spent 20 years as a Senior Deputy Prosecuting Attorney in King County, where she chaired the Therapeutic Alternative Unit, and served for 10 years as a Crisis Intervention Training instructor for the Washington Criminal Justice Training Commission [S1]. She holds a J.D. from the University of Washington and a B.A. in History and Political Science from UC Berkeley [S1]. Her community service includes serving on the Hopelink board and co-founding API Chaya, an organization supporting survivors of gender-based violence [S1].

## Positions

- Public safety and justice: says she has led efforts to strengthen public safety, improve victim services, strengthen accountability, and expand crisis response systems, working alongside survivors, law enforcement, and community organizations [S1]; her campaign site highlights police accountability measures, including co-sponsoring SB 5066 on officer intervention requirements (record) [S3].
- Behavioral health: emphasizes expanding behavioral health care and connecting people experiencing mental illness and addiction with treatment [S1]; previously chaired the Senate Behavioral Health Subcommittee (2019-2021) (record) [S3].
- Healthcare: says she will work to improve healthcare access, including support for community health centers [S1][S3].
- Reproductive rights and civil rights: says she has led efforts to protect reproductive freedom and defend civil rights [S1].
- Economy and workers: pledges to defend workers, seniors, small businesses, and immigrants; supports responsible investment in transportation and continued advancement of social equity [S1]; campaign site lists workforce development and labor partnerships as priorities [S3].
- Climate: pledges to "fight for a safe and livable planet" [S1].

## Record

- Incumbent State Senator (45th LD) since 2017; Deputy Majority Leader; Chair of the Senate Law & Justice Committee; member of Ways & Means and Environment, Energy & Technology; serves on joint committees on topics including trafficking, poverty reduction, crisis response, substance use recovery, and missing and murdered Indigenous persons [S1][S4].
- Has sponsored and passed legislation on domestic violence and sexual assault, firearm violence prevention, property tax relief for seniors and people with disabilities, financial fraud prosecution, and criminal justice reform [S5].
- Former Senior Deputy Prosecuting Attorney, King County (20 years); chaired the office's Therapeutic Alternative Unit [S1].
- Recognitions cited by her campaign include the NAACP President's Award, the American Psychiatric Association's Jacob K. Javits Award (2022), WACOPS Legislator of the Year (2019), and NAMI Behavioral Health Champion (2019, 2021) [S3].

## Endorsements

Her pamphlet statement lists endorsements from Planned Parenthood, One America Votes, Washington Conservation Action, labor unions, nurses, and community leaders across the Eastside [S1]. Her campaign website also lists IAM 751 (machinists/aerospace workers) and nursing associations [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Incumbent with senate leadership roles (Deputy Majority Leader, Law & Justice chair) and a legislative record centered on behavioral health, public safety, and justice policy [S1][S3][S4].
- Prosecutorial background combined with therapeutic-alternatives and crisis-intervention work is the throughline of her stated policy focus [S1].
