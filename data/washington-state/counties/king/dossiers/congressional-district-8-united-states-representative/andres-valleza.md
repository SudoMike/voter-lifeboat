## Background

- Republican-preference candidate; only elected experience is high school freshman class president; calls himself "a public servant, not a politician" [S1].
- Born in Sunnyside, WA; grew up in Granite Falls; B.A. in Political Science from Washington State University (2018) [S1][S4].
- Career in frontline social services and corrections: worked in group homes with teenagers, in mental health and developmental disability support, in schools across Washington, and as a parole officer in both the juvenile and adult systems [S1][S2][S4].

## Positions

- Campaign platform rests on three pillars — "Justice. Accountability. Affordability." [S1][S2]:
- **Public safety / justice:** Supports federal prosecutions for violent gang and gun crimes, drawing on his corrections experience; supports the Second Amendment [S1][S2].
- **Government accountability:** Wants to restore trust through federal audits, including audits and investigations of Washington state government "waste, mismanagement, and broken systems" [S1][S2].
- **Affordability / taxes:** Supports permanently securing tax relief for seniors and working Americans; site adds eliminating capital gains taxes [S1][S2].
- **Economy / energy:** Supports investments in reliable energy and trades-profession apprenticeships, and creating "high-paying, AI proof jobs" [S1][S2].
- **Anti-establishment framing:** Describes himself as the underdog with "no establishment backing, no flashy ads, no big money," emphasizing "principles over party" [S1].

## Endorsements

None found. His pamphlet statement itself notes he has "no establishment backing" [S1].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate in the field with direct experience inside the criminal-justice system (parole officer, juvenile detention, group homes), which shapes his public-safety-first platform [S1][S2].
- Smallest reported campaign among FEC-registered challengers: about $18,000 raised and roughly $400 cash on hand as of March 31, 2026 [S3].
- Unlike Meline and Hagglund, has no county Republican Party organization endorsements; the Douglas County GOP considered him but endorsed Hagglund [S5].
- Pitches generational change ("a new generation of principled, common sense leadership") and eastern/rural roots (Sunnyside, Granite Falls) rather than business or party credentials [S1].
