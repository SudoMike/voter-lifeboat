## Background

- Republican-preference candidate and long-time Republican Party officer: Precinct Committee Officer since 2017, county Legislative District Chairman 2018–2022, county (Snohomish) Republican Party Chairman 2022–2023 [S1][S4].
- Born and raised in Everett in a working-class family; lives in Granite Falls with his wife [S2][S5].
- Four decades of professional experience in science, engineering, data science, machine learning, and business intelligence; commercial clients have included Amazon, Microsoft, Nike, UnitedHealth, and Kaiser; government clients include King County Metro, Apple Health, Medicare/Medicaid, Veterans Affairs, and the Department of Defense [S1]. Described in news coverage as a 60-year-old healthcare data scientist who previously worked for UnitedHealthcare [S4][S5].
- Repeat statewide/countywide candidate: ran in the 2023 Snohomish County Executive primary and placed third in the 2024 lieutenant governor primary with 319,071 votes; has also campaigned for Secretary of State [S4][S5].
- Community service: Scouting troop committee chairman and assistant scoutmaster; election observer [S1].

## Positions

- **Cost of living:** Cites high costs of housing, food, energy, and healthcare as the central problem; wants voters to "keep more of your hard-earned money" [S1][S2].
- **Economy:** Favors "innovative, free-market solutions proven in the real world"; platform emphasizes reducing costs and local job growth [S1][S2].
- **Energy/environment:** Supports clean energy and "environmental leadership without punishing working families" [S2].
- **Education:** Supports empowering parents and local communities over bureaucracies [S2].
- **Public safety:** Supports strong policing and opposes lenient prosecutors [S2].
- **Immigration:** Supports border security while protecting agricultural communities [S2].
- **Foreign policy:** "Peace through strength" with American interests prioritized [S2].
- **Style:** "America First" framing combined with a stated commitment to finding common ground, accessibility, and accountability; criticizes politicians "obsessed with... generating clicks and likes" [S1].

## Endorsements

- Douglas County Republican Party (endorsed April 6 by an 11–1 vote of precinct committee officers, with two abstentions, over Meline, Valleza, and Ha) [S5].
- Snohomish County Republican Party, per his campaign site [S2]. (Note: Spencer Meline's pamphlet statement also claims a Snohomish County Republican Party endorsement; the two claims could not be reconciled from available sources.)
- Pamphlet states endorsements include "multiple Legislative District and County Party committees on both sides of the mountains" [S1].

## Scoring notes

- The most party-establishment-connected Republican in the field (former county GOP chairman, multiple prior campaigns), directly contrasting with Meline's and Valleza's outsider framing [S1][S4][S5].
- Has run for office repeatedly without winning (county executive 2023, lieutenant governor 2024), which gives him name-recognition experience but no elected record [S4][S5].
- Distinct emphasis among the Republicans on clean energy and data-driven problem solving, reflecting his data-science career [S1][S2].
- No campaign-finance totals reported to the FEC for the 2026 cycle as of mid-July 2026, suggesting minimal fundraising compared with Meline and Ha [S3].
