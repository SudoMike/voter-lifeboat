## Background

- Republican-preference candidate; first run for office ("I'm not a career politician") [S1].
- Vietnamese-American; her family fled communist Vietnam after her father, a South Vietnamese intelligence officer, spent seven years in labor camps; the family came to the U.S. in 1995 through a humanitarian program for survivors of communist persecution [S1][S2][S5].
- Ph.D. in Comparative Literature from UC Berkeley; B.A. from UC Irvine; taught history and literature at Berkeley and managed a scholarly journal at UC Press [S1].
- Federal consultant with the Department of Veterans Affairs for six years (2019–2025 per her site), working on fraud prevention and caregiver support; says she helped recover millions of dollars in taxpayer funds and expanded veterans' access to education; also worked with DOJ, Education, Treasury, HUD, and the Social Security Administration [S1][S2].
- Volunteers with the US-Vietnam Research Center on Vietnam War research and events [S1].

## Positions

- **Cost of living:** Says gas, groceries, housing, childcare, and healthcare are slipping out of reach for working families; running "to lower costs for families" [S1].
- **Economy:** Supports small businesses and farmers; site frames this as a "People-First Economy" [S1][S2].
- **Public safety and immigration:** Wants to strengthen public safety and secure the border; site lists "Safe Streets" and "Smart Immigration" [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Wants to "restore focus on core academics"; site lists "Strong Schools" and parental engagement themes [S1][S2].
- **Veterans:** Emphasizes veteran services, drawing on her VA consulting work [S1][S2].
- **Values framing:** Campaign centers on "faith, freedom, family, personal responsibility, and service to others" and the theme "Defend Freedom. Empower Families. Renew America." [S1][S2].
- **Term limits:** Signed the U.S. Term Limits pledge to support congressional term limits [S6].

## Endorsements

- Federation of Asian American Voters [S3].
- Campaign endorsements page features testimonials from a former naval officer, community figures, and local officials, but does not list major institutional or newspaper endorsements [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Only challenger in the field with federal-agency work experience (VA fraud investigation and program consulting), which she presents as insider knowledge of government waste [S1][S2].
- Personal refugee narrative — freedom lost "slowly, then all at once" — anchors an anti-communist, pro-limited-government framing distinct from the other Republicans' small-business or public-safety framings [S1].
- Second-best-funded Republican in the race: roughly $103,000 raised and $83,000 cash on hand as of March 31, 2026 — competitive with Meline among challengers but a small fraction of the incumbent's war chest [S4].
- Unlike Meline and Hagglund, no county Republican Party organization endorsements identified; her listed support is from an Asian American voter federation and individual testimonials [S3].
