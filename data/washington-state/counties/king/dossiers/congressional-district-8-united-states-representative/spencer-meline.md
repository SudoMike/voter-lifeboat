## Background

- Republican-preference candidate; no prior elected office [S1].
- Small business owner and restaurateur in Leavenworth, Washington; built multiple businesses, managing employees, payroll, budgets, permitting, and daily operations [S1]. (A Douglas County GOP post describes him as a small business owner "from Cashmere," a neighboring town [S4].)
- Completed coursework at Edmonds Community College while pursuing business opportunities [S1].
- Husband and father of four, raised in Washington; his campaign emphasizes his faith and outdoor recreation across the district [S1][S2].
- Community involvement: provides meals to the community through his business; supports local schools, youth activities, churches, and community events [S1].

## Positions

- **Cost of living:** Core message is that housing, groceries, fuel, and insurance costs are pushing the American Dream out of reach; priorities are lowering costs and supporting family-wage jobs [S1][S2].
- **Small business:** Says small businesses are closing at alarming rates; wants federal policy shaped by real-world business experience [S1][S2].
- **Public safety:** "Protecting our communities" / "Safe Communities" is a listed priority [S1][S2].
- **Immigration:** Supports keeping the border secure; site frames it as "Smart & Secure Immigration" [S1][S2].
- **Public lands:** Supports "responsibly managing our public lands for future generations" [S1][S2].
- **Education:** Supports parental rights in education [S2].
- **Anti-politician framing:** "I'm not a politician" — positions himself as a citizen-candidate against career politicians [S1][S2].

## Endorsements

- King County Republican Party [S1][S2].
- Snohomish County Republican Party [S1][S2]. (Note: Bob Hagglund's campaign also claims a Snohomish County Republican Party endorsement; the guide could not reconcile the two claims from available sources.)

## Scoring notes

- The best-funded Republican challenger: about $126,000 raised and $103,000 cash on hand as of June 30, 2026 [S3].
- Only challenger endorsed by the King County Republican Party — notable since King County casts a large share of the district's votes [S1].
- Runs on private-sector, small-business credentials rather than government or party-organization experience, contrasting with Ha (federal consultant), Hagglund (party officer), and Valleza (corrections) [S1][S4].
- Lives on the east side of the Cascades (Leavenworth), giving him a geographic base in the district's rural half [S1].
