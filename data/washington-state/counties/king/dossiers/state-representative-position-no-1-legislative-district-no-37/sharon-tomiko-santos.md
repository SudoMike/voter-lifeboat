## Background

Incumbent State Representative for LD 37, serving since 1999 [S3]. The
first Japanese American woman elected to a mainland state legislature [S2].
Chairs the House Education Committee; member of the Finance and Consumer
Protection & Business committees [S1]. Professional background in nonprofit
governance (founded three nonprofits) and local government policy [S1]. BA
from The Evergreen State College; MA from Northeastern University; Franklin
High School graduate [S1]. Community roles include board member of
Neighborhood House and founding board member of OneAmerica (Hate Free Zone)
and KUOW Puget Sound Public Radio [S1].

## Positions

- **Education:** As House Education Chair, focused on accessible quality
  education and, per her campaign, a "fundamental reimagining" of education
  delivery, childcare affordability, and workforce pathways [S1][S2].
- **Cost of living/taxes:** Lowering cost of living, tax fairness for
  working families, protecting essential services [S1].
- **Housing:** Increasing housing supply; expanding affordable housing near
  transit and preventing displacement [S1][S2].
- **Public safety:** Community-based violence prevention and expanded
  behavioral health services [S2].
- **Transportation:** Reliable transit infrastructure and pedestrian safety
  [S2].
