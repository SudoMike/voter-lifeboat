## Background

- Incumbent State Representative for the 12th Legislative District, first elected in 2016 and now seeking a sixth term in Position 2 [S1][S4]. Past Deputy Minority Leader of the state House [S1].
- Lives in Chelan (age 43); previously served two terms on the Chelan City Council [S1][S4].
- Executive Director of the Lake Chelan Chamber of Commerce (20+ years) and CFO of his family's sixth-generation apple and cherry orchard, in operation over 100 years [S1][S3][S4].
- Earlier career: White House Office of Political Affairs and staff to Washington State House Republican leadership [S1].
- Education: Pacific Lutheran University, degrees in Business Administration and Political Science; salutatorian, Manson High School [S1].

## Positions

- **Small business/economy:** lower taxes and reduce red tape; cites health insurance costs, regulatory burdens, and B&O taxes as small-business pain points [S2][S3][S4].
- **Budget/taxes:** says the state has a spending problem, not a revenue problem; favors balanced budgets protecting taxpayers [S2][S4].
- **Education:** wants to move K-12 from "factory style" education toward hands-on, competency-based learning; supports career/technical training expansion [S2][S4].
- **Healthcare:** expand affordable options with price transparency and cost control [S2].
- **Public safety:** opposes decriminalization; emphasizes personal responsibility for offenders and improving officer-community relationships [S4].
- **Transportation/infrastructure:** prioritizes fixing roads, naming U.S. 2, Highway 522, Highway 18, and the I-90 corridor [S4].
- **Environment:** frames stewardship around comprehensive protections and forest-fire prevention [S4].

## Record

- Ranking (minority-lead) member of the House Capital Budget Committee for nine years; also serves on K-12 Education and Consumer Protection & Business committees [S2][S4]. Calls the capital budget "the only bipartisan, unanimous budget" in the state [S4].
- Official site cites a bill modernizing shared business space regulations signed into law, and over $32 million secured for district projects in the 2026 supplemental capital budget [S3].
- Campaign finance: $84,999 in itemized contributions across 137 records for the 2026 cycle as of 2026-07-16; top donors include the House Republican Organizing Committee ($5,000), the Snoqualmie Tribe, Regence, ABC PAC WA, and the WA Association of Nurse Anesthetists PAC [S5]. HeraldNet reported $53,849 raised as of June 30, 2026 [S4].

## Endorsements

- Washington State Labor Council, House Republican Organizational Committee, Washington State Medical Association, Associated General Contractors of Washington, and APRNs of Washington State [S4].

## Scoring notes

- The incumbent, running on a bipartisan-dealmaker record centered on the capital budget rather than on ideological positioning; his cross-spectrum endorsements (state labor council plus business and medical associations) are unusual for a Republican and distinguish him from his GOP challenger [S4].
- Deep North Central Washington roots (Chelan, orcharding, chamber of commerce); the district now also includes eastern King County communities, where he has held town halls (e.g., North Bend) [S1][S3].
- Substantially out-fundraised by fellow Republican Adam James (~$344k vs. ~$85k on PDC records), making this a rare incumbent-at-financial-disadvantage primary [S5].
- Positions overlap with James on taxes/spending and public safety, but Steele does not campaign on election-law changes, school choice, or social/religious themes that define James's platform [S2][S4].
