## Background

Rodney 'Star' Thornley is a first-time candidate (no elected experience) for
State Representative, 46th Legislative District Position 2, who states a
preference for the "Independent Party" [S1][S3]. He reports 35 years with
the Lake Washington Education Association, service as a two-time state WEA
representative, and long involvement in sports officiating and water ski
organizations, including past two-term president and current secretary of
the PNBOA officials association with 25 years of board service, and
presidencies of the Pacific Northwest Water Ski Association and Lake
Washington Ski Club [S1]. His campaign website adds that he is a University
of Washington graduate and Roosevelt High School alumnus with decades of
coaching at Roosevelt HS and long service with a Pacific NW basketball
officials association [S2]. He attended Seattle Central Community College,
Seattle Pacific College, and the University of Washington [S1]. Community
service listed includes Husky football/basketball first aid volunteering,
First Aid/CPR/AED instruction, driving for the Seattle Seafair Scholarship
Program, coordinating an inner-city youth water ski program, and
coordinating "Seattle Night Out" in his University District neighborhood
[S1].

## Positions

- **Governing approach (says):** Running as an independent because "Olympia
  needs a culture change"; argues too many officials follow party leadership
  rather than the people they serve [S1]. Campaign theme is "Every
  Washington resident represented," emphasizing timely responses to
  constituents and constituent needs over party interests [S2].
- **Immigration (says):** Supports "Washington State citizenship for
  undocumented residents" [S1].
- **Higher education (says):** Proposes a three-year college path [S1].
- **Fiscal policy (says):** Calls for fiscal accountability and "a
  government that listens to citizens before it spends their money" [S1].

## Endorsements

None listed in the pamphlet statement or on the campaign website [S1][S2].
