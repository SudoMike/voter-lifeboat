## Background

Darya Farivar is the incumbent Washington State Representative for the 46th
Legislative District, Position 2 (Democrat), and describes herself as the
first Middle Eastern woman elected to the Washington State Legislature
[S1][S2]. She serves as an ADA & Disability Equity Specialist with the King
County Executive's Office, working on accessibility, equity, and inclusion
[S1]. She holds a B.A. from the University of Redlands [S1]. Her community
service includes co-chairing the Seattle Women's Commission, chairing policy
for the State Special Education Advisory Council, and board membership with
Peyvand and the Washington Coalition for Police Accountability; she was
recognized as Disability Community Champion by the Community Employment
Alliance and as SEIU 775 legislator of the year [S1].

Note: her campaign website (daryaforhouse.com) could not be retrieved during
research (access blocked); positions below rely on the pamphlet and official
legislative sources.

## Positions

- **Food access / consumer protection (has done):** Passed legislation
  preventing stores from using corporate loopholes ("negative use covenants")
  to block new grocers and pharmacies from opening, citing the loss of a
  major grocery store in Lake City [S1][S3].
- **Health care / caregiving (has done):** Passed legislation expanding the
  caregiver workforce, aimed at families struggling to access care and
  caregivers facing employment barriers [S1][S4].
- **Governing approach (says):** Emphasizes "practical, solutions-focused
  leadership" — listening to people, identifying problems, and delivering
  solutions [S1].
- **Disability rights:** Professional and community background centers on
  disability equity and accessibility (King County ADA role, Special
  Education Advisory Council, disability facility legislation) [S1][S4].

## Record

- Incumbent State Representative, 46th LD Position 2; committee roles include
  Chair of the Joint Administrative Rules Review Committee, Vice Chair of
  Civil Rights & Judiciary, and member of Community Safety, State Government
  & Tribal Relations, and Rules [S2].
- Prime sponsor of HB 2294 (2026), prohibiting restrictive covenants that
  block new grocery stores or pharmacies from opening in vacant buildings;
  passed the House and advanced through the Senate [S3].
- Sponsor of HB 1395, removing barriers preventing qualified caregivers from
  working, passed and sent to the Governor [S4].
- Also passed 2026 House bills updating disability facility names and
  strengthening transparency in Residential Habilitation Centers [S4].

## Endorsements

Per her pamphlet statement: 46th District Democrats, Pro-Choice Washington,
Washington State Labor Council, Attorney General (Nick) Brown, and
Congresswoman Pramila Jayapal [S1].
