## Background

Christopher (Chris) Roberts is a Shoreline City Councilmember who has served four terms on the council and two terms as Shoreline Mayor [S2]. His pamphlet statement cites 17 years of experience as a councilmember and two-term mayor [S1]; his campaign website and announcement coverage describe 16 years of elected office as of July 2025 [S2][S4]. He holds a Ph.D. from the University of Washington, a B.A. from Willamette University, a national insurance credential (AINS), and an Energy Planning Policy Certificate from the University of Idaho [S1]. His listed occupation is Government Affairs Coordinator and Public Policy Instructor, and he is a member of the IAAO (International Association of Assessing Officers) [S1]. He is an enrolled member of the Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma; local coverage of his campaign launch noted that, if elected, he would be the first Native American to hold an elected King County government position [S2][S4][S5]. He announced his campaign for King County Assessor in early July 2025 [S4][S5].

## Positions

- **Leadership and integrity of the Assessor's office:** Describes the office as being in a "leadership crisis," citing the then-current Assessor's personal legal issues and delays in processing property tax relief applications; attributes problems to leadership rather than frontline staff, pointing to understaffing, budgetary mismanagement, and lack of attention to customer service (says he will provide a "fresh start") [S1]. His campaign launch was framed around restoring integrity and transparency to the office [S4][S5].
- **Oversight and transparency:** Says he will establish a citizen oversight committee for the office [S1]. His platform lists "Fairness First" — transparent, equitable assessments regardless of neighborhood [S2].
- **Tribal relations:** Says he will create a tribal liaison position in the Assessor's office to honor sovereignty [S1].
- **Property tax relief and housing affordability:** Says he will prioritize improving processing of senior and low-income property tax exemptions so residents can remain in their homes [S4][S5]. His website states that "no senior on a fixed income, veteran, or family should be priced out of their home by skyrocketing taxes," and says he plans to work with the legislature on homestead exemptions [S2].
- **Operations and modernization:** Says he will clear backlogs, cut red tape, and modernize operations [S1]. His platform includes "Data with Purpose" — modernizing assessment tools and improving public access to data — and "Community Access" — expanding tools to help residents understand assessments [S2].
- **Fiscal approach:** Platform lists "Fiscal Responsibility," described as balancing fiscal concerns with intergovernmental collaboration [S2].

## Record

- Four-term Shoreline City Councilmember and two-term Shoreline Mayor [S1][S2].
- Chairs the Public Issues Committee of the Sound Cities Association, the association of King County's suburban cities [S2].
- Serves on the National League of Cities Executive Board and co-founded the Local Indigenous Leaders Circle at the National League of Cities [S2].
- His pamphlet states that as a councilmember and mayor he managed complex budgets and "delivered housing affordability" [S1]; campaign launch coverage describes his tenure as spanning a period of significant growth in Shoreline [S4].
- Campaign finance: as of 2026-07-16, PDC records show 277 reported contributions totaling approximately $39,604 to his 2026 County Assessor campaign; for comparison, the same dataset shows approximately $45,860 for Al Dams and $59,226 for Rob Foxcurran [S3].

Note: the City of Shoreline's official website could not be retrieved during research (HTTP 403), so specific council votes and ordinances are not documented here; tenure and regional roles above are sourced to the pamphlet, campaign website, and news coverage.

## Endorsements

- Pamphlet lists: King County Executive Girmay Zahilay (quoted), former King County Assessor Lloyd Hara (quoted), Congresswoman Suzan DelBene, Lt. Gov. Denny Heck, King County Councilmembers Rod Dembowski and Teresa Mosqueda, Seattle Councilmember Alexis Mercedes-Rinck, suburban mayors, multiple Democratic organizations, and union labor [S1].
- Campaign website additionally lists State Senators Javier Valdez, John Lovick, and Claudia Kauffman, and State Representatives Gerry Pollet, Amy Walen, Shelley Kloba, Derek Stanford, Lauren Davis, Chris Stearns, and others, plus mayors, councilmembers, and school board members across the region [S2].
- Launch coverage (July 2025) reported backing from more than 60 local and elected leaders, including Port of Seattle Commissioners Fred Felleman and Ryan Calkins [S4][S5].

## Scoring notes

- Only candidate in the race positioned as a sitting elected official; his case rests on legislative/executive experience (council, mayoralty, regional boards) rather than appraisal practice, though he cites IAAO membership [S1][S2].
- Carries the largest roster of elected-official endorsements in the field as documented here, including the King County Executive and a former King County Assessor [S1][S2][S4].
- Signature office-specific proposals: citizen oversight committee, tribal liaison position, and legislative work on homestead exemptions [S1][S2].
- Frames the race around restoring trust after the incumbent's legal troubles, a contrast with the system-critique framing of pamphlet opponent Scarimbolo, who emphasizes personal appeals experience and audit proposals [S1][S5].
- Reported fundraising (~$39.6k) trails Foxcurran (~$59.2k) and Dams (~$45.9k) as of mid-July 2026 [S3].
