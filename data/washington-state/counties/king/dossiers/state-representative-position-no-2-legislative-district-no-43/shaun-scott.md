## Background

First-term incumbent State Representative for LD 43 Position 2, elected
November 2024 [S1][S3]. Executive Director of Got Green (2025–present);
previously a policy analyst at the Statewide Poverty Action Network
(2020–2024) [S1]. Member of Seattle Democratic Socialists of America since
2017; former 43rd LD Democrats precinct committee officer [S1]. Describes
himself as the first open socialist in the Washington Legislature since the
1910s [S1][S3]. Studied history at the University of Washington [S1]. Vice
Chair of the House Labor & Workplace Standards Committee; member of the
Education and Finance committees [S2][S3]. He is the only candidate in this
contest [S1].

## Positions

- **Taxes/corporate power:** Ending corporate tax breaks; frames major
  corporate lobbying as an oligarchic threat to public services; says his
  work preserved $645 million in biennial spending on low-income housing,
  wildfire mitigation, public schools, and higher education [S1].
- **Labor:** Prioritizes labor rights and workplace standards as an
  organizing lever; cites committee work on extending unemployment benefits
  to striking workers [S3].
- **Housing:** Passed legislation requiring large financial institutions to
  pay standard document recording fees, generating tens of millions
  annually for affordable housing [S3].

## Record

- Sponsored and passed the Wildfire Alleviation Support Act funding
  wildfire response (2026 session) [S3].
- Document recording fee parity bill for large financial institutions,
  funding affordable housing [S3].
- Corporate tax-break repeal work he credits with preserving $645 million
  in biennial spending [S1].

## Endorsements

Pamphlet states he has been endorsed across his two campaigns by the
Washington State Labor Council, Planned Parenthood Alliance Advocates, and
Washington Conservation Action, among others [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Unopposed: advances to the general election automatically [S1].
- First-term incumbent with a record concentrated in labor standards,
  corporate tax policy, and affordable-housing revenue [S1][S3].
- Openly democratic-socialist framing distinguishes his politics within the
