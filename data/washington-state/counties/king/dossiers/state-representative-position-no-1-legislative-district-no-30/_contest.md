## What the office does
One of two state representatives for the 30th Legislative District (Federal Way and nearby south King County communities) in the Washington State House. Two-year term.

## Race dynamics
Two candidates — incumbent Democrat Jamila E. Taylor and Republican first-time candidate Tiffany Bowyer — so both advance from the primary to the November general election regardless of primary results. Research depth is intentionally light for this reason.

## Differentiating axes
- Incumbency and record: Taylor chairs the Civil Rights & Judiciary Committee and cites passed legislation on gun violence, police training, tax credits, and housing; Bowyer has no legislative record and runs on spending accountability.
- Public safety approach: root-causes plus police-training reform (Taylor) vs. full police staffing and repeat-offender accountability (Bowyer) — with some overlap on behavioral-health coordination.
- Taxes: expanded working-family tax credits and targeted relief (Taylor) vs. opposing new taxes and cutting spending growth (Bowyer).
- Endorsements: broad Democratic/labor/advocacy list plus a claimed Seattle Times endorsement (Taylor) vs. none listed (Bowyer).
