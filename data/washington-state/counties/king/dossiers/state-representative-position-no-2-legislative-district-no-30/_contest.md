## What this office does
A Washington State Representative is one of two House members elected from each legislative district for a two-year term. Representatives write and vote on state law and the operating, capital, and transportation budgets — the levers controlling K-12 funding, state taxes, housing rules, behavioral-health programs, and public-safety statutes.

## The race
Depth: light. Two candidates filed, so **both advance from the August 4, 2026 top-two primary to the November general election regardless of the primary result** [S1]:

- **Kristine Reeves (D, incumbent)** — State Representative 2017-2019 and 2023-present; chairs the House Agriculture and Natural Resources Committee [S1].
- **Paul McDaniel (prefers Independent Party)** — former Federal Way City Councilmember, Marine Corps veteran, and small business owner [S1].

## Differentiating issue axes
1. **Incumbent legislative record vs. outsider change message** — Reeves runs on passed legislation and committee leadership; McDaniel runs on accountability and against "Olympia demanding more" [S1].
2. **Tax-and-spending posture** — McDaniel calls for reducing taxes; Reeves emphasizes program delivery ($100M local public safety investment, food access) [S1].
3. **Party alignment** — Democratic incumbent vs. an independent challenger with tax-cutting, public-safety, and girls'-sports positions [S1].
