## Background
Alex (Avrum) Tsimerman is a Bellevue resident running under the "Standup-America Party" preference [S1][S2]. He is a perennial candidate who has run repeatedly for offices including Seattle mayor (2017), Seattle City Council, and State Senate in the 46th District (2022) [S3][S4][S5]. His voter-pamphlet statement submits no elected experience, professional experience, education, or community service information [S1].

## Positions
- His full pamphlet statement is a series of slogans ("Make America great again! ... Viva Trump! Viva new America Revolution!") and identifies him as "American Jew against Chinese occupation"; it contains no policy proposals for the office [S1].

## Record
- No elected or governmental record [S1].
- The Public Disclosure Commission has brought multiple enforcement cases against him across the 2021–2024 election cycles for failing to file candidate registrations (C-1), personal financial affairs statements (F-1), and contribution/expenditure reports (C-4), resulting in fines; the PDC noted in April 2023 that prior fines had gone unpaid, and later cases imposed additional penalties for unfiled 2022 State Senate expenditure reports [S3][S4].
- In 2017, while a mayoral candidate, he was banned from Seattle City Hall for a year for violating the city's rules of conduct during public-comment sessions [S5].
- Campaign finance: no contributions for a 2026 Tsimerman committee appear in the PDC contributions dataset as of 2026-07-16 [S6].
