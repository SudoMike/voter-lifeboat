## Background

- Former Seattle City Councilmember; served three terms over a decade (2014 to January 2024) as the council's sole socialist, then departed voluntarily [S5]. She was the first socialist elected to Seattle's council in nearly a century [S4].
- Former teacher and union member; holds a PhD in Economics [S1].
- Founded Workers Strike Back, which she describes as "a nationwide movement fighting back against the billionaires" [S1]; she is running as an independent socialist and says "we need a new party for working people" [S1].
- Launched her WA-9 campaign in mid-2025 as what press coverage described as a "working-class, antiwar, anti-genocide" challenge to incumbent Adam Smith [S4].

## Positions

- **Healthcare:** "Free healthcare for all funded by taxing the rich" (says she will); she is the only candidate in the race making this specific pledge, per her statement [S1][S2].
- **Israel/Gaza:** End all U.S. military aid to Israel; her stated first priority in Congress is an arms embargo against Israel, per The Stranger's endorsement write-up [S1][S6].
- **Immigration:** "Shut down ICE and the detention centers" (says she will) [S1].
- **Housing:** National rent control and millions of affordable homes (says she will) [S1][S2].
- **Wages:** Called for a $25/hour national minimum wage at her campaign kickoff [S4].
- **Technology/economy:** "Stop the Data Centers & AI Layoffs" through democratic public ownership; "Tax the rich!" [S2].
- **Salary pledge:** If elected, says she will accept only an average worker's wage and donate the rest of her congressional salary to workers' movements, as she did on the council [S1][S2].

## Record

- Led the movement that made Seattle the first major U.S. city to adopt a $15 minimum wage (2014); former Councilmember Nick Licata said she and Socialist Alternative "played a critical role, basically grabbing the establishment by the collars" [S5]. Her campaign states the wage is now the nation's highest among major cities [S1][S2].
- Considers the JumpStart payroll tax on large employers — which she calls the "Amazon tax" — a triumph of her tenure; it funds affordable housing [S5][S1].
- Championed the 2023 ordinance making Seattle the first U.S. city to ban caste-based discrimination [S5][S4].
- Campaign statement claims renters'-rights wins including a cap on late fees and publicly funded eviction defense, plus Indigenous Peoples' Day and a council resolution on Gaza [S1].
- Survived a recall election in 2021; the recall petition cited misuse of city resources, and she also faced ethics complaints and defamation suits from a landlord and police officers (suits described in press coverage) [S4][S5].
- Her confrontational style was divisive on the council; supporters interrupted meetings with chants and she called colleagues "capitalist tools of big business," per KUOW's retrospective [S5]. NPI's Civic Heartbeat polling found she left office in 2024 with a very negative job-performance rating, including in her own council district [S6].
- Campaign finance (2026 cycle, through 2026-06-30): $609,417 raised — effectively all from individual contributions ($609,417 individual, $0 from PACs) — $585,348 spent, $24,069 cash on hand [S3]. Campaign materials state "not corporate cash" [S2].

## Endorsements

- The Stranger (sole endorsement in this race, July 2026): wrote that she "has the best chance of unseating incumbent Rep. Adam Smith" [S6][S2].
- Journalist/author Chris Hedges, per her campaign site [S2].

## Scoring notes

- The only candidate with a decade-long municipal legislative record of enacted left-economic policy (minimum wage, employer payroll tax, renter protections).
- Runs as an independent socialist explicitly against both major parties, versus Chaudhry (Democrat) and Smith (Democrat); says the goal is a new workers' party.
- Most sharply defined on foreign policy: arms embargo on Israel as first priority, directly opposing the incumbent's Armed Services role.
- Highest-funded challenger (about $609K, all individual donations), though with little cash remaining as of June 30, 2026.
- Council tenure was polarizing: survived a 2021 recall and left office with negative local approval per NPI polling, while retaining a strong activist base and The Stranger's endorsement.
