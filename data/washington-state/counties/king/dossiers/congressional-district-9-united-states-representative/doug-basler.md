## Background

- Founder and president of Prologue Digital Inc., a Washington advertising and video production company; media and marketing professional since 1990; sales trainer and small business owner [S1].
- Former King County Republican Party vice chairman; former Eastside Republican Club president and current vice president; longtime Republican precinct committee officer [S1].
- Attended Multnomah University; former youth pastor and worship pastor; homelessness outreach with rescue missions, Union Gospel Mission, and the Salvation Army; work with at-risk youth [S1].
- Repeat candidate against Adam Smith: general-election results include 25.7% (2020) and 28.2% (2022); he also ran in earlier cycles (2014, 2016, 2018, 2024 per Ballotpedia's election history) and ran for King County Elections Director in 2023, losing to incumbent Julie Wise 83.9%–15.9% [S4].

## Positions

All items are campaign statements ("says he will") — he has held no legislative office [S1][S2].

- **Economy/opportunity:** Frames his campaign as an "opportunity revolution"; argues "big government policies are making it harder to build, hire, afford, and stay" and that hostility toward major regional employers costs jobs [S1]. Website theme: "hard work leads somewhere" — start a business, buy a home, learn a trade [S2].
- **Affordability/energy:** Says the state's Climate Commitment Act "is driving up the cost of energy while doing little to directly improve the environment," and cites high gas taxes as raising transportation, food, and energy costs [S1].
- **Public safety:** "Compassion to people in crisis while still holding people accountable for crime, addiction-fueled disorder" [S2].
- **Tone/approach:** Says he will be "a voice of common sense, moderation, and practical solutions"; emphasizes "family, public safety, faith in God, and faith in each other" and rejects "division, anger, and blame" [S1].

## Record

- No elected-office record. Electoral history: lost to Smith in multiple general elections (25.7% in 2020; 28.2% in 2022) and lost the 2023 King County Elections Director race with 15.9% [S4].
- Campaign finance (2026 cycle, through 2026-03-31): $0 receipts, $0 disbursements, $543 cash on hand [S3].

## Endorsements

None found on his campaign site or in Tier 1/2 sources reviewed [S2][S4].

## Scoring notes

- The only Republican in this five-candidate field; NPI analysis notes he could reach the general election if left-leaning voters split among Smith's three left-flank challengers.
- The only candidate arguing affordability problems stem from state climate/energy policy (Climate Commitment Act, gas taxes) and regulatory burden rather than from corporate power or underfunded programs.
- Perennial-candidate profile: five prior runs against Smith with general-election ceilings under 29%, and effectively no 2026 fundraising.
- Positions himself as a moderate, faith-oriented Republican focused on cost of living and public safety rather than national culture-war themes.
