## Background
Incumbent Democratic state representative for the 11th Legislative District (Position 2) [S1]. History/social studies teacher in the Renton School District (Lindbergh High School) and one of the only active classroom teachers serving in the legislature; Renton Education Association union member [S1][S2]. Former small-business owner of Aces Tennis, which ran the City of Renton Parks and Recreation tennis programs [S1][S2]. Serves on the House Education and Appropriations committees [S2]. BA in political science and history, University of Washington; Masters in Teaching, Western Washington University; Hazen High School (Renton) graduate; family in the community four generations [S1][S2]. Board member of Special Olympics Washington, Renton Historical Society & Genealogy Center, and the Renton Regional Community Foundation [S1].

## Positions
- Education/childcare: expand programs so working families can afford childcare, preschool (Ready K), and college [S1].
- Higher education: worked with nonprofits, districts, and cities to pass HB 1587 college promise scholarships; cites Renton/Tukwila Promise-style programs [S1].
- Housing: help create more housing so people can afford to live near where they work [S1].
- Budget: says his working-parent and active-teacher perspective informs state budget discussions [S1].

## Record
- Cites passage of HB 1587 (college promise scholarships) as a signature accomplishment [S1].

## Endorsements
- Pamphlet cites teachers, nurses, firefighters, and the 11th District Democrats, among others [S1].
