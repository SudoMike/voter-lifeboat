# Ferry County Sheriff

The sheriff is elected to a four-year term and is responsible under Washington law for preserving the peace, arresting people who commit public offenses, executing court process, and operating the county jail [S1]. The 2026 primary has two candidates: three-term incumbent Ray Maycumber and former Ferry County deputy and U.S. Border Patrol agent Sam Olson [S2].

The clearest differences in the submitted statements concern experience and law-enforcement priorities. Maycumber emphasizes his record as sheriff, recruitment and retention, and his challenge to state legislation governing sheriffs [S2]. Olson emphasizes prior patrol, narcotics, Border Patrol, and K9 work and proposes an aggressive response to theft and drug abuse [S2].
