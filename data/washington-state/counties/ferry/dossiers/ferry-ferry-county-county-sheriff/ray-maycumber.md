## Background

Maycumber says he has served as Ferry County sheriff since January 2015 and previously worked as a Republic police officer, Ferry County deputy sheriff, chief criminal deputy, and 911 coordinator [S1]. He reports a bachelor's degree in criminal justice from Eastern Washington University [S1]. A 2026 Washington Supreme Court ruling identifies him in his official capacity as Ferry County sheriff, confirming that the court matter concerns the same officeholder [S2].

## Positions

- Maycumber identifies deputy recruitment and retention as a major challenge and attributes part of the difficulty to the county's limited ability to fund competitive hiring [S1].
- He opposes 2026 legislation changing sheriff qualifications and retention rules and says he is participating in the court challenge to it [S1]. The Washington Supreme Court ruling lists Maycumber among the respondent sheriffs in that litigation [S2].
- He presents individual liberty and willingness to challenge state action as priorities [S1].

## Record

- Maycumber reports three completed terms as sheriff and more than 25 years as a certified peace officer in Ferry County [S1].
- In the 2026 litigation over SSSB 5974, he was a named respondent in both his individual and official capacities [S2].
