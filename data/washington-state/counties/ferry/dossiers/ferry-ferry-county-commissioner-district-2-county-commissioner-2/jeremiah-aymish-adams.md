## Background

Adams reports working as a clerk at the Television Association of Republic and previously as a Habitat for Humanity manager, industrial shop supervisor and maintenance worker, and groundskeeper for the Ferry County Fairgrounds and Republic Cemetery [S1]. He says he graduated from Republic High School, attended Eastern Washington University for four years, and serves as president of the Ferry County Edible Resource Team [S1].

## Positions

- Adams proposes focusing county investment on Ferry County residents [S1].
- He says existing county resources could assist cottage industries, increase youth retention, and broaden the county's economic viability [S1].
- He frames economic development around local opportunity and Ferry County's rural, self-reliant traditions [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Adams is the only candidate in this contest proposing an explicit people-focused investment strategy using existing county resources [S1].
- Cottage-industry support and youth retention are his most specific economic-development priorities [S1].
