Franklin County Official Local Voters’ Pamphlet #FutureVoter If you are 16 or 17, become a Future Voter! 16- and 17-year olds can use the Voter Registration form to sign up as Future Voters and be automatically registered to vote when they qualify. Every January on Temperance and Good Citizenship Day, high school students 16 and older are given the opportunity to complete a voter registration in class. Elections and Voter Registration Access Your Ballot Online • visit VoteWA.gov • log in using your first, last name and date of birth • print your ballot • sign the declaration • drop it in a ballot drop box before 8:00 p.m. on Election Day Qualifications To register to vote in the State of Washington, you must be: • a citizen of the United States; • a resident of Washington State; • at least 18 years old; • not disqualified from voting due to a court order; • not currently serving a sentence of total confinement in prison for a felony conviction. How Do I Register to Vote or Update my Current Information? Online: Visit VoteWA.gov; By mail: Request a paper form be mailed to you by calling (509) 545-3538 or print your own at sos.wa.gov/elections. In person: Visit the Franklin County Elections Divison at 404 W Clark St. in Pasco, or any elections office in the state. Voter Registration and Update Deadlines By mail or online: Your application must be received by July 27. In person: Visit us in person no later than 8:00 p.m. on August 4. Accessible Voting Voters can vote independently using our Accessible Voting Unit (AVU). The AVU is a large touch-screen device that: • displays the ballot in large font • provides an audio ballot • can connect to an Audio Tactile Interface Keypad (ATI) with Braille The AVU is available 18 days before Election Day at the Franklin County Elections Division Office. Keep Your Registration Current We are constantly working to keep our voter rolls up to date with the help of the Department of Licensing, the USPS, local obituaries and the Department of Health, but you can also help by following these simple steps: Update your address Call our office to update your address or go online to VoteWA.gov. You can also email us or visit us in person! Set up a mailing address Contact our office to set up a seasonal, temporary, or a different mailing address you need your ballot mailed to. Return to sender! If you receive a ballot for a person who does not live at your address, write “Return to Sender” on the unopened envelope and put it back in the mail. Do not drop it in a ballot drop box. If you receive a ballot for a deceased voter, write “Deceased” on the unopened envelope and put it back in the mail. We will research and follow up. Franklin County Official Local Voters’ Pamphlet Page 5 Elections and Voter Registration Voting Tips Use a Blue or Black Ink Pen to Fill in Your Choices . You are not required to vote in every race. Sign Your Envelope Your signature must match the signature on your voter registration record in order for your ballot to move on to the next step. Check the Status of Your Ballot on VoteWA.gov to verify if it has been received by the Franklin County Elections Division. Election staff will contact you if: • Your signature is missing • Your signature does not match the signature on your voter registration record View Election Results Online After 8:00 p.m. on election night, tallied results are posted at sos.wa.gov/elections. Results are unofficial and may change until certification. Return Your Ballot Early! Whether returning your ballot by mail or in a drop box, don’t wait until Election Day. Vote your ballot early! If Using a Ballot Drop Box: • Deposit your ballot before 8:00 p.m. on Election Day • There are 8 ballot drop boxes in Franklin County, available 18 days before an election • All ballot drop boxes close at 8:00 p.m. on Election Day • Ballot boxes are only emptied by our permanent election staff If Returning By Mail: • No postage necessary • Mail your ballot as soon as possible • If Election Day is close, drop your ballot in an official ballot drop box to ensure it is received on time If you cannot personally return your ballot, only let people you trust deliver it for you. If You Cannot Sign Your Ballot: • mark an “x” on the signature line in the presence of two witnesses; • the two witnesses must sign in the provided spaces.

For the limited purpose of voting in a precinct committee officer race, a voter affiliates with a major political party when they vote for a candidate of that party.
