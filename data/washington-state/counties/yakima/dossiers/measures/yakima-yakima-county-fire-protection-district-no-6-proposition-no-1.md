## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would restore Fire Protection District No. 6's regular property-tax levy for collection in 2027 to no more than $1.00 per $1,000 of assessed value [S1]. It would authorize a 103% annual limit factor for each of the next nine years, and the final year's maximum levy would become the base for later levy limits under RCW 84.55 [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- At the maximum stated rate, the levy equals $100 annually per $100,000 of assessed value, or $500 on property assessed at $500,000 [S1].
- The ballot title permits up to 3% annual growth in the levy amount for nine years; actual tax bills also depend on assessed value and the rate imposed [S1].
- This restores an existing regular levy rather than authorizing a bond [S1].

## Arguments for

The official ballot describes the purpose as restoring the district's regular levy, which supplies local fire-protection funding [S1]. No pro-committee statement was available in the official source set reviewed, so no broader campaign claims are attributed here.

## Arguments against

No con-committee statement was available in the official source set reviewed. The direct trade-off visible in the ballot text is higher and potentially growing property-tax collections versus the district revenue supported by the restored levy [S1].

## Axis notes

- Local property-tax burden versus fire-protection funding.
- Nine years of levy-growth authority versus annual fiscal restraint.
