## What it does

Restores the district's regular levy from about $1.31 to no more than $1.50 per $1,000 for 2027 collection, authorizes a 106% limit factor for nine following years, and makes the final maximum levy the later base [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The pro committee estimates the $0.19 rate increase at about $5.54 monthly for a $350,000 home [S1].
- The district says revenue would support six firefighters, an engine replacement, water rescue, prevention, equipment, and facilities [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee says calls and costs have grown faster than levy revenue and restoration is needed for staffing, equipment, response times, and reliability [S1].

## Arguments against

No con statement was submitted after repeated recruitment [S1]. The direct trade-off is the rate and nine years of levy-growth authority versus the described service and capital funding.

## Axis notes

- Higher property tax and a reset base versus expanded staffing and emergency-service capacity [S1].
