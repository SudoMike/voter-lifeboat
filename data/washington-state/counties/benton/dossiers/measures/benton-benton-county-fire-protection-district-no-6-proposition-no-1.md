## What it does

Reestablishes for ten years an EMS property-tax levy of no more than $0.50 per $1,000, beginning with 2027 collections; revenue is restricted to EMS [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- At the maximum rate, the levy is $50 annually per $100,000 of assessed value, or $250 on $500,000 [S1].
- Revenue supports EMS personnel, equipment, facilities, ambulance service, and current service levels [S1].

## Arguments for

The official explanation says reestablishment is needed to maintain EMS amid rising costs [S1]. Although a pro committee was appointed, no pro statement was submitted [S1].

## Arguments against

No con statement was submitted after repeated recruitment [S1]. The trade-off is a ten-year dedicated property-tax levy versus EMS continuity.

## Axis notes

- Dedicated local property taxation versus continued emergency-medical service [S1].
