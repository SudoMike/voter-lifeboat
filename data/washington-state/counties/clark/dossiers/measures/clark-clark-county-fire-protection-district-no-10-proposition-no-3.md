## What it does

Proposition 3 authorizes up to $15.2 million in general-obligation bonds, maturing within 20 years, to construct and equip a replacement fire station and authorizes excess property taxes to repay them [S1].

Cost line: the district estimates $0.45 per $1,000 of assessed value, about $292.50 annually or $24.38 monthly for a $650,000 home [S1].

## Arguments for

The submitted pro committee says the 1963 station has water damage, asbestos, outdated infrastructure, earthquake damage, and inadequate space for 70 firefighters and modern operations; it says assessments found replacement more responsible than retrofit [S1].

## Arguments against

No person volunteered to submit a con statement or rebuttal, so the official pamphlet contains no organized opposition argument [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes versus capital replacement: voters weigh a 20-year bond levy against replacement of an aging station [S1].
- The record is one-sided because no con statement was submitted [S1].
