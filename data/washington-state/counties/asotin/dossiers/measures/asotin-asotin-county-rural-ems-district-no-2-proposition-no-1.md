## What it does

Approves a continuing property-tax levy for rural emergency medical services for six years beginning with 2027 collections [S1][S2].

## Fiscal mechanics

Cost line: The maximum rate rises from $0.15 to $0.28 per $1,000 of assessed value, equal to $28 annually per $100,000 or $140 on $500,000 at the maximum rate [S1].

## Arguments for

The official material identifies continued emergency medical services as the use; no committee argument was available in the reviewed official material [S1][S2].

## Arguments against

No committee argument was available; the direct tradeoff is continued EMS funding versus an increased property-tax rate [S1][S2].

## Axis notes

- A YES vote continues and increases a local property-tax levy for EMS [S1].
