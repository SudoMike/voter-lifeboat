## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would renew for 14 years Cheney's tax on electrical-energy and natural-gas businesses at 16.75% of gross revenue, six percentage points above the otherwise authorized 10.75% rate [S1][S2]. The added proceeds are dedicated to residential street and sidewalk repair; the rate would revert to 10.75% after the term [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- Renewal, not a newly created tax; Cheney says this funding method has supported residential street and sidewalk maintenance since 1998 [S1].
- The pamphlet gives no model household cost, so the customer-level effect is not quantified here [S1].
- The city says other resources are inadequate to preserve residential streets and sidewalks [S1].

## Arguments for

No volunteer submitted a pro statement. The official explanation says renewal supplies dedicated maintenance money and protects long-term street and sidewalk integrity and travel safety [S1].

## Arguments against

No volunteer submitted a con statement after repeated recruitment attempts [S1]. The trade-off is continuation of a high utility-business tax rate, which may be passed through to customers, versus dedicated residential transportation maintenance; the pamphlet does not quantify pass-through [S1].

## Axis notes

- Utility-tax burden versus street and sidewalk maintenance.
- Dedicated infrastructure funding versus lower local taxation.
