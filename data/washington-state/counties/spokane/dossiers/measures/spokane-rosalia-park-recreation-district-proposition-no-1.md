## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would authorize Rosalia Park and Recreation District No. 5 to collect $85,000 in each of 2027 and 2028 for pool operations, maintenance, improvements, supplies, salaries, and utilities [S1][S2]. It requires a 60% supermajority [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- Estimated rate: about $0.39 per $1,000 of assessed value, subject to final valuation, with a stated maximum of $0.60 [S1].
- Duration: two collection years, 2027 and 2028 [S1].
- At the estimate, a $300,000 property would pay about $117 per year; this arithmetic is derived from the stated rate, not an official pamphlet estimate [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee argues stable local funding enables maintenance, trained lifeguards, affordable access, safe recreation, and avoidance of larger deferred repairs [S1].

## Arguments against

No volunteers submitted a con statement after repeated recruitment attempts [S1]. The unresolved trade-off is a temporary property-tax levy versus reduced hours, higher user fees, or deterioration if the pool lacks operating revenue.

## Axis notes

- Property-tax appetite versus a locally controlled recreational amenity.
- Short-term dedicated operations funding and access to community facilities.
