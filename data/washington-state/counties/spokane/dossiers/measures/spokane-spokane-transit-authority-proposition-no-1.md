## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would reauthorize an existing Spokane Transit Authority sales and use tax of up to 0.2%, effective January 1, 2029, through no later than December 31, 2048 [S1][S3]. Revenue would maintain and enhance fixed-route, paratransit, and rideshare service and could fund service expansion, new modes, programs, and facilities under STA's plans [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- This is a continuation of a tax approved in 2016 and scheduled to expire at the end of 2028, not an increase in its maximum rate [S1].
- Maximum rate: two-tenths of one percent, or 20 cents per $100 of taxable purchases [S1].
- Duration: up to 20 years, ending no later than December 31, 2048 [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee says the tax funded expanded service, stations, and the City Line, and would support Division Street bus rapid transit, mobility-on-demand pilots, longer service hours, and wider coverage [S2]. It argues reserves are designated for capital, fleet replacement, and risk and says 2025 ridership exceeded 10.9 million [S2].

## Arguments against

The con committee says STA collected about $169 million while reserves grew from roughly $40 million to over $270 million, and argues existing revenues and reserves should be used before extending the tax [S2]. It also questions cost per ride and says the sunset is an accountability checkpoint [S2].

## Axis notes

- Transit service and expansion versus sales-tax burden.
- Dedicated long-range funding versus reliance on accumulated reserves and existing revenue.
- Local public transportation investment and government fiscal accountability.
