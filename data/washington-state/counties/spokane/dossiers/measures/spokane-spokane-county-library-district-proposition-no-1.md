## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would restore the library district's regular property-tax levy to $0.45 per $1,000 of assessed value for collection in 2027 [S1][S2]. That amount would become the basis for future levy limits. Revenue would support library operations, materials, mobile service, facilities, accessibility, and security [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The rate would rise from approximately $0.33 to $0.45 per $1,000, below the $0.50 statutory maximum [S1].
- The pro statement estimates about $3.50 per month for a $350,000 home [S1].
- The district says property tax supplies 97% of its budget and ordinary annual revenue growth is limited to 1% without voter approval [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee cites ten branches, mobile services, 154,000 cardholders, nearly 700,000 visits, and 2.2 million loans, and argues inflation threatens programs, staffing, hours, maintenance, and accessibility improvements [S1].

## Arguments against

No volunteers submitted a con statement after repeated recruitment attempts [S1]. The unresolved trade-off is therefore the higher property-tax bill versus the described service reductions if the measure fails.

## Axis notes

- Property-tax appetite versus library service, accessibility, and facility maintenance.
- Local public services and dedicated operational funding.
