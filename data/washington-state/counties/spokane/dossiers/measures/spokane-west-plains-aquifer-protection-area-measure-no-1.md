## What it does

Measure No. 1 would create a West Plains Aquifer Protection Area for 20 years and authorize fees for water withdrawal and on-site sewage disposal [S1][S3]. Approximately $400,000 annually would fund planning, monitoring, public education, sewage and stormwater work, water-quality facilities, and other activities authorized by chapter 36.36 RCW [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- Household maximums are $1.25 per month for water withdrawal and $1.25 per month for on-site sewage disposal [S1].
- Non-household water fees vary by meter size, from $1.25 for a three-quarter-inch meter up to $80 for meters six inches or larger; the on-site sewage fee also applies [S1].
- The area depends on participating municipalities' written approval [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee says growth, runoff, improper disposal, and industrial spills threaten the West Plains' separate aquifers and argues a dedicated program can fund science, planning, and prevention similar to the longstanding Spokane Valley-Rathdrum Prairie program [S2].

## Arguments against

The con committee argues contaminated residents should not pay, says the measure will not itself clean existing contamination, and calls for responsible airport and Air Force entities to fund a larger cleanup [S2]. The pro rebuttal agrees the measure is preventive protection rather than a contamination-cleanup program [S2].

## Axis notes

- User fees and local preventive stewardship versus polluter-funded remediation.
- Environmental protection, water infrastructure, and responsibility for existing contamination.
