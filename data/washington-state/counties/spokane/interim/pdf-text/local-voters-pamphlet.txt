5 Your Ballot Signature Matters Your vote is powerful! To make sure your vote counts, it's crucial to sign your ballot and understand the impact your signature has on validating your vote. Signature verification plays a vital role in upholding the integrity of elections and ensuring your voice is heard. Here are a few points to emphasize the importance of your signature: Signature Required To ensure your ballot is valid, please remember to sign the return envelope. Action Required If the signature on your ballot isn't similar to the signature in your voter registration record or if the signature was missing, don't worry. You can still "cure" your ballot so it counts. Our office will contact you with directions on how to cure your ballot. The Power of Your Signature Your signature validates your ballot, your choices, and your participation in the democratic process. i voted! A FEW TIPS: If you registered to vote online, your signature from the Department of Licensing is your voter registration signature. Take a moment and look at the signature on your drivers’ license or state ID. If you registered with a paper form, your signature from the form is your voter registration signature. The signature on your ballot does not need to be perfect. It just needs to be consistent and match what we have on file. If you are not sure what signature we have on file, you can update your signature. • Print out a registration form and mail us the form with your new signature; or • Call 509-477-2320 or email elections@spokanecounty.gov to request a Signature Update form; or • Visit Spokane County Elections to fill out a form in person 1 2 3 4 Signature Verification Your signature, whether in print or cursive, is compared to the signature we have in your voter registration record. That's right, we look at each signature on each ballot that is returned.

7 Did You Know? August Primary A 17-year-old who will be 18 by the November General Election can vote in the August Primary. These primary-only voters are authorized to participate only in races that are selecting candidates for the general election. Measures and races that do not go to the general election will not appear on a primary-only voter’s ballot. I'm 17, why did I get a ballot? November General What is a Precinct Committee Officer (PCO)? Who donates to campaigns? View information on financial contributions for federal candidates: Federal Election Commission www.fec.gov Toll Free 1 (800) 424-9530 View financial contributions for state and local candidates and measures: Public Disclosure Commission pdc@pdc.wa.gov www.pdc.wa.gov Toll Free 1 (877) 601-2828 The major parties have a PCO position in each precinct in Spokane County. The PCO assists the party in party functions. A candidate for PCO will only compete against other PCO candidates of the same political party within their precinct. PCO races appear on the Primary ballot in each even-numbered year, only if two or more candidates within the same party are running for the same precinct. This means not every voter will have a PCO race on their ballot. If a PCO race appears on your ballot: You may choose a candidate in the race if you identify with that party. If you do not identify with the party, do not vote that race. All other races you voted will be counted. PCO candidates are not printed in the Voters’ Pamphlet. For more information on PCOs, contact the party. Spokane County Democratic Party (509) 324-8525 spokanedemocrats.org Spokane County Republican Party (509) 838-6162 spokanegop.com Top two candidates move on to November! In each Primary race, you may vote for any one candidate. The two candidates who receive the most votes will advance to the General election, even if they affiliate with the same party. Candidates for partisan office may state a political party they prefer, but a candidate's preference does not mean the candidate is nominated, endorsed, or approved by the party.
