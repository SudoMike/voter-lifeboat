## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would authorize Fire Protection District No. 10 to acquire, build, and equip a replacement fire station; issue up to $25 million in general-obligation bonds maturing within 20 years; and levy annual excess property taxes to repay them [S1][S2].

Cost to households: the official material reviewed states the maximum principal and repayment mechanism but does not state a household estimate or projected tax rate [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The bonds finance a capital facility rather than ordinary operations and would be repaid through voter-approved excess property-tax levies [S1].
- The final interest cost and annual tax collection depend on bond issuance and repayment schedules within the 20-year maximum [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee says the existing station was built for a largely volunteer department, is undersized and outdated, lacks modern decontamination and exhaust separation, and cannot accommodate a diverse workforce; it argues replacement would improve firefighter health and emergency operations [S1].

## Arguments against

No statement against was submitted for the pamphlet [S1]. The central unargued trade-off is up to $25 million in debt plus the excess property taxes needed for repayment [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes: YES authorizes additional property taxes to repay long-term bond debt [S1].
- Spending and safety: YES funds a new public-safety capital facility, with the pro case centered on operational capacity and occupational health [S1].
