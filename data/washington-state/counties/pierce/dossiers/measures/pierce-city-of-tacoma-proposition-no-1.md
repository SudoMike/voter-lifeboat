## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would reinstate for ten years a 1.5% utility tax on natural-gas, electric, and telephone utilities and a $0.20-per-$1,000 regular property-tax increase, with a maximum 2027 city rate of $1.95 per $1,000 [S1]. Revenue would be dedicated to Connect Tacoma projects including school routes, pothole repair, paving, sidewalks, street maintenance and lighting, traffic calming and safety, ADA upgrades, multimodal improvements, greenways, and neighborhood connections [S1].

Cost to households: the official explanation estimates $101.52 annually in property tax for the average homeowner and $23.64 annually in utility tax, about $125.16 combined [S1]. Qualifying seniors and others may be exempt from the property-tax portion [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- Both taxes begin in 2027 and last through 2036; the 2027 levy becomes the base for later levies under RCW 84.55 [S1].
- The explanation describes this as replacing an expired voter-approved measure while retaining the same overall tax structure [S1].

## Arguments for

The pro committee says the earlier program attracted three dollars in partner funding per city dollar, improved 48 school crossings and 340 miles of streets, and that renewal funds safer access, potholes, and neighborhood work [S1]. It also points to senior, disabled, and low-income assistance [S1].

## Arguments against

The con committee says too little of the $200 million package is reserved for neighborhood paving, criticizes the safety category as insufficiently defined, cites Tacoma's existing tax burden and budget deficit, and argues the city should present a narrower road-repair measure [S1]. It also argues the tax could increase rents [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes: YES reinstates two city taxes for ten years, although supporters describe the rates as a renewal of expired taxes [S1].
- Spending: YES dedicates substantial public revenue to a broad transportation capital and maintenance program [S1].
- Transportation: the disagreement is partly over multimodal/safety spending versus concentrating funds on paving and road repair [S1].
