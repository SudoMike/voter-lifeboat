## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would reauthorize Carbonado to levy up to $0.50 per $1,000 of assessed value in each of six consecutive years to continue basic and advanced emergency medical services [S1][S2]. This is a time-limited renewal, not permanent authority [S1].

Cost to households: up to $50 annually per $100,000 of assessed value, or $250 on a $500,000 property, before any applicable exemptions [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The levy is a regular property tax dedicated to emergency medical services [S1].
- The authorization lasts six years and would require another vote to continue beyond that period [S1].

## Arguments for

- The official measure materials present the levy as continuing local EMT and paramedic services through a dedicated revenue source [S1].

## Arguments against

- No statement against was identified in the official pamphlet material reviewed; the principal trade-off is the continued property-tax cost [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes: a YES vote continues a dedicated local property tax for six years, making the tax direction clear even though it is a renewal [S1].
- Local services: the revenue remains tied to locally delivered basic and advanced EMS [S1].
