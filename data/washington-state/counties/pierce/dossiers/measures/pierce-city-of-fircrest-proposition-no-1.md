## What it does

Proposition No. 1 would renew Fircrest's authority to levy up to $0.50 per $1,000 of assessed value for emergency medical care and services in 2027 through 2032 [S1][S2]. Fircrest contracts with Tacoma for fire suppression and EMS and forwards the EMS levy revenue as part of that contract payment [S1].

Cost to households: up to $50 annually per $100,000 of assessed value, or $250 on a $500,000 property [S1].

## Fiscal mechanics

- This renews six-year authority voters last granted in 2020; 2026 is the final collection year under the current authorization [S1].
- A simple majority is required under the cited EMS levy statute [S1].

## Arguments for

- The city resolution says uninterrupted levy authority is needed to continue emergency medical care and services under the Tacoma service contract [S1].

## Arguments against

- No opposition statement was identified in the official material reviewed; the measurable trade-off is continuation of the property levy [S2].

## Axis notes

- Taxes: YES renews, rather than creates, a property tax for six years [S1].
- Regional service delivery: local tax revenue supports a contracted Tacoma fire/EMS service model [S1].
