## Background

Solan is a 27-year Seattle police officer and former president of the Seattle Police Officers Guild [S1]. A Superior Court order restored him to the ballot after the county initially applied a one-year district voter-registration requirement [S3].

## Positions

- He prioritizes deputy staffing, public safety, fiscal accountability, and limits on rising county costs and overlapping taxes [S1][S2].
- He calls for a plan to replace the Fox Island bridge, protection of shorelines and docks, and land-use policies he describes as fair to unincorporated communities [S1].
- He presents police labor negotiations as relevant experience for county governance [S1].

## Scoring notes

- Strongest emphasis on conventional law enforcement and deputy staffing [S1][S2].
- Most explicit skepticism of taxes and county spending in this field [S1].
