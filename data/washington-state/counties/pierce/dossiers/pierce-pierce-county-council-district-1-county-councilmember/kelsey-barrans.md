## Background

Barrans works in student support at Green River College and describes social-work training and nonprofit-board service [S2][S4]. Pierce County identifies her as the elected chair of the 2026 Charter Review Commission [S3].

## Positions

- She identifies the absence of public transit in communities such as Orting and Buckley as a barrier for older residents, people with mobility limitations, young people, and low-income residents [S4].
- She supports expanding bus service and reducing dependence on single-occupancy vehicles [S4].
- Her campaign emphasizes listening, cross-partisan common ground, and making county decisions responsive to community needs [S2].

## Record

- She chaired the 21-member Charter Review Commission during its 2026 review [S3].

## Endorsements

- The Alliance for Gun Responsibility lists Barrans among its 2026 endorsed candidates [S4].

## Scoring notes

- Most explicit transit-expansion position in this field [S4].
- Emphasizes deliberative, participatory governance [S2][S3].
