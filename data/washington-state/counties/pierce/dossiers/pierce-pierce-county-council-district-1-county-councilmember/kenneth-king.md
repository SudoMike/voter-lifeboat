## Background

King describes himself as a lifelong Pierce County resident and behavioral-health program manager with crisis-response and resource-coordination experience [S2]. He has served on the Pierce County Continuum of Care governing board, whose official minutes identify him as a member [S2][S3].

## Positions

- His campaign emphasizes infrastructure-led growth and protection of the district's rural and natural character [S2].
- He supports coordinating homelessness, housing, food-access, mental-health, and crisis services regionally to improve affordability and effectiveness [S2].
- He presents behavioral-health diversion and voluntary stabilization as ways to reduce pressure on emergency services [S2].

## Record

- He reports work on a 988 mobile rapid-response team and Pierce County's Recovery Navigator Program [S2].
- Official minutes document his participation in Continuum of Care governance and advocacy for communication with member organizations [S3].

## Scoring notes

- Strongest differentiator is direct behavioral-health crisis and homelessness-system experience [S2][S3].
- Connects growth decisions to infrastructure capacity and rural preservation [S2].
