## What it does

Authorizes up to $7.64 million in general-obligation bonds maturing within six years for station fleet-facility work, fire and EMS vehicles, and safety equipment, repaid through annual excess property taxes [S1].

Cost to households: The estimated rate is $0.18 per $1,000; the official estimate is $7.92 per month for the average North Kitsap home assessed at $528,283 [S1].

## Arguments for

- The pro committee says the fleet facility and an engine, two medic units, and a marine unit need renovation or replacement under the department's long-term plan, with all bonds repaid by 2032 [S1].

## Arguments against

- No against committee formed, so the pamphlet contains no organized opposition argument [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes versus capital replacement: YES authorizes short-term bond debt and excess property taxes for facilities, vehicles, and safety equipment [S1].
- The official record is one-sided because no con committee formed [S1].
