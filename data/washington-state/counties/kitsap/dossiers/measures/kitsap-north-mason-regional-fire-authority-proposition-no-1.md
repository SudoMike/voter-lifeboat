## What it does

Continues for six years the authority's dedicated EMS levy at an initial rate of up to $0.50 per $1,000 beginning in 2027; later annual revenue growth is generally limited to one percent and the rate remains capped at $0.50 [S1].

Cost to households: Up to $50 annually per $100,000 assessed value, about $4.17 per month per $100,000, or $250 annually on $500,000 [S1].

## Arguments for

- The pro committee says the levy has funded EMS for more than 40 years and supports paramedics, EMTs, ambulance transport, equipment, training, and round-the-clock readiness; funds are legally restricted to EMS [S1].

## Arguments against

- No against committee formed, so the pamphlet contains no organized opposition argument [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes versus EMS continuity: YES renews an existing six-year dedicated levy rather than creating a new tax [S1].
- Local accountability: voters must reconsider the levy every six years, and the funds are restricted to EMS [S1].
- The official record is one-sided because no con committee formed [S1].
