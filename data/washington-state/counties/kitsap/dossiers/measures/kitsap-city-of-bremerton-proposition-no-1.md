## What it does

Raises Bremerton's regular property-tax rate by $0.48 to a total authorized $1.95 per $1,000 for collection in 2027, with that levy amount becoming the base for later levies, to fund public safety and other city services [S1].

Cost to households: The gross increase is $48 annually per $100,000 assessed value, or $240 on $500,000. The pro committee says expiring public-safety levies reduce the net increase to $0.39 per $1,000 in 2027 and $0.26 thereafter [S1].

## Arguments for

- The pro committee says the levy would hire nine firefighters, replace two engines and a ladder truck, modernize Station 1, and establish a revolving equipment-replacement fund; it cites a 24% call-volume increase over a decade [S1].

## Arguments against

- The con committee calls the increase permanent and excessive, argues the plan overlooks drones, robots, and lighter medical-response vehicles, and says equipment replacement should be funded through budget reprioritization [S1].

## Axis notes

- Taxes versus staffing and capital investment: YES permanently resets the regular levy base upward to fund personnel, vehicles, facilities, and other services [S1].
- Service model and accountability: the pro case prioritizes added conventional capacity; the con case prioritizes technology, lower-cost response vehicles, and budget reallocation [S1].
