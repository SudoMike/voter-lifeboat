## What it does

Continues the fire district benefit charge for six years, allowing it to provide up to 60% of the operating budget. [S1]

## Fiscal mechanics

Cost line: The charge may fund up to 60% of the district operating budget; the official summary does not state a uniform parcel rate because benefit charges vary by property characteristics. Arithmetic examples are derived from the official rate and are not county model-home estimates [S1].

## Arguments for

The official material identifies the revenue for fire and emergency operations; no pro committee statement was available in the archived material [S1].

## Arguments against

No con committee statement was available; the direct trade-off is the named public service funding versus the property-tax levy or benefit charge [S1].
