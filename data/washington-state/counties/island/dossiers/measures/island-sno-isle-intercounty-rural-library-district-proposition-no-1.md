## What it does

Restores the library district's regular property-tax levy rate to $0.47 per $1,000 for collection in 2027 and uses that levy as the base for later statutory limits [S1][S2].

## Fiscal mechanics

- The official pamphlet estimates an increase of $124.25 annually, or $10.35 monthly, for a home assessed at $748,100 [S0].
- Revenue supports general library operations and maintenance [S1][S2].

## Arguments for

The official pro statement says restoration protects literacy programs, hours, digital access, bookmobile and student support, and library collections [S0].

## Arguments against

The official con statement characterizes the proposal as a 55% rate increase, questions budget growth since 2018, and argues the district should reassess services and priorities before increasing taxes [S0].

## Axis notes

- Property-tax increase versus continued library operations and services [S0][S1][S2].
